# ArtKech Design Studio — Project Pricing & Quotation Process

## 1. Purpose

Consistent, transparent pricing builds client trust and protects studio profitability. This SOP defines how we estimate project costs, structure quotes, handle negotiations, and manage scope changes that affect pricing. Every team member involved in client-facing work must understand these guidelines.

## 2. Pricing Philosophy

ArtKech prices on **value delivered**, not hours spent. Our rates reflect:
- The strategic thinking behind the design (not just execution time)
- The business impact of the deliverable (a logo used for 10 years vs. a one-time flyer)
- The complexity and constraints of the project
- The client's market position and budget reality

We never compete on price alone. If a client's primary selection criterion is "cheapest option," they are not our target client.

## 4. Quotation Process

### 4.1 Quote Request to Delivery Timeline

| Step | Duration | Responsible |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Receive inquiry | — | Studio Coordinator |
| Discovery call / brief intake | Within 48 hours | Account Manager |
| Internal estimation | Within 24 hours of brief | Account Manager + Creative Director |
| Quote document prepared | Within 24 hours of estimation | Account Manager |
| Quote sent to client | Within 48 hours of discovery call | Account Manager |
| Follow-up (if no response) | Day 3 after sending | Account Manager |
| Second follow-up | Day 7 after sending | Account Manager |
| Quote expiry | 30 days from issue date | Automatic |

### 4.2 Quote Document Structure

| Section | Content |
| :--- | :--- |
| Cover | ArtKech branding, client name, project title, date |
| Introduction | 2-3 sentences acknowledging the project and our excitement |
| Scope of work | Detailed list of deliverables with specifications |
| Process overview | 4-5 step timeline showing how we'll work together |
| Investment | Itemized pricing with subtotals and total |
| Timeline | Key milestones with estimated dates |
| Terms | Payment schedule, revision policy, ownership transfer, cancellation |
| Next steps | Clear CTA: "To proceed, please sign below and submit 50% deposit" |

### 4.3 Estimation Formula

```
Project Price = (Estimated Hours × Role Rate) × Complexity Multiplier × Value Multiplier

Complexity Multiplier:
- Simple (straightforward brief, single deliverable): 1.0
- Standard (clear brief, multiple deliverables): 1.2
- Complex (ambiguous brief, many stakeholders, technical constraints): 1.5
- Highly complex (naming + strategy + identity + applications): 1.8

Value Multiplier:
- Local small business: 1.0
- Regional brand: 1.2
- National brand: 1.5
- International brand: 2.0
- Funded startup / corporate: 1.5-2.0
```

## 5. Payment Terms

### 5.1 Standard Payment Schedule

| Project Value | Deposit | Mid-Project | Final |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| < 10,000 MAD | 100% upfront | — | — |
| 10,000 – 30,000 MAD | 50% upfront | — | 50% before final delivery |
| 30,000 – 80,000 MAD | 50% upfront | 25% at concept approval | 25% before final delivery |
| > 80,000 MAD | 40% upfront | 30% at concept approval | 30% before final delivery |

### 5.2 Payment Methods Accepted

| Method | Preferred | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Bank transfer (virement) | Yes | Primary method, receipt issued |
| Check (chèque) | Acceptable | Must clear before work begins |
| Cash | For amounts < 5,000 MAD only | Receipt issued, legal limit respected |
| International wire | For foreign clients | Client covers transfer fees |
| PayPal | For foreign clients | +5% processing fee passed to client |

### 5.3 Late Payment Policy

| Days Overdue | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| 1-7 days | Friendly reminder email |
| 8-14 days | Phone call from Account Manager, work paused |
| 15-30 days | Formal letter, all work suspended, files not delivered |
| 30+ days | Legal notice (mise en demeure), relationship terminated |

## 6. Negotiation Guidelines

### 6.1 Acceptable Negotiations

| Client Request | Our Response |
| :--- | :--- |
| "Can you do it for less?" | Reduce scope (fewer deliverables or revision rounds), not rate |
| "We have a smaller budget" | Offer a phased approach (Phase 1 now, Phase 2 later) |
| "Competitor quoted less" | Explain our value difference, offer to match scope (not price) |
| "Can we get a discount for multiple projects?" | 10-15% package discount for 3+ projects committed upfront |
| "We're a startup with limited funds" | Offer starter package, or equity/barter arrangement (rare, case-by-case) |

### 6.2 Non-Negotiable Policies

- We never reduce our rate below the internal hourly minimum
- We never start work without a signed contract and deposit
- We never deliver final files before full payment is received
- We never agree to unlimited revisions
- We never work on spec (free pitches) for new clients

## 7. Scope Change Management

### 7.1 Change Request Process

| Step | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Client requests additional work or change in direction |
| 2 | Account Manager assesses impact (time, cost, timeline) |
| 3 | If minor (< 2 hours additional work): absorb and note for future reference |
| 4 | If significant: prepare change order with revised cost and timeline |
| 5 | Client approves change order in writing |
| 6 | Work proceeds on revised scope |

### 7.2 Change Order Template

> **Change Order #[X]** — [Project Name]
>
> **Requested change:** [Description]
>
> **Impact on timeline:** +[X] business days
>
> **Additional cost:** [Amount] MAD
>
> **New project total:** [Amount] MAD
>
> **Approval:** Client signature + date
