### 3.3 Champion Development

**Identifying Potential Champions:**
- They proactively share information about internal dynamics
- They introduce you to other stakeholders
- They advocate for your solution in internal meetings
- They share competitive intelligence
- They tell you about budget cycles and decision timelines

**Champion Enablement Package:**
1. Internal presentation deck (their branding, their data)
2. ROI calculator pre-filled with their numbers
3. Competitive comparison (for internal use)
4. Implementation timeline specific to their org
5. Reference customers in their industry
6. Executive summary for their boss's boss

**Champion Coaching Questions:**
- "What would need to be true for this to get approved?"
- "Who else needs to be convinced, and what do they care about?"
- "What's happened with similar initiatives in the past?"
- "What's the biggest risk to this deal from your perspective?"
- "How can I make you look good in this process?"

## 4. Discovery Framework for Enterprise

### 4.1 MEDDPICC Qualification

| Element | Question | Must-Have Answer |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| **M**etrics | What quantifiable outcomes would justify this investment? | Specific KPIs with targets |
| **E**conomic Buyer | Who signs the check and what do they care about? | Named individual + priorities |
| **D**ecision Criteria | What factors will determine the winner? | Ranked criteria list |
| **D**ecision Process | What steps happen between now and signature? | Timeline with milestones |
| **P**aper Process | What does procurement/legal require? | Specific requirements + timeline |
| **I**dentify Pain | What's the cost of the current situation? | Quantified pain ($, time, risk) |
| **C**hampion | Who is actively selling internally for us? | Named champion + their motivation |
| **C**ompetition | Who else are they evaluating? | Named competitors + their positioning |

### 4.2 Enterprise Discovery Questions

**Business Context:**
- "Walk me through how operational knowledge flows in your organization today."
- "What happens when a key person leaves — how much institutional knowledge walks out?"
- "How does your current approach scale as you grow? What breaks first?"
- "What's the cost of a new hire taking 6 months to become fully productive?"

**Pain Quantification:**
- "How many hours per week do your teams spend searching for information?"
- "What's the revenue impact of inconsistent process execution?"
- "How much did your last consulting engagement cost to document operations?"
- "What's your estimated cost of knowledge loss from turnover?"

**Decision Process:**
- "If we could demonstrate clear value, what would the path to a decision look like?"
- "Who else would need to be involved in evaluating a solution like this?"
- "Is there a budget allocated for this, or would we need to create a business case?"
- "What's your timeline — is there an event or deadline driving urgency?"

## 7. Procurement & Legal Navigation

### 7.1 Procurement Process Stages

| Stage | Duration | Key Activities | Your Actions |
|-------|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Vendor registration | 1–2 weeks | Complete vendor forms | Submit immediately, follow up daily |
| RFP/RFI (if required) | 2–4 weeks | Respond to formal requirements | Tailor response, highlight differentiators |
| Pricing negotiation | 1–3 weeks | Negotiate terms and pricing | Know your floor, trade value for concessions |
| Legal review | 2–4 weeks | MSA/DPA/SLA negotiation | Have redlines ready, be flexible on non-critical |
| Approval routing | 1–2 weeks | Internal signatures | Keep champion informed, offer to help |
| PO issuance | 1 week | Purchase order creation | Confirm details, celebrate |

### 7.2 Legal Negotiation Strategy

**Terms to Protect (non-negotiable):**
- Limitation of liability (cap at 12 months fees)
- IP ownership (we own the platform and AI)
- Indemnification scope (reasonable, mutual)
- Termination for convenience (with notice period)

**Terms to Flex (negotiable):**
- Payment terms (Net 30 → Net 45 or Net 60)
- SLA credits (increase if needed)
- Data handling specifics (accommodate reasonable requests)
- Insurance limits (increase if required)
- Audit rights (annual, with notice)
- Custom terms (if reasonable and precedented)

**Common Legal Objections & Responses:**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We need unlimited liability" | "We cap at 12 months of fees, which is industry standard. We can discuss increasing the cap for an additional fee." |
| "We need to own all outputs" | "Customers own their content and data. The platform, AI models, and underlying technology remain ours." |
| "Your DPA doesn't meet our requirements" | "Let's review specific gaps. We're happy to accommodate reasonable data protection requirements." |
| "We need a custom SLA" | "Our standard 99.9% SLA includes credits. For custom SLAs, we can discuss enterprise-tier options." |

## 9. Post-Close: Setting Up for Expansion

### 9.1 Land-and-Expand Strategy

**Initial Land (Year 1):**
- 1 department, 50–100 users
- Starter or Professional tier
- Prove value, build champions
- Document ROI meticulously

**Expand Phase 1 (Month 6–12):**
- Adjacent departments (2–3 more)
- Upgrade to Enterprise tier
- Add integrations and modules
- Grow to 200–300 users

**Expand Phase 2 (Year 2):**
- Company-wide rollout
- Executive sponsorship
- Custom development
- 500+ users, $200K+ ACV

### 9.2 Expansion Triggers

| Trigger | Signal | Action |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Department success | NPS > 50, usage > 80% | Propose adjacent department pilot |
| New leader hired | LinkedIn alert, champion intel | Intro meeting with new stakeholder |
| Company growth | Funding round, acquisition | Capacity planning conversation |
| Renewal approaching | 90 days before renewal | Expansion proposal with renewal |
