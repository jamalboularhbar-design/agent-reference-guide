# Riad & Routes — Online Reputation & Review Management

## 1. Purpose

Online reviews are the single most influential factor in a traveler's booking decision for boutique properties. This SOP defines how Riad & Routes proactively generates, monitors, responds to, and leverages guest reviews across all platforms to maintain our position as a top-rated property.

## 3. Review Generation Strategy

### 3.1 The "Perfect Moment" Ask

Reviews are requested at the moment of peak satisfaction — not at checkout when guests are rushed. Optimal moments:

| Moment | Method | Script |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| After an exceptional experience (tour, dinner, spa) | In-person, casual | "I'm so glad you enjoyed it! If you have a moment during your stay, we'd love a review on TripAdvisor — it really helps other travelers find us." |
| Morning after a great night's sleep | At breakfast, by server | "How did you sleep? Wonderful! If you'd like to share your experience, here's our TripAdvisor page." (hand card with QR code) |
| Post-departure (T+1 day) | WhatsApp message | [See template in WhatsApp SOP — includes direct review link] |
| Post-departure (T+3 days) | Email (automated) | Personalized email with review links for TripAdvisor + Google |

### 3.2 Review Request Rules

- Never ask more than twice per stay (once in-person, once post-departure)
- Never offer incentives for reviews (violates platform policies)
- Never ask for a "5-star review" — ask them to "share their experience"
- If a guest had any complaint during their stay, do NOT request a review (focus on recovery first)
- Staff who generate the most organic review mentions receive recognition (see Staff Training SOP)

### 3.3 QR Code Placement

Discreet QR code cards (linking to TripAdvisor) are placed in:
- Welcome folder in room (subtle, not pushy)
- Restaurant check presenter
- Spa relaxation lounge (after treatment)
- Departure gift bag

## 4. Review Monitoring

### 4.1 Daily Monitoring Routine (09:00)

The Guest Relations Manager checks all platforms daily:

1. TripAdvisor: new reviews, ranking changes, competitor reviews
2. Google Business: new reviews, questions, photo uploads
3. Booking.com: new reviews, guest scores, internal messages
4. Airbnb: new reviews, response reminders
5. Instagram: tagged posts, mentions, DMs

### 4.2 Alert System

- New review notification: instant push notification to Guest Relations Manager
- Negative review (below 4 stars): immediate alert to GM
- Review mentioning specific staff: forwarded to that staff member (positive) or their manager (negative)
- Competitor review milestone: flagged in weekly report

## 5. Response Protocol

### 5.1 Response Timeline

| Review Type | Response Deadline | Responder |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 5-star positive | Within 48 hours | Guest Relations Manager |
| 4-star positive with feedback | Within 24 hours | Guest Relations Manager |
| 3-star mixed | Within 12 hours | GM reviews draft, Guest Relations posts |
| 1-2 star negative | Within 6 hours | GM writes response personally |

### 5.2 Response Templates

**5-star response (personalized):**
> Dear [Name], thank you so much for your beautiful review! We are delighted that you enjoyed [specific thing they mentioned]. It was a pleasure having you with us, and [staff name] was thrilled to read your kind words. We hope to welcome you back to Riad [Property] soon. Warm regards, [GM Name]

**Negative review response (empathetic, solution-focused):**
> Dear [Name], thank you for taking the time to share your feedback. We sincerely apologize that [specific issue] did not meet your expectations — this is not the experience we strive to deliver. Since your stay, we have [specific action taken to address the issue]. We would welcome the opportunity to make this right and invite you to contact us directly at [email] so we can discuss how to ensure your next visit exceeds expectations. With sincere apologies, [GM Name], General Manager

### 5.3 Response Rules

- Always personalize (reference specific details from their review)
- Never be defensive or argue with the reviewer
- Never reveal private guest information in a public response
- Never blame staff by name in a response
- Always end with an invitation to return or continue the conversation privately
- For factually incorrect reviews: politely correct the record without being confrontational
- For suspected fake reviews: flag to platform for investigation, respond neutrally in the meantime

## 6. Negative Review Recovery

When a negative review appears:

1. **Identify the guest** in PMS (match name, dates, room)
2. **Review internal records** — was this complaint raised during the stay? What was done?
3. **Contact the guest directly** (email or phone, not public reply) within 24 hours
4. **Offer resolution** — complimentary future stay, refund of specific service, or genuine apology
5. **If guest updates review** — thank them publicly
6. **If guest doesn't respond** — post the public response and move on
7. **Internal debrief** — what went wrong, how do we prevent it?
