# ARG-Builder: Intellectual Property Strategy

## Executive Summary

This document defines ARG-Builder's intellectual property strategy — covering patent protection, trade secret management, open-source policy, trademark strategy, and IP assignment agreements. For an AI-powered SaaS company, IP is the primary source of competitive moat and enterprise value. This strategy ensures that all innovations are properly protected, employee contributions are clearly assigned, and open-source usage is managed responsibly.

## Patent Strategy

### Patent Decision Framework

| Criteria | Patent | Trade Secret | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Visible in product | Yes | No | Competitors can reverse-engineer |
| Independently discoverable | Yes | No | Others will likely develop independently |
| Core to competitive advantage | Consider both | Yes (if not visible) | Maximum protection |
| Expensive to develop | Yes | Yes | Protect investment |
| Short-lived innovation | No | No | Patent takes 2–3 years |
| Process/methodology | Selective | Yes | Hard to enforce patents on methods |

| Step | Action | Timeline | Cost |

## Open-Source Policy

### Usage Policy

| Category | Policy | Approval Required |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| Permissive licenses (MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD) | Approved for use | No (auto-approved) |
| Weak copyleft (LGPL, MPL) | Approved with conditions | Engineering lead review |
| Strong copyleft (GPL, AGPL) | Restricted — case-by-case | CTO + Legal approval |

## IP Assignment & Employment

### IP Assignment Agreement (Key Terms)

| Clause | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Assignment scope | All work product created during employment belongs to company |
| Prior inventions | Employee discloses pre-existing IP (excluded from assignment) |
| Moral rights waiver | Employee waives moral rights to work product |
| Cooperation | Employee assists with patent/trademark filings |
| Survival | Assignment survives termination of employment |
| Consideration | Employment itself is consideration (plus equity) |

### Contractor IP Terms

| Scenario | IP Ownership | Agreement Required |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Full-time contractor (dedicated) | Company owns all work product | Work-for-hire + assignment |
| Part-time contractor (project) | Company owns project deliverables | Work-for-hire + assignment |
| Consultant (advisory) | Company owns recommendations; consultant retains methodology | Consulting agreement + IP clause |
| Agency (design, marketing) | Company owns deliverables; agency retains tools/templates | Agency agreement + IP schedule |
