# ARG Builder — Competitive Battle Card

**Purpose:** Quick-reference guide for live sales conversations when a prospect mentions a competitor. Keep this open during demos and discovery calls.

---

## Positioning Statement

ARG Builder is the **AI-powered operational intelligence platform** for mid-market companies that have outgrown wikis but can't justify enterprise-class tools. We combine structured knowledge management with AI governance, reading analytics, and interactive knowledge graphs — capabilities no single competitor offers together.

---

## vs. Notion ($10/seat)

**When they say:** "We already use Notion for everything."

**Your response:** "Notion is excellent for writing and collaboration — it's a horizontal tool designed for flexibility. The challenge is that flexibility becomes chaos at scale. When you have 200+ people, how do you know which SOPs are current? Who's actually reading the onboarding docs? Where are the gaps? Notion doesn't answer those questions because it wasn't built for operational governance. ARG Builder was built specifically for that problem — we add AI-powered freshness scoring, duplicate detection, and reading depth analytics on top of structured operational content."

**Key differentiators:** No content governance, no reading analytics beyond page views, no knowledge graph, no approval workflows, no staleness detection. Notion is a blank canvas; ARG Builder is a governed operational system.

**Trap question to ask:** "How do you currently know when an SOP is outdated? Who's responsible for auditing content freshness across 500+ documents?"

---

## vs. Confluence ($6/seat)

**When they say:** "We're on Confluence — it's part of our Atlassian stack."

**Your response:** "Confluence is deeply integrated with Jira, which makes it great for engineering teams. But for operational knowledge — SOPs, playbooks, onboarding guides — it's notoriously difficult to navigate. The search is poor, content gets buried in space hierarchies, and there's no way to measure whether people are actually reading and understanding the docs. We see a lot of companies where Confluence becomes a graveyard of outdated pages. ARG Builder gives you AI that actively monitors content health and tells you exactly what's stale, duplicated, or missing."

**Key differentiators:** Poor search UX, no AI governance, no reading depth analytics, complex permission model, no knowledge graph visualization. Confluence is built for technical documentation, not operational governance.

**Trap question to ask:** "When was the last time someone audited your Confluence spaces for outdated content? How many pages haven't been updated in 12+ months?"

## vs. Trainual ($249/month flat)

**When they say:** "We use Trainual for onboarding and training."

**Your response:** "Trainual is solid for onboarding workflows and training completion tracking. Where it falls short is beyond onboarding — ongoing operational knowledge that your team references daily. Trainual doesn't have a knowledge graph, doesn't detect duplicate content across your library, and doesn't give you reading depth analytics. At $249/month for 10 seats, you're paying more per seat than our Professional tier, and you're limited to training use cases. ARG Builder covers the full operational knowledge lifecycle — from creation through governance to retirement."

**Key differentiators:** Narrow use case (onboarding only), no knowledge graph, no AI governance, limited to training completion metrics, expensive per-seat at scale. Trainual is a training tool; ARG Builder is an operational intelligence platform.

**Trap question to ask:** "What happens to knowledge after onboarding? Where do your teams go for day-to-day operational reference — is that also in Trainual, or scattered across other tools?"

## vs. Guru ($25/seat)

**When they say:** "We looked at Guru — they have AI features too."

**Your response:** "Guru is card-based, which works for quick-reference snippets but breaks down for structured operational content like multi-page SOPs, complex playbooks, or process frameworks. At $25/seat, it's also 67% more expensive than ARG Builder per user. Our AI goes deeper — we don't just surface answers, we actively govern your entire knowledge base: detecting duplicates across hundreds of documents, scoring content freshness, identifying knowledge gaps, and mapping how everything connects through an interactive knowledge graph. Guru gives you verified answers; ARG Builder gives you a healthy, governed knowledge system."

**Key differentiators:** Card-based (not structured documents), 67% more expensive per seat, no knowledge graph, no reading depth analytics, limited governance features. Guru is a knowledge verification tool; ARG Builder is a full operational intelligence platform.

**Trap question to ask:** "How do you handle complex, multi-step operational procedures in Guru's card format? Do your teams find themselves needing to chain 10+ cards together for a single process?"

## vs. "We'll just use Google Docs / SharePoint"

**When they say:** "We don't need a dedicated tool — Google Docs works fine."

**Your response:** "Google Docs works until it doesn't. At 50 people, you can find things. At 200+, you have thousands of documents across dozens of folders with no governance, no analytics, and no way to know what's current. The average mid-market company loses $4.3 million per year in productivity to this exact problem. ARG Builder isn't replacing Google Docs for collaboration — it's adding the governance, analytics, and AI layer that turns scattered documents into a reliable operational system your team can trust."

**Trap question to ask:** "If a new hire starts Monday, how long does it take them to find every SOP relevant to their role? Can you guarantee everything they find is current?"

---

## vs. "We'll build it internally"

**When they say:** "Our engineering team could build something custom."

**Your response:** "You absolutely could — and the first version would take 6–12 months of engineering time. We've shipped 288 features across 25 development batches, with 92 database tables and 307 tests. That's roughly $500K–$1M in engineering cost at market rates. Then you need to maintain it, add AI capabilities, build analytics, and keep it current. At $15/user/month, ARG Builder costs less than one engineer's monthly coffee budget and gives you a production-grade platform today. Your engineering team should be building your core product, not internal tooling."

**Trap question to ask:** "What's the opportunity cost of pulling 2–3 engineers off your product roadmap for 6 months to build internal documentation tooling?"
