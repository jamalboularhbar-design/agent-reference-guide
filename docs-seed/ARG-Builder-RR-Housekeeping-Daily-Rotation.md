# Riad & Routes — Housekeeping Daily Rotation & Room Standards

## 1. Purpose

This SOP governs the daily housekeeping rotation across all Riad & Routes properties. Housekeeping is the invisible backbone of guest satisfaction — a perfectly maintained room communicates care, attention, and luxury without a single word. Every housekeeper must internalize these standards until they become instinct.

## 2. Shift Structure

| Shift | Hours | Primary Responsibilities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Morning (A) | 06:00 – 14:00 | Departure rooms, stayover service, public area deep clean |
| Afternoon (B) | 14:00 – 22:00 | Turndown service, late check-out rooms, evening public areas |
| Night (C) | 22:00 – 06:00 | Emergency cleaning only, laundry processing, supply restocking |

Staffing ratio: 1 housekeeper per 4 rooms (luxury standard). For properties with 12+ rooms, a Housekeeping Supervisor is on-shift at all times.

## 3. Morning Routine (06:00 – 14:00)

### 3.1 Shift Briefing (06:00 – 06:15)

The Housekeeping Supervisor conducts a 15-minute briefing covering:

- Occupancy status: which rooms are departures, stayovers, arrivals expected today
- VIP alerts: any special requirements (extra pillows, hypoallergenic bedding, specific minibar items)
- Maintenance issues: rooms with reported problems (plumbing, AC, electrical)
- Supply check: confirm trolley stock levels before starting rounds

### 3.2 Room Priority Order

Rooms are serviced in the following priority:

1. **Departure rooms** (guest has checked out) — must be fully reset for next arrival
2. **VIP stayover rooms** — serviced first among occupied rooms
3. **Standard stayover rooms** — serviced by floor, top to bottom
4. **Vacant rooms** — light dust and air circulation check

### 3.3 Stayover Room Cleaning Standard (Target: 25 minutes per room)

| Step | Time | Standard |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Knock and announce ("Housekeeping, may I come in?") | 0:00 | Knock 3 times, wait 10 seconds, knock again, wait 10 seconds before entering |
| Open windows for ventilation | 0:30 | All windows open for minimum 5 minutes |
| Strip and remake bed with hospital corners | 1:00 | Fresh top sheet daily, duvet cover every 3 days unless requested |
| Collect used towels, replace with fresh set | 5:00 | Bath towel, hand towel, face cloth, bath mat — all folded in thirds |
| Clean bathroom: toilet, sink, shower, mirror | 7:00 | No water spots on mirror, no hair in drain, toilet rim sanitized |
| Replenish amenities (soap, shampoo, conditioner, lotion) | 12:00 | All bottles minimum 50% full; replace if below |
| Dust all surfaces (nightstands, desk, windowsills, headboard) | 14:00 | White glove test standard — zero visible dust |
| Vacuum/mop floors (tiled floors mopped, carpeted areas vacuumed) | 18:00 | Under bed checked, corners reached |
| Restock minibar (check consumption log) | 21:00 | All items at par level, expiry dates checked |
| Final inspection: lights, AC setting, curtain alignment, scent diffuser | 23:00 | Room temperature 22°C, curtains symmetrical, diffuser at level 2 |
| Close windows, exit, mark room as "clean" in system | 25:00 | Door locked, DND sign repositioned if present |

### 3.4 Departure Room Reset (Target: 45 minutes per room)

In addition to the stayover standard, departure rooms require:

- Full linen change (all sheets, pillowcases, duvet cover, mattress protector inspection)
- Deep bathroom clean (grout check, drain flush, amenity tray fully replaced)
- Wardrobe and safe inspection (check for forgotten items — log and store in Lost & Found)
- Minibar full reset with fresh inventory count
- Welcome amenity placement for next guest (per arrival checklist)

## 4. Turndown Service (18:00 – 21:00)

Turndown service is performed for all occupied rooms between 18:00 and 21:00:

1. Knock and announce. If guest is present, ask permission. If DND sign is displayed, skip and log.
2. Close curtains/blinds fully.
3. Turn down the bed: fold duvet at 45° angle on the guest's preferred side (check CRM notes for side preference).
4. Place slippers beside the bed and robe on the bed if not already out.
5. Place a small sweet (date or chocolate) on the pillow with a "Good Night" card.
6. Refresh water carafe on nightstand with fresh filtered water and clean glass.
7. Dim lights to "evening mode" (bedside lamps only, overhead off).
8. Adjust AC to "night mode" (20°C).
9. Empty waste bins if more than 25% full.
10. Replace used towels only (do not disturb hung towels — eco policy).

## 5. Linen & Laundry Standards

| Item | Change Frequency | Par Stock (per room) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Bed sheets | Every departure + every 3 days for stayovers | 3 sets |
| Pillowcases | Daily | 4 sets |
| Duvet cover | Every departure + weekly for stayovers | 2 sets |
| Bath towels | Daily (unless guest opts for eco reuse) | 4 per room |
| Hand towels | Daily | 3 per room |
| Bath mat | Daily | 2 per room |
| Bathrobes | Every departure + upon request | 2 per room |

All linen is processed in-house using commercial-grade machines. Whites are washed at 60°C minimum. Stain-treated items that don't recover after 2 wash cycles are retired to staff use.

## 6. Quality Inspection Protocol

The Housekeeping Supervisor inspects a minimum of 30% of cleaned rooms daily (random selection). The inspection uses a 50-point checklist:

- Bed making (10 points): hospital corners, symmetry, no wrinkles, pillow fluffing
- Bathroom (15 points): mirror clarity, grout cleanliness, amenity placement, drain check, towel folding
- Surfaces (10 points): dust-free, polish on wood, no fingerprints on glass
- Floor (5 points): no debris, mop streaks, carpet vacuum lines visible
- Ambiance (5 points): scent level, temperature, lighting preset, curtain alignment
- Minibar (5 points): stock level, label facing forward, temperature correct

**Pass threshold: 45/50.** Rooms scoring below 45 are re-cleaned immediately. Three consecutive failures by the same housekeeper trigger retraining.

## 7. Lost & Found Procedure

1. Any item found in a departure room is photographed, bagged, labeled (room number, date, description), and stored in the locked Lost & Found cabinet.
2. Front Desk is notified immediately for high-value items (electronics, jewelry, documents).
3. Guest is contacted within 2 hours of discovery via email/WhatsApp.
4. Items are held for 90 days. After 90 days, non-valuable items are donated; valuable items are held for 12 months.

## 8. Chemical Safety & Green Practices

- All cleaning products are eco-certified (EU Ecolabel or equivalent).
- Housekeepers must wear gloves when using bathroom chemicals.
- Microfiber cloths are color-coded: blue (glass/mirrors), green (surfaces), red (bathroom), yellow (kitchen).
- Towel reuse program: guests who hang towels on the rack indicate reuse; towels on the floor are replaced.
