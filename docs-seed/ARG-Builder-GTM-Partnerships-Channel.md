# ARG-Builder: Go-to-Market Partnerships & Channel Strategy

## Framework for Building Revenue-Generating Partner Ecosystems

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Partnerships can accelerate growth by 2–5x when executed well — and waste years of effort when executed poorly. The difference is strategic clarity: knowing which partners to pursue, what value exchange to offer, and how to operationalize the relationship for mutual benefit. This document defines ARG-Builder's complete partnership and channel strategy, from partner identification through program design to revenue attribution and optimization.

## 5. Reseller / Channel Program

### 5.1 Channel Program Tiers

| Tier | Requirements | Discount | Benefits |
|------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Registered | Sign agreement, complete training | 15% off list | Deal registration, basic support |
| Silver | $100K annual revenue, 2 certified reps | 20% off list | Co-marketing funds, priority support |
| Gold | $300K annual revenue, 4 certified reps | 25% off list | Dedicated partner manager, MDF, leads |
| Platinum | $500K+ annual revenue, 6+ certified reps | 30% off list | Executive access, joint planning, custom |

### 5.2 Channel Conflict Resolution

| Scenario | Resolution Rule |
|----------|----------------|
| Partner and direct sales on same deal | First to register wins (90-day protection) |
| Two partners on same deal | First to register wins |
| Customer comes inbound (no partner) | Direct sale (partner can be added if they add value) |
| Partner-sourced, direct closes | Partner gets commission (sourced credit) |
| Direct-sourced, partner influences | Reduced commission (influenced credit) |
| Existing customer expansion | Original seller (direct or partner) owns |

### 5.3 Channel Economics

| Metric | Direct Sale | Channel Sale | Comparison |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Average discount | 10% | 25% (to partner) | Channel costs more per deal |
| Sales cost (internal) | $15K CAC | $3K CAC (partner does selling) | Channel much lower internal cost |
| Net margin | 80% | 65% | Direct is higher margin |
| Scale potential | Limited by headcount | Unlimited (partner leverage) | Channel scales better |
| Customer relationship | Direct ownership | Shared or partner-owned | Direct gives more control |
| Time to revenue | Faster (control) | Slower (partner ramp) | Direct is faster initially |

## 6. Co-Sell Partnerships

### 6.1 Co-Sell Motion Design

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Ideal co-sell partner | Complementary product, same buyer, non-competitive |
| Joint value proposition | "Together we solve X that neither solves alone" |
| Account mapping | Identify shared accounts and prospects |
| Joint pipeline | Shared opportunities, coordinated pursuit |
| Revenue model | Each sells their own product (no commission) |
| Success metric | Influenced pipeline and revenue |

### 6.2 Account Mapping Process

| Step | Activity | Tool | Output |
|------|----------|------|--------|
| 1 | Export target account lists (both sides) | CRM | Account lists |
| 2 | Match accounts (automated) | Crossbeam/Reveal | Overlap report |
| 3 | Identify high-value overlaps | Joint review | Priority account list |
| 4 | Assign joint account owners | Both teams | Ownership matrix |
| 5 | Create joint pursuit plans | Collaborative | Account plans |
| 6 | Execute coordinated outreach | Both teams | Pipeline |

### 6.3 Co-Sell Playbook

| Stage | Our Action | Partner Action | Joint Action |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Prospecting | Identify accounts using partner | Identify accounts needing us | Warm introductions |
| Discovery | Understand full tech stack | Understand operational needs | Joint discovery call |
| Demo | Demo our product | Demo their product | Joint demo (if appropriate) |
| Proposal | Our proposal | Their proposal | Coordinated pricing/timing |
| Close | Our contract | Their contract | Aligned close dates |
| Success | Our onboarding | Their onboarding | Joint success plan |

## 9. Revenue Attribution & Measurement

### 9.1 Partner Revenue Categories

| Category | Definition | Attribution |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Partner-sourced | Partner identified and referred the opportunity | 100% partner credit |
| Partner-influenced | Partner involved in deal but didn't source | 50% partner credit |
| Partner-closed | Partner sold and closed the deal (reseller) | 100% partner credit |
| Marketplace-sourced | Customer found us via marketplace | 100% marketplace credit |
| Integration-influenced | Customer chose us because of integration | Tracked but not commissioned |

### 9.2 Partner Program Metrics

| Metric | Target (Year 1) | Target (Year 2) | Target (Year 3) |
|--------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| Partner-sourced revenue (% of new ARR) | 10% | 20% | 30% |
| Partner-influenced revenue | 15% | 30% | 40% |
| Active partners (submitting referrals) | 20 | 50 | 100 |
| Partner-sourced pipeline | $500K | $2M | $5M |
| Partner satisfaction (NPS) | > 40 | > 50 | > 60 |
| Average deal size (partner-sourced) | 1.2x direct | 1.3x direct | 1.5x direct |
| Partner-sourced win rate | > 30% | > 35% | > 40% |

### 9.3 Partner ROI Calculation

| Component | Calculation |
|-----------|------------|
| Partner revenue | Total ARR from partner-sourced + influenced deals |
| Partner cost | Commissions + partner team salaries + program costs |
| Partner ROI | (Partner revenue - Partner cost) / Partner cost |
| Target ROI | > 3x in Year 1, > 5x in Year 2+ |
| Break-even timeline | < 12 months per partner |
