# ARG-Builder: Performance Review & Talent Development Framework

## Building a High-Performance Culture Through Structured Growth

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This document establishes ARG-Builder's performance management system — a continuous development framework that replaces traditional annual reviews with ongoing feedback, clear expectations, and structured growth paths. The system drives accountability while fostering psychological safety and professional development.

---

## 2. Performance Philosophy

### Core Beliefs

1. **Growth over judgment** — Performance management exists to develop people, not rank them
2. **Continuous over periodic** — Feedback happens in real-time, not once a year
3. **Outcomes over activity** — Measure impact and results, not hours or busyness
4. **Context matters** — Performance is evaluated relative to role level, tenure, and circumstances
5. **Two-way dialogue** — Managers and reports share responsibility for development

### Performance Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Results | 40% | Achievement of OKRs and key deliverables |
| Competencies | 30% | Demonstration of role-specific skills |
| Values | 20% | Embodiment of company values and culture |
| Growth | 10% | Learning, development, and stretch |

## 3. Performance Cycle

### Annual Cadence

| Month | Activity | Duration | Participants |
|-------|----------|----------|-------------|

| June | Mid-year review | 60 min | Manager + Report |
| September | Q3 check-in | 45 min | Manager + Report |
| November | Year-end review | 90 min | Manager + Report |

### Continuous Elements

**Weekly:**
- 1:1 meetings (30 min minimum)
- Real-time feedback (praise and constructive)
- Progress updates on goals

**Monthly:**
- Goal progress review
- Development conversation (15 min within 1:1)
- Peer feedback collection

**Quarterly:**
- Formal check-in with written summary
- Goal adjustment if needed
- Career development discussion

## 4. Goal Setting Framework

### 4.1 Goal Structure (per quarter)

Each team member sets:
- **3–5 Outcome Goals** — Measurable results tied to team/company OKRs
- **1–2 Development Goals** — Skills, knowledge, or capabilities to build
- **1 Values Goal** — Specific way to embody company values

### 4.2 Goal Quality Criteria (SMART+)

| Criterion | Definition | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Specific | Clear, unambiguous outcome | "Reduce churn from 5% to 3.5%" |
| Measurable | Quantifiable success criteria | "Measured by monthly cohort analysis" |
| Achievable | Challenging but realistic | "Based on current trajectory + intervention" |
| Relevant | Aligned to team/company goals | "Supports NRR target of 120%" |
| Time-bound | Clear deadline | "By end of Q2 2026" |
| + Impactful | Meaningful business impact | "Worth $180K ARR saved annually" |

### 4.3 Goal Alignment Cascade

```
Company Annual Goals
    → Department Quarterly OKRs
        → Team Sprint Goals
            → Individual Outcome Goals
```

**Alignment Check Questions:**
- Does this goal contribute to a team OKR?
- Can I explain how this connects to company strategy?
- If I achieve this, will it matter to the business?
- Is this the highest-impact thing I could work on?

## 5. Performance Rating Scale

### 5-Point Scale

| Rating | Label | Definition | Distribution Target |
|--------|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds expectations; top 5% performer | 5–10% |
| 4 | Exceeds | Regularly exceeds expectations; strong contributor | 20–25% |
| 3 | Meets | Consistently meets expectations; solid performer | 50–60% |
| 2 | Developing | Partially meets expectations; improvement needed | 10–15% |
| 1 | Below | Does not meet expectations; urgent intervention | 0–5% |

### Rating Calibration Guidelines

**Rating 5 (Exceptional):**
- Delivered results significantly beyond goals
- Elevated the entire team's performance
- Demonstrated leadership beyond their role
- Created lasting positive impact on the organization
- Would be devastating to lose

**Rating 4 (Exceeds):**
- Achieved stretch goals consistently
- Proactively solved problems beyond scope
- Mentored others effectively
- Demonstrated readiness for next level
- High-impact contributor

**Rating 3 (Meets):**
- Achieved all core goals
- Reliable, consistent performer
- Meets role expectations fully
- Growing appropriately for tenure
- Valued team member

**Rating 2 (Developing):**
- Missed some key goals
- Inconsistent performance
- Needs additional support or coaching
- May be in wrong role or level
- Improvement plan recommended

**Rating 1 (Below):**
- Failed to meet minimum expectations
- Significant gaps in core competencies
- Negative impact on team
- PIP required immediately
- Separation may be appropriate

## 7. Feedback Framework

### 7.1 Feedback Types

| Type | Frequency | Format | Purpose |
|------|-----------|--------|---------|
| Praise | Daily | Verbal/Slack/Written | Reinforce positive behaviors |
| Coaching | Weekly | 1:1 conversation | Develop skills and capabilities |
| Constructive | As needed | Private conversation | Address gaps or concerns |
| Peer | Monthly | Written (anonymous option) | 360-degree perspective |
| Formal | Quarterly | Written + discussion | Official performance record |

### 7.2 SBI Feedback Model

**Situation → Behavior → Impact**

```
"During yesterday's customer call [Situation],
you asked three clarifying questions before proposing a solution [Behavior],
which helped the customer feel heard and led to them agreeing to expand [Impact]."
```

### 7.3 Peer Feedback Questions

**Quarterly Peer Feedback (3–5 peers selected):**

1. What is this person's greatest strength that the team benefits from?
2. What is one area where they could improve to be even more effective?
3. How well do they embody our company values? (1–5 scale + example)
4. Would you want to work with this person again on a critical project? Why?
5. What advice would you give them for their career development?

### 7.4 Upward Feedback (Report → Manager)

1. How well does your manager set clear expectations?
2. How effectively do they remove blockers for you?
3. How well do they provide feedback and coaching?
4. How safe do you feel raising concerns or disagreeing?
5. What could they do differently to better support your growth?

## 9. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

### 9.1 PIP Triggers

A PIP is initiated when:
- Rating of 2 for two consecutive quarters
- Rating of 1 at any review point
- Significant behavioral issue after verbal warning
- Manager + HR agreement that formal intervention is needed

### 9.2 PIP Structure

**Duration:** 30–60 days (depending on severity)

**Components:**
1. Clear statement of performance gap
2. Specific, measurable improvement targets
3. Resources and support provided
4. Weekly check-in schedule
5. Success criteria and timeline
6. Consequences of not meeting targets

### 9.3 PIP Template

```markdown
# Performance Improvement Plan

## Employee: [Name]
## Manager: [Name]
## HR Partner: [Name]
## Start Date: [Date]
## End Date: [Date]

### Performance Gap
[Specific description of what's not meeting expectations]

### Expected Performance Level
[Clear description of what "meeting expectations" looks like]

### Improvement Goals (SMART)
1. [Goal 1] — Measured by: [Metric] — Target: [Number]
2. [Goal 2] — Measured by: [Metric] — Target: [Number]
3. [Goal 3] — Measured by: [Metric] — Target: [Number]

### Support Provided
- [Training, coaching, mentoring, reduced workload, etc.]

### Check-in Schedule

### Outcomes
- ✅ Meets all goals → PIP closed, return to normal performance cycle
```

5. **CEO signs off** on exceptions and top performers
6. **Communication** to employee with context and rationale

## 11. Manager Toolkit

### 11.1 1:1 Meeting Framework

**Weekly 1:1 Agenda (30 min):**
- Check-in (5 min): How are you doing? Energy level?
- Updates (10 min): Progress, blockers, priorities
- Coaching (10 min): Development, feedback, problem-solving
- Action items (5 min): Commitments for next week

**Monthly Development 1:1 (additional 15 min):**
- IDP progress check
- Skill development discussion
- Career aspiration alignment
- Feedback exchange (both directions)

### 11.2 Difficult Conversations Guide

**Preparation:**
1. Gather specific examples (SBI format)
2. Identify the desired outcome
3. Anticipate reactions and prepare responses
4. Choose appropriate time and private setting
5. Plan follow-up actions

**Conversation Structure:**
1. State the purpose clearly
2. Share specific observations (not judgments)
3. Listen to their perspective
4. Agree on the gap and its impact
5. Collaborate on solutions
6. Commit to next steps and follow-up

### 11.3 Calibration Preparation

**Before calibration, managers prepare:**
- Written justification for each rating
- 3 specific examples supporting the rating
- Comparison to level expectations (not peers)
- Development recommendations
- Promotion nominations with evidence
