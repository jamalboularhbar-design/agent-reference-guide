# ARG-Builder: Expansion Revenue Playbook

## Executive Summary

Expansion revenue — upsells, cross-sells, and seat expansions — is the engine that drives net revenue retention above 100% and compounds growth without proportional increases in customer acquisition cost. For ARG-Builder, the target is 120%+ net revenue retention, meaning the existing customer base grows 20%+ annually even with zero new customers. This playbook defines the triggers, motions, pricing, and execution strategies for systematically expanding revenue within the installed base.

## Expansion Motions

### Motion 1: Seat Expansion (Lowest Friction)

**Trigger:** New team members join, or departments request access

**Process:**

| Step | Action | Owner | Timeline |
|------|--------|-------|----------|
| 1 | Detect new team members or access requests | System/CSM | Automatic |
| 2 | Send "Add Your Team" email with self-serve link | System | Day 0 |
| 3 | If no action in 5 days: CSM follow-up | CSM | Day 5 |
| 4 | Process seat addition (self-serve or assisted) | Customer/CSM | Day 5–7 |
| 5 | Onboard new users with quick-start guide | System | Day 7 |

**Pricing:** $200–$500/seat/month depending on tier. Volume discounts at 25+ seats (10%), 50+ seats (15%), 100+ seats (20%).

### Motion 2: Tier Upgrade

**Trigger:** Customer hitting plan limits, requesting premium features, or demonstrating high maturity

**Process:**

| Step | Action | Owner | Timeline |
|------|--------|-------|----------|
| 1 | Identify upgrade signal (usage limits, feature requests) | System/CSM | Automatic |
| 2 | Prepare upgrade business case (ROI of premium features) | CSM | Day 1–2 |
| 3 | Schedule "strategic review" meeting | CSM | Day 3 |
| 4 | Present upgrade recommendation with ROI analysis | CSM | Meeting |
| 5 | Handle objections, offer trial of premium features | CSM | Meeting |
| 6 | Process upgrade (immediate or at renewal) | AE | Day 7–14 |
| 7 | Activate premium features and provide training | CSM | Day 14 |

**Upgrade Paths:**

| From | To | ACV Increase | Key Unlocks |
|------|-----|-------------|-------------|
| Starter ($2K/mo) | Professional ($5K/mo) | +150% | Advanced analytics, API access, priority support |
| Professional ($5K/mo) | Enterprise ($10K/mo) | +100% | Custom integrations, dedicated CSM, SLA, SSO |
| Starter ($2K/mo) | Enterprise ($10K/mo) | +400% | All premium features + white-glove service |

## Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Framework

The QBR is the primary vehicle for identifying and advancing expansion opportunities. Every QBR should include an expansion discussion.

**QBR Agenda (60 minutes):**

| Segment | Duration | Purpose | Expansion Angle |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------------|
| Value delivered | 15 min | Review metrics, ROI, success stories | Build case for more investment |
| Adoption insights | 10 min | Usage analytics, team engagement | Identify underserved teams |
| Roadmap preview | 10 min | Upcoming features and capabilities | Create desire for premium features |
| Strategic alignment | 15 min | Business goals for next quarter | Map expansion to their priorities |
| Expansion discussion | 10 min | Present specific expansion recommendation | Close or advance deal |

**QBR Expansion Preparation Checklist:**

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Usage analytics report | Demonstrate value and identify gaps |
| ROI calculation | Quantify return on current investment |
| Expansion recommendation | Specific, data-backed suggestion |
| Pricing proposal | Ready to present if interest is shown |
| Success story from similar customer | Social proof for expansion |
| Roadmap items relevant to their goals | Create urgency for premium tier |
