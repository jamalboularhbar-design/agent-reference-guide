# ARG-Builder: Crisis Communications Playbook

## Executive Summary

This document defines ARG-Builder's crisis communications framework — the protocols, templates, spokesperson guidelines, and reputation management strategies that ensure clear, consistent, and trustworthy communication during any crisis. In the SaaS industry, how a company communicates during a crisis often matters more than the crisis itself. This playbook ensures the team responds swiftly, transparently, and professionally to protect customer trust and brand reputation.

## Crisis Classification

### Severity Levels

| Level | Definition | Examples | Response Time | Spokesperson |
|-------|-----------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Critical (P0)** | Existential threat to business or customers | Data breach, extended outage (> 4 hours), legal action, executive misconduct | < 1 hour | CEO |
| **High (P1)** | Significant impact on customers or reputation | Partial outage, security vulnerability, negative press, key employee departure | < 4 hours | VP/Director |
| **Medium (P2)** | Moderate impact, contained scope | Feature malfunction, billing error, customer complaint going viral | < 24 hours | Communications lead |
| **Low (P3)** | Minor issue, limited exposure | Typo in communications, minor UX bug, individual customer complaint | < 48 hours | Support team |

### Crisis Categories

| Category | Examples | Primary Concern |
|----------|---------|-----------------|
| Technical | Outage, data loss, security breach | Customer data safety, service restoration |
| Legal | Lawsuit, regulatory action, IP dispute | Legal exposure, compliance |
| People | Executive departure, layoffs, misconduct | Culture, trust, continuity |
| Financial | Missed targets, funding issues, cash crunch | Investor confidence, employee retention |
| Reputational | Negative press, viral complaint, competitor attack | Brand trust, market position |
| External | Market crash, pandemic, geopolitical | Business continuity, customer support |

## Communication Templates

### Data Breach Notification

**Subject:** Important Security Notice from ARG-Builder

> Dear [Customer Name],
>
> We are writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected your account. On [date], we detected [brief, factual description of what happened].
>
> **What happened:** [Clear, factual description without technical jargon]
>
> **What information was involved:** [Specific data types affected]
>
> **What we are doing:** [Actions taken — investigation, remediation, prevention]
>
> **What you can do:** [Specific steps customers should take]
>
> **How to reach us:** [Dedicated support channel for this incident]
>
> We take the security of your data extremely seriously. We are committed to full transparency as our investigation continues, and we will provide updates as we learn more.
>
> [CEO Name], CEO, ARG-Builder

### Service Outage Communication

**Status Page Update:**

> **[Timestamp] — Investigating**
> We are aware of an issue affecting [specific service/feature]. Our engineering team is actively investigating. We will provide updates every 30 minutes.
>
> **[Timestamp] — Identified**
> We have identified the root cause: [brief technical explanation]. Our team is implementing a fix. Estimated resolution: [time estimate].
>
> **[Timestamp] — Resolved**
> The issue has been resolved. All services are operating normally. We will publish a full post-incident report within 48 hours.
>
> **Root Cause:** [Technical explanation]
> **Impact:** [Duration, affected customers, data impact]
> **Prevention:** [Steps taken to prevent recurrence]

### Negative Press Response

**For Media Inquiries:**

> "We are aware of [the article/report/claim]. [Acknowledge any valid points]. [Correct any inaccuracies with facts]. [State what you're doing about it]. We remain committed to [relevant company value/mission]. We're happy to provide additional context — please contact [media contact]."

### Employee Departure (Key Executive)

**Internal (All-Hands):**

> Team, I want to share that [Name] has decided to [leave/pursue other opportunities]. [Brief, respectful context]. [Name]'s contributions to [specific achievements] have been significant, and we wish them well.
>
> [Transition plan]. [Who's taking over]. [Timeline]. [Reassurance about company direction].

**External (If public-facing):**

> "[Name] has made the decision to pursue new opportunities. We thank them for their contributions to ARG-Builder and wish them well. [Transition plan]. Our leadership team remains strong, and we are excited about the road ahead."

## Spokesperson Guidelines

### Media Training Principles

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Bridge technique | Acknowledge → Bridge → Key message ("That's an important point, and what I want to emphasize is...") |
| Rule of three | Maximum 3 key messages per communication |
| Plain language | No jargon, no acronyms, speak like a human |
| Facts only | Never speculate, guess, or share unconfirmed information |
| Empathy first | Lead with impact on people, not technical details |
| Stay in lane | Only comment on what you know; defer to experts for specifics |

### Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Acknowledge the situation quickly | Ignore or "no comment" |
| Express empathy for those affected | Blame others or make excuses |
| Provide factual information | Speculate or guess |
| Commit to transparency and updates | Over-promise on timeline |
| Take responsibility where appropriate | Admit legal liability without counsel |
| Direct to official channels | Engage with trolls or hostile actors |
| Prepare written statements | Go off-script in high-stakes situations |

## Post-Crisis Review

### Post-Incident Report Template

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Executive summary | What happened, impact, resolution (3 sentences) |
| Timeline | Detailed chronological account of events |
| Root cause | Technical and organizational root cause |
| Impact assessment | Customers affected, data impact, financial impact |
| Response evaluation | What went well, what didn't, communication effectiveness |
| Corrective actions | Specific steps to prevent recurrence (with owners and dates) |
| Lessons learned | Organizational learnings and process improvements |
| Follow-up | Customer remediation, ongoing monitoring |

### Review Meeting Agenda (Within 1 Week)

| Topic | Duration | Participants |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Timeline review (facts only) | 20 min | Full crisis team |
| What went well | 15 min | Full crisis team |
| What could improve | 20 min | Full crisis team |
| Communication effectiveness | 15 min | Communications + CS |
| Corrective actions (assign owners) | 20 min | Full crisis team |
| Process improvements | 10 min | Crisis Lead |
