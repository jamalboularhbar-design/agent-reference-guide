# ArtKech Design Studio — New Business & Lead Generation

## 1. Purpose

A healthy creative studio maintains a consistent pipeline of new business opportunities. Relying on word-of-mouth alone creates dangerous revenue gaps. This SOP defines how ArtKech proactively generates leads, qualifies prospects, and converts inquiries into signed projects — while maintaining the premium positioning that justifies our rates.

## 3. Lead Qualification

### 3.1 BANT Framework (Adapted for Creative Services)

| Criterion | Questions to Ask | Qualified If |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget** | "Do you have a budget range in mind?" | Budget aligns with our minimum (> 8,000 MAD) |
| **Authority** | "Who makes the final decision on this project?" | Decision-maker is involved in conversations |
| **Need** | "What's driving this project? What problem does it solve?" | Clear business need (not just "we want something pretty") |
| **Timeline** | "When do you need this completed?" | Realistic timeline (not "yesterday") |

### 3.2 Qualification Scoring

| Factor | Score 1 (Low) | Score 3 (Medium) | Score 5 (High) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Budget fit | Below our minimum | At our minimum | Comfortable with our range |
| Decision process | Committee, unclear | 2-3 people, identified | Single decision-maker |
| Project clarity | Vague, undefined | General direction | Clear brief and objectives |
| Timeline | Unrealistic (< 1 week) | Tight but doable | Comfortable |
| Brand fit | Doesn't match our expertise | Adjacent to our work | Perfect for our portfolio |
| Growth potential | One-off, no future work | Possible future projects | Ongoing relationship likely |

**Score 24-30:** Hot lead — prioritize, respond within 2 hours
**Score 16-23:** Warm lead — respond within 24 hours, nurture
**Score 10-15:** Cool lead — respond within 48 hours, assess fit
**Score < 10:** Not qualified — polite decline or refer to another studio

### 3.3 Disqualification Criteria (Immediate Decline)

| Red Flag | Response |
| :--- | :--- |
| "Can you do it for free/very cheap? We'll give you exposure" | Polite decline, explain our pricing reflects professional value |
| Budget is < 5,000 MAD for a full brand identity | Refer to a junior freelancer, wish them well |
| Needs it "by tomorrow" with no rush fee discussion | Explain our process requires adequate time for quality |
| Wants to own the source files without paying full price | Explain IP transfer is included in our standard pricing |
| Has burned through 3+ designers on the same project | Red flag — ask why, likely unreasonable expectations |
| Requests spec work (free design before commitment) | Firm decline, explain our paid discovery process |

| Inquiry Source | First Response | Full Response |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

### 4.2 First Response Template

> Subject: Re: [Their inquiry topic] — ArtKech Design Studio
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for reaching out! We'd love to learn more about your project.
>
> To give you the best possible guidance, I'd love to schedule a brief discovery call (15-20 minutes) where we can discuss:
> - Your business goals and what you're looking to achieve
> - The scope and timeline you have in mind
> - How we might be able to help
>
> Would [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time] work for you? We can meet at our studio in Guéliz, or connect via Google Meet — whichever is more convenient.
>
> In the meantime, here are a few recent projects that might be relevant to your needs: [2-3 portfolio links]
>
> Looking forward to connecting!
>
> [Name], Account Manager
> ArtKech Design Studio

### 4.3 Discovery Call Agenda (15-20 minutes)

| Time | Topic | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 0-3 min | Introduction, rapport building | Build connection |
| 3-8 min | Their business, goals, challenges | Understand context |
| 8-12 min | Project specifics (scope, timeline, budget) | Qualify |
| 12-15 min | Our relevant experience (2-3 examples) | Build confidence |
| 15-18 min | Next steps (brief, quote, timeline) | Move forward |
| 18-20 min | Questions from them | Address concerns |

## 5. Proposal & Conversion

### 5.1 Proposal Timeline

| Step | Duration | Responsible |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Discovery call completed | — | Account Manager |
| Internal estimation | Within 24 hours | Account Manager + Creative Director |
| Proposal drafted | Within 24 hours of estimation | Account Manager |
| Proposal reviewed | Within 4 hours | Creative Director |
| Proposal sent to prospect | Within 48 hours of discovery call | Account Manager |
| Follow-up (if no response) | Day 3 | Account Manager |
| Second follow-up | Day 7 | Account Manager |
| Final follow-up | Day 14 | Account Manager |
| Proposal expires | Day 30 | Automatic |

### 5.2 Conversion Tactics

| Situation | Tactic |
| :--- | :--- |
| Prospect is comparing studios | Emphasize our unique value (strategic thinking, local knowledge, ongoing support) |
| Prospect is hesitant on price | Offer phased approach or reduced scope (never reduce rate) |
| Prospect needs internal buy-in | Provide a summary document they can share with decision-makers |
| Prospect goes silent | Send a relevant case study or industry insight (add value, don't just follow up) |
| Prospect says "not now" | Schedule a check-in for their stated timeline, add to nurture list |

### 5.3 Win/Loss Analysis

After every proposal (won or lost), document:
- Why we won: What resonated? Price, portfolio, chemistry, speed?
- Why we lost: Price too high? Wrong fit? Competitor advantage? Timing?
- Lessons: What would we do differently next time?

## 7. CRM & Pipeline Management

### 7.1 Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Definition | Expected Duration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| New inquiry | Just received, not yet responded | < 4 hours |
| Discovery | Initial conversation scheduled or completed | 1-3 days |
| Proposal sent | Quote delivered, awaiting response | 3-14 days |
| Negotiation | Active discussion on scope/price/terms | 3-7 days |
| Won (deposit received) | Contract signed, deposit paid | — |
| Lost | Declined or no response after 30 days | — |
| Nurture | Not ready now, future potential | Ongoing |

### 7.2 Pipeline Health Metrics

| Metric | Healthy Range | Action if Outside |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Total pipeline value | 3-5x monthly revenue target | Increase outreach if below 3x |
| Proposals outstanding | 5-10 at any time | Increase lead gen if below 5 |
| Average time in pipeline | < 21 days (inquiry to decision) | Improve follow-up speed |
| Win rate | 35-50% | Review qualification if below 35% |
| Pipeline coverage ratio | > 3:1 (pipeline value : revenue target) | Increase prospecting |
