# Go-to-Market Strategy: OpsCanvas by ARG Builder

## Confidential — Prepared May 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This document outlines the complete go-to-market strategy for launching **OpsCanvas** — an AI-powered operational reference and knowledge management platform — into the North American mid-market and enterprise segments. The launch is targeted for **mid-June 2026** with in-person meetings in New York and San Francisco.

OpsCanvas transforms how companies build, maintain, and consume operational knowledge. Unlike generic wikis (Notion, Confluence) or narrow training tools (Trainual), OpsCanvas delivers **structured operational intelligence** with AI-powered features, dual-persona workflows, analytics, and enterprise-grade governance — all in a premium, purpose-built experience.

The AI-driven knowledge management market is projected to grow from $5.23 billion in 2024 to $7.71 billion, with worldwide SaaS spending reaching $299 billion in 2025 [1] [2]. OpsCanvas enters this market with a differentiated product that is already built and functional, giving us a significant advantage over competitors still in development.

## 2. Product Naming and Positioning

### Brand Name: **OpsCanvas**

**Tagline:** "Paint the complete picture of how your company operates."

**Why OpsCanvas:** The name combines "Ops" (immediately signals operational focus) with "Canvas" (implies a creative, visual, comprehensive workspace). It positions the product above utilitarian wiki tools while signaling that this is purpose-built for operations — not another generic docs platform. The name is unique in the SaaS landscape, memorable, and works equally well for mid-market HR directors and enterprise CTOs.

**Alternative Names (Backup):**

| Name | Tagline | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Meridian** | "Navigate operational complexity with confidence." | Premium, geographic metaphor. Some name conflicts in adjacent spaces. |
| **Cadence** | "Operational rhythm for growing teams." | Beautiful word but Cadence Design Systems is a $70B company. |
| **Clarion** | "Clear signals for every team." | Strong but bioMerieux has a healthcare product called CLARION. |

### Positioning Statement

> For VP/Directors of Operations and HR leaders at mid-market and enterprise companies who struggle with scattered, outdated operational knowledge across multiple tools, **OpsCanvas** is an AI-powered operational reference platform that delivers structured, governed, and measurable knowledge management. Unlike Notion (too generic), Confluence (too technical), Trainual (too narrow), and Guru (too expensive), OpsCanvas provides dual-persona workflows, AI-powered content intelligence, and enterprise analytics in a premium experience that teams actually want to use.

### Category Creation

OpsCanvas does not compete in the "knowledge base" or "wiki" category. We are creating the **Operational Intelligence Platform** category — a new segment that combines:

- Structured operational documentation (not free-form wikis)
- AI-powered content governance (not manual curation)
- Operational analytics and benchmarks (not just page views)
- Role-based operational views (not one-size-fits-all)

## 3. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary Segment: Mid-Market Companies (200–2,000 employees)

These companies have outgrown Google Docs and basic wikis but cannot afford or justify the complexity of enterprise platforms like ServiceNow Knowledge Management.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Company size** | 200–2,000 employees |
| **Revenue** | $20M–$500M ARR |
| **Industries** | SaaS/Technology, Professional Services, Financial Services, Healthcare, E-commerce |
| **Growth stage** | Series B through pre-IPO |
| **Pain signal** | Recently hired VP/Director of Ops, or recently reorganized teams |
| **Tech stack** | Slack, Google Workspace or Microsoft 365, Notion/Confluence (frustrated with it) |

### Secondary Segment: Enterprise (2,000–10,000 employees)

Larger organizations with dedicated operations teams looking to consolidate knowledge tools and add governance.

### Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: "Operations Olivia" — VP/Director of Operations**

Olivia manages 5–15 direct reports across operations, customer success, and process improvement. She has tried building an internal wiki in Notion three times. Each time, it became a graveyard within 6 months because nobody maintained it and there was no way to measure adoption.

- **Title:** VP Operations, Director of Business Operations, Head of Ops
- **Reports to:** COO or CEO
- **Budget authority:** $5K–$50K/year for operational tools
- **Key pain points:** Scattered SOPs, no visibility into doc freshness, new hires take 3–6 months to find processes, no analytics on knowledge consumption
- **Buying triggers:** New hire wave, failed audit, post-incident review revealing process gaps
- **Decision timeline:** 2–4 weeks for mid-market, 6–12 weeks for enterprise

**Persona 2: "HR Hannah" — Director of People / HR**

Hannah owns onboarding and is measured on time-to-productivity for new hires. She currently uses a combination of Google Docs, Loom videos, and a Notion workspace that nobody updates.

- **Title:** Director of People, Head of HR, VP Human Resources
- **Reports to:** CEO or COO
- **Budget authority:** $10K–$100K/year (L&D budget)
- **Key pain points:** Inconsistent onboarding, can't track if new hires read required materials, no way to measure knowledge retention
- **Buying triggers:** Rapid hiring phase, onboarding NPS drop, compliance requirements

**Persona 3: "CTO Carlos" — VP Engineering / CTO**

Carlos needs engineering playbooks, incident response docs, and architectural decision records in one place. His team uses Confluence but hates it.

- **Title:** CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Platform
- **Reports to:** CEO
- **Budget authority:** $10K–$50K/year (engineering tools budget)
- **Key pain points:** Tribal knowledge, outdated runbooks, incident response docs scattered across Slack threads
- **Buying triggers:** Major incident caused by missing documentation, SOC 2 audit preparation

## 4. Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Philosophy

Position OpsCanvas as a **premium, purpose-built platform** — not a cheap wiki alternative. Price above Trainual ($249/mo) and below Guru Enterprise (custom, typically $25+/seat). The goal is to signal quality while remaining accessible for mid-market budgets.

### Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price | Target | Includes |
|------|-------|--------|----------|
| **Starter** | $299/month flat (up to 25 users) | Small teams, departments | AI-powered document generation, full-text search, basic analytics, 3 custom workflows, knowledge graph (read-only), 14-day free trial |
| **Professional** | $15/user/month (25-user minimum = $375/mo floor) | Mid-market companies (25–500 employees) | Everything in Starter + interactive knowledge graph, duplicate detection, approval workflows, advanced analytics, SSO/SAML, custom branding, priority support, dedicated CSM (100+ seats) |
| **Enterprise** | Custom (starting $2,500/mo) | Enterprise 500+ employees | Everything in Professional + custom AI model training, on-premise deployment, custom integrations & API, compliance (SOC 2, HIPAA), multi-workspace, 24/7 white-glove support, quarterly business reviews |

### Pricing Justification

Guru charges $25/seat/month for their self-serve plan with a 10-seat minimum ($250/mo floor) [3]. Trainual charges $249/month flat for up to 10 seats [4]. ARG Builder at $299/month for 25 users delivers 2.5x more seats than Trainual at the same price point, while including AI features that competitors charge extra for. The $15/user Professional tier undercuts Guru by 40% and positions ARG Builder as the clear value leader in the mid-market AI knowledge management space.

### Implementation Services

| Service | Price | Scope |
|---------|-------|-------|
| **Standard Implementation** | $5,000 one-time | AI-powered operational audit, system configuration, and team onboarding (1–2 weeks) |
| **Custom Implementation** | $15,000–$50,000 | Multi-department deployment with custom integrations, data migration, and dedicated project manager |

### Free Trial

- **14-day free trial** on Professional tier, no credit card required
- Pre-loaded with industry-specific template content (not an empty workspace)
- Guided onboarding tour (already built into the product)
- Automatic email sequence during trial (Days 1, 3, 7, 10, 13)

### Annual Discount

- 20% discount for annual commitment:
  - Starter: $239/month (saves $720/year)
  - Professional: $12/user/month (saves $900/year at 25 seats)
- Enterprise: negotiated annually with multi-year discounts available
