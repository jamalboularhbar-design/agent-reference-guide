# Riad & Routes — Staff Onboarding & Training Program

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the onboarding journey for new team members and the ongoing training framework for all Riad & Routes staff. In boutique luxury hospitality, every single team member is a brand ambassador — there is no "back of house" invisible to guests. Our training ensures consistent excellence regardless of role.

## 2. Onboarding Timeline

### 2.1 Week 1: Foundation

| Day | Focus | Activities | Responsible |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Day 1 | Welcome & orientation | Property tour, meet the team, uniform fitting, locker assignment, HR paperwork | HR Manager + GM |
| Day 2 | Brand immersion | Brand story, values, guest personas, competitive positioning, service philosophy | GM |
| Day 3 | Systems training | PMS login, WhatsApp protocols, shift scheduling app, emergency contacts | IT + Dept Head |
| Day 4 | Department-specific | Role-specific procedures, shadow experienced colleague, observe service | Department Head |
| Day 5 | Guest interaction basics | HEART method, greeting standards, name usage, body language, dress code | Guest Relations Manager |

### 2.2 Week 2: Shadowing

- New hire shadows an experienced team member for their entire shift (5 days)
- Gradually takes on simple tasks under supervision
- End-of-day debrief with Department Head (15 minutes): what went well, questions, concerns
- No solo guest interaction yet (always paired)

### 2.3 Week 3: Supervised Practice

- New hire performs role independently but with supervisor nearby
- Handles guest interactions with backup available
- Receives daily feedback (written notes from supervisor)
- Takes the "Service Standards Quiz" (must score 85%+ to proceed)

### 2.4 Week 4: Assessment & Sign-Off

- Department Head conducts formal assessment (practical + written)
- GM conducts a 15-minute conversation about the brand and service philosophy
- If passed: new hire is fully operational, probation continues for 3 months
- If not passed: extend training by 1 week with specific improvement areas identified

### 5.2 Cross-Training Program

Selected high-performing staff spend 2 days per quarter in a different department:
- Housekeepers spend time at front desk (understand guest communication)
- Front desk staff join a tour (understand the experience they're selling)
- Kitchen staff serve in the restaurant (understand guest-facing pressure)
- All staff do one hammam observation (understand the wellness offering)

Benefits: empathy between departments, backup capability, career development, reduced silos.

## 6. Performance Assessment

### 6.1 Probation Review (3 months)

| Criteria | Weight | Pass Threshold |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Technical competence (role-specific skills) | 30% | 80% |
| Guest interaction quality | 25% | 85% |
| Reliability (punctuality, attendance) | 20% | 95% |
| Team collaboration | 15% | 80% |
| Initiative & attitude | 10% | 75% |

### 6.2 Annual Performance Review

Conducted every January by Department Head + HR:
- Self-assessment form (completed by staff member)
- Manager assessment (using same criteria as probation, plus growth metrics)
- Guest feedback compilation (any mentions of the staff member by name)
- Goal setting for the coming year (2-3 specific, measurable goals)
- Career development discussion (where do they want to be in 2 years?)

## 7. Disciplinary Framework

| Level | Trigger | Action | Record |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Verbal warning | Minor tardiness, uniform issue, small oversight | Private conversation with supervisor | Noted in file, expires after 3 months |
| Written warning | Repeated minor issues, single moderate failure | Formal meeting with HR + Dept Head | In file for 6 months |
| Final warning | Serious failure, repeated written warnings | Meeting with GM, improvement plan (30 days) | In file for 12 months |
| Dismissal | Gross misconduct, theft, violence, guest safety breach | Immediate suspension pending investigation | Permanent |

**Gross misconduct (immediate dismissal):** theft, violence, intoxication on duty, guest harassment, deliberate property damage, sharing guest information externally, photographing guests without consent.
