# ARG-Builder: Board Deck Template & Reporting Framework

## Executive Summary

This document provides a complete board reporting framework for ARG-Builder, including monthly investor update templates, quarterly board deck structures, KPI dashboards, and strategic discussion frameworks. The templates are designed to build investor confidence through transparent, data-driven communication that demonstrates operational excellence and strategic clarity.

## Monthly Investor Update (Email Format)

Send to all investors, advisors, and key stakeholders on the 5th of each month. Keep it concise (under 500 words) and scannable. The goal is to maintain momentum, build trust, and make specific asks.

### Template Structure

---

**Subject:** ARG-Builder — [Month Year] Update: [Key Headline]

**Example:** ARG-Builder — June 2026 Update: Crossed $200K MRR, Signed Enterprise Pilot

---

**HIGHLIGHTS** (Top 3 wins — lead with the strongest)

1. [Revenue/growth milestone with specific number]
2. [Product/customer milestone]
3. [Team/operational milestone]

**KEY METRICS**

| Metric | This Month | Last Month | MoM Change |
|--------|-----------|------------|------------|
| MRR | $XXX,XXX | $XXX,XXX | +XX% |
| Customers | XX | XX | +X |
| Pipeline Value | $X.XM | $X.XM | +XX% |
| Team Size | XX | XX | +X |
| Burn Rate | $XXX,XXX | $XXX,XXX | — |
| Runway | XX months | XX months | — |

**CHALLENGES** (1–2 honest challenges — shows self-awareness)

1. [Challenge + what you're doing about it]
2. [Challenge + what you're doing about it]

**ASKS** (Be specific — investors want to help)

1. [Specific introduction request: "Looking for intro to VP Ops at [Company]"]
2. [Specific advice request: "Anyone have experience with [topic]?"]

**WHAT'S NEXT** (3 priorities for next 30 days)

1. [Priority with measurable target]
2. [Priority with measurable target]
3. [Priority with measurable target]

---

### Monthly Update Best Practices

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Send consistently on the same date | Skip months (even bad months) |
| Lead with metrics and data | Use vague language ("things are going well") |
| Be honest about challenges | Hide problems or spin failures |
| Make specific, actionable asks | Say "let me know if you can help" |
| Keep under 500 words | Write essays |
| Include a forward-looking section | Only report on the past |

## Quarterly Board Deck (12 Slides)

Present quarterly to the board of directors (or advisory board pre-Series A). The deck should take 30–45 minutes to present with 15–30 minutes for discussion.

### Slide 1: Title & Agenda

Content: Company name, quarter (Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 20XX), date, attendees, and 4–5 agenda items.

### Slide 2: Executive Summary

A single slide that tells the story of the quarter in 4–5 sentences. Include the quarter's theme, key achievement, primary challenge, and outlook for next quarter. This slide should be readable in 30 seconds and convey whether the company is on track.

**Template:**

> Q[X] was a [strong/challenging/transitional] quarter defined by [key theme]. We [primary achievement with metric]. Our biggest challenge was [honest challenge], which we're addressing by [action]. Looking ahead to Q[X+1], we're focused on [top priority] with a target of [specific metric].

## KPI Dashboard Framework

### Tier 1: North Star Metrics (Review Daily)

| Metric | Definition | Target | Alert Threshold |
|--------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| MRR | Monthly recurring revenue | Growth plan | < 90% of plan |
| Active Customers | Customers with active subscription | Growth plan | Any churn |
| Team Adoption Rate | Avg. adoption across all customers | > 85% | < 75% |

### Tier 2: Growth Metrics (Review Weekly)

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| New Pipeline Created | Dollar value of new opportunities | $XXK/week |
| Qualified Meetings | Discovery calls with qualified prospects | X/week |
| Win Rate | Deals won / deals closed | > 18% |
| Time to Value | Days from contract to full deployment | < 14 days |
| NPS Score | Net Promoter Score | > 50 |

### Tier 3: Operational Metrics (Review Monthly)

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | > 68% |
| CAC | Total S&M spend / new customers | < $5K |
| LTV/CAC Ratio | Customer LTV / CAC | > 10x |
| Burn Multiple | Net Burn / Net New ARR | < 2x |
| Rule of 40 | Revenue growth % + profit margin % | > 40% |
| Magic Number | Net New ARR / Prior Quarter S&M | > 0.75 |

## Strategic Discussion Frameworks

### Framework 1: Build vs. Buy vs. Partner

When evaluating whether to build a feature internally, acquire a company/product, or partner with an existing solution:

| Criterion | Build | Buy | Partner |
|-----------|-------|-----|---------|
| Time to market | Slow (months) | Fast (weeks) | Medium |
| Cost | Engineering time | Cash outlay | Revenue share |
| Control | Full | Full | Partial |
| Quality | Custom | Variable | Variable |
| Maintenance | Ongoing | Ongoing | Shared |

### Framework 2: Market Expansion Decisions

When evaluating new market segments, geographies, or verticals:

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Market size | How large is the opportunity? |
| Fit with current product | How much adaptation is needed? |
| Competition | Who else serves this market? |
| Go-to-market | Can we reach them with existing channels? |
| Unit economics | Will CAC/LTV remain healthy? |
| Strategic value | Does this strengthen our overall position? |
