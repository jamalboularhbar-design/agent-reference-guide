## 2. Negotiation Psychology

### 2.1 Buyer Psychology Framework

| Buyer Motivation | Signal | Counter-Strategy |
|-----------------|--------|-----------------|
| Price anchoring | "Your competitor quoted $X" | Reframe to value/TCO, don't match blindly |
| Authority play | "I need to check with my boss" | Get decision-maker involved earlier |
| Time pressure | "We need to decide by Friday" | Verify urgency, don't rush your process |
| Scarcity creation | "We have other options" | Acknowledge, differentiate on value |
| Reciprocity | "We gave you access to our team" | Acknowledge, but don't over-concede |
| Social proof | "Other vendors gave us better terms" | Ask for specifics, verify claims |
| Loss aversion | "We'll lose our data if we don't renew" | Never exploit — build trust instead |

### 2.2 Negotiation Mindset

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Negotiate value, not price | Always bring conversation back to outcomes |
| Understand their constraints | Budget cycles, approval processes, internal politics |
| Create options, not ultimatums | "Here are three ways we could structure this" |
| Silence is powerful | After stating price, wait — don't fill silence |
| Never negotiate against yourself | Don't offer discounts before being asked |
| Separate the people from the problem | Procurement is doing their job, not attacking you |
| Know when to walk away | Bad deals cost more than no deals |

## 3. Deal Structuring Options

### 3.1 Pricing Levers (Ordered by Preference)

| Lever | Description | Revenue Impact | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| Multi-year commitment | 2–3 year term for discount | Positive (guaranteed revenue) | Customer wants discount, we want predictability |
| Annual prepayment | Pay full year upfront | Positive (cash flow) | Customer has budget, wants discount |
| Volume commitment | More seats/units for lower per-unit | Positive (higher total ACV) | Customer growing, wants scale pricing |
| Bundling | Add features/modules for package price | Positive (higher ACV, more stickiness) | Customer evaluating multiple products |
| Payment timing | Quarterly instead of monthly | Neutral | Customer needs cash flow flexibility |
| Scope adjustment | Fewer seats/features at lower price | Negative (but retains customer) | Budget truly constrained |
| Straight discount | Reduce price without getting anything | Negative | Last resort only |

### 3.2 Creative Deal Structures

| Structure | How It Works | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Ramp deal | Start at lower price, increase over time | Startups with limited current budget but growth |
| Success-based pricing | Base + bonus tied to outcomes | Customers wanting ROI guarantee |
| Pilot-to-full | Reduced scope/price for 90 days, then full contract | Risk-averse buyers |
| Land-and-expand | Start with one team, expand pricing built in | Large organizations, bottom-up sale |
| Co-marketing deal | Discount in exchange for case study + reference | Brand-building phase |
| Advisory board seat | Discount for product input + reference | Strategic customers |
| Prepaid credits | Buy credits at discount, use over time | Usage-based components |
| Consortium pricing | Multiple related entities under one agreement | Holding companies, franchise groups |

### 3.3 Deal Structure Decision Matrix

| Customer Situation | Recommended Structure | Discount Range |
|-------------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Large budget, wants best price | Multi-year + annual prepay | 15–25% |
| Limited budget, high potential | Ramp deal (Year 1 discount, Year 2 full) | 20% Year 1, 0% Year 2+ |
| Risk-averse, needs proof | 90-day pilot → annual contract | Pilot at 50%, full at 0% |
| Procurement-driven, needs "win" | Slightly inflated list → negotiated discount | 10–15% (from inflated list) |
| Strategic account, long-term value | Advisory board + co-marketing | 10–15% + non-monetary value |
| Multiple business units | Enterprise agreement with volume tiers | 10–20% based on total commitment |
| Competitive displacement | Migration credits + extended trial | 15% + $X migration credit |

## 4. Objection Handling

### 4.1 Price Objection Responses

| Objection | Response Framework | Example |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe to value/ROI | "Let's look at what this saves you — at 10 hours/week saved across 5 people, that's $X/year in productivity" |
| "Competitor is cheaper" | Differentiate, TCO comparison | "What's included in their price? Let me show you the total cost comparison including implementation, support, and features" |
| "Over our budget" | Explore creative structures | "What if we structured this as a ramp deal — start lower and grow into full pricing as you see value?" |
| "Need a discount" | Trade for value | "I can offer X% if you commit to a 2-year term. What would make a longer commitment work for you?" |
| "Can't justify the cost" | Build business case together | "Let me help you build the internal business case. What metrics does your CFO care about?" |
| "Free alternatives exist" | Value differentiation | "Free tools cost you in time, risk, and missed outcomes. Let me quantify that..." |

### 4.2 Non-Price Objections

| Objection | Category | Response |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| "We need feature X" | Product gap | "That's on our roadmap for Q3. Would you like to be a design partner?" |
| "Implementation seems complex" | Risk | "Here's our implementation plan — 80% of customers are live in 2 weeks" |
| "What if it doesn't work?" | Risk | "Let's define success criteria for a 90-day pilot" |
| "We need to involve more stakeholders" | Process | "Who else should we include? I can prepare materials for each audience" |
| "Not the right time" | Timing | "What would need to change? Let's set a follow-up for when timing is better" |
| "We're locked into a contract" | Switching cost | "When does it expire? We can plan migration and offer a bridge" |

## 5. Discount Governance

### 5.1 Discount Authority Matrix

| Discount Level | Approver | Required Justification |
|---------------|----------|----------------------|
| 0–10% | AE (self-approve) | Standard competitive/multi-year |
| 11–15% | Sales Manager | Written justification + deal structure |
| 16–20% | VP Sales | Business case + strategic rationale |
| 21–25% | CRO/CEO | Executive sponsor + board-level strategic value |
| > 25% | CEO + Board awareness | Exceptional circumstances only |

### 5.2 Discount Justification Framework

| Justification | Acceptable | Not Acceptable |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| Multi-year commitment | Yes (standard) | — |
| Annual prepayment | Yes (standard) | — |
| Volume/seats above threshold | Yes (tiered pricing) | — |
| Strategic reference customer | Yes (with co-marketing agreement) | — |
| Competitive pressure | Yes (with evidence) | "They said competitor is cheaper" (unverified) |
| Budget constraint | Maybe (with ramp structure) | "They just want it cheaper" |
| End-of-quarter urgency | No | Never discount just to hit quota |
| Relationship/friendship | No | Never |
| "They'll churn without it" | Maybe (retention, not new deal) | Without data supporting claim |

### 5.3 Discount Impact Tracking

| Metric | Target | Tracking |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Average discount (new deals) | < 12% | CRM reporting |
| Average discount (renewals) | < 5% | CRM reporting |
| Discount-to-close correlation | Track | Does discounting actually close more deals? |
| Revenue impact of discounts | Track | Total ARR left on table |
| Discount by AE | Track | Identify over-discounters |
| Discount by segment | Track | Are some segments over-discounted? |
| Win rate with/without discount | Track | Is discounting necessary? |

## 6. Competitive Pricing Situations

### 6.1 Competitive Response Framework

| Situation | Strategy | Tactics |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Head-to-head bake-off | Win on value, not price | ROI calculator, reference calls, custom demo |
| Customer shares competitor pricing | Acknowledge, don't match | "Let me show you why our customers pay more and stay longer" |
| Competitor offers free trial/freemium | Differentiate on outcomes | "Free tools cost you in hidden ways — let me quantify" |
| Customer using competitor as leverage | Call the bluff respectfully | "If [competitor] truly meets your needs, that might be the right choice" |
| Competitor undercutting significantly | Question sustainability, differentiate | "At that price point, here's what you're likely giving up..." |
| Incumbent advantage (we're displacing) | Reduce switching cost | Migration assistance, overlap period, training |

### 6.2 Competitive Intelligence in Negotiation

| Intel Needed | Source | Use in Negotiation |
|-------------|--------|-------------------|
| Competitor pricing | Win/loss interviews, G2, sales intel | Understand market positioning |
| Competitor weaknesses | Customer feedback, reviews | Differentiate without attacking |
| Competitor contract terms | Customer disclosure (sometimes) | Offer better terms where possible |
| Competitor roadmap | Public announcements, analyst reports | Position our roadmap advantage |
| Customer's switching costs | Discovery questions | Quantify total cost of alternative |

## 7. Enterprise Procurement Navigation

### 7.1 Procurement Tactics & Counter-Tactics

| Procurement Tactic | What They're Doing | Your Counter |
|-------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| "We need 3 quotes" | Creating competition | Differentiate on value, not price |
| "Our standard terms" | Shifting to their contract | Start with yours, negotiate from there |
| "Volume discount required" | Anchoring on lower price | Tie discount to actual volume commitment |
| "Year-end budget pressure" | Creating urgency for you | Don't rush — their urgency isn't yours |
| "Previous vendor gave us X%" | Anchoring on competitor terms | "Every vendor is different — let's focus on value" |
| "Take it or leave it" | Power play | Pause, don't react emotionally, explore options |
| "We need to see your cost structure" | Justifying their price target | "Our pricing reflects value delivered, not cost" |
| Silence after proposal | Waiting for you to negotiate against yourself | Wait. Ask "What questions do you have?" |

### 7.2 Procurement Relationship Building

| Approach | Implementation |
|----------|---------------|
| Respect their role | Procurement adds value — acknowledge it |
| Give them a win | Structure deal so they can show savings |
| Be responsive | Fast turnaround on their requests |
| Be consistent | Same terms across their organization |
| Build long-term | This deal sets precedent for future |
| Understand their metrics | They're measured on savings — help them show it |

## 8. Renewal Pricing Strategy

### 8.1 Renewal Pricing Framework

| Scenario | Approach | Price Change |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Healthy, expanding customer | Standard increase + expansion | +5–7% base + expansion |
| Healthy, stable customer | Modest increase | +3–5% (CPI-linked) |
| At-risk customer | Price lock or reduction | 0% or -5–10% |
| Customer requesting downgrade | Offer alternatives first | Flexible (retain revenue) |
| Multi-year renewal | Discount for commitment | -5–10% for 2–3 year |
| Competitive threat at renewal | Value reinforcement + modest concession | 0–5% increase (not decrease) |

### 8.2 Price Increase Communication

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|-------------|
| Timing | 90+ days before renewal |
| Justification | New features, value delivered, market rates |
| Framing | "Investment in your success" not "price increase" |
| Options | Offer ways to manage cost (annual prepay, multi-year) |
| Relationship | CSM delivers, not billing system |
| Escalation path | Clear path if customer pushes back |
