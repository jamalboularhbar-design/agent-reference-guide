# ARG-Builder: M&A Strategy & Acquisition Playbook

## Executive Summary

This document defines ARG-Builder's mergers and acquisitions strategy — both as a potential acquirer of complementary technologies and as a potential acquisition target. M&A is a strategic lever for accelerating growth, filling capability gaps, and ultimately maximizing shareholder value.

## Acquisition Strategy (ARG-Builder as Acquirer)

### Strategic Rationale for Acquisitions

| Objective | Description | Priority | Timeline |
|-----------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Technology acceleration | Acquire capabilities that would take 12+ months to build | High | Year 2+ |
| Market expansion | Enter new verticals or geographies via established player | Medium | Year 3+ |
| Talent acquisition (acqui-hire) | Acquire teams with specialized expertise | High | Year 1+ |
| Customer base | Acquire customers in target segments | Medium | Year 2+ |
| Competitive elimination | Remove emerging competitors | Low | Year 3+ |

### Acquisition Process

| Phase | Duration | Activities | Key Deliverables |
|-------|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Identification | Ongoing | Market scanning, network, conferences | Target list (20–30 companies) |
| Initial assessment | 2 weeks | Public research, mutual introduction | One-pager assessment |
| First meeting | 1 week | Founder meeting, vision alignment | Meeting notes, interest level |
| Deep dive | 4 weeks | Technical review, financial analysis, customer calls | Due diligence memo |
| Valuation | 2 weeks | Financial modeling, comparable analysis | Valuation range |
