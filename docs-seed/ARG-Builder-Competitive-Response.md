## 4. Reactive Response Protocols

### 4.1 Scenario: Customer Evaluating Competitor

**Detection Signals:**
- Customer mentions competitor by name
- Asks for feature comparison
- Requests pricing match
- Engagement metrics declining
- Champion goes quiet

**Response Protocol (DEFEND):**

1. **D**iscover — What triggered the evaluation? What's the real need?
2. **E**mpathize — Acknowledge their due diligence is smart
3. **F**rame — Reframe the evaluation criteria in our favor
4. **E**vidence — Provide proof points (case studies, ROI data)
5. **N**eutralize — Address specific competitor claims
6. **D**ifferentiate — Reinforce unique value only we provide

**Response Template:**
```
"I appreciate you doing thorough due diligence — that's exactly the kind of 
rigor we'd expect from a team like yours. 

I'd love to understand what's driving the evaluation. Is it [specific need]? 

What I've found is that teams evaluating [Competitor] alongside ARG-Builder 
often discover they're comparing two fundamentally different approaches. 
[Competitor] requires [manual effort/time/limitation], while ARG-Builder 
[autonomous capability/speed/depth].

Would it be helpful if I connected you with [Customer Name] who evaluated 
both and can share their experience?"
```

### 4.2 Scenario: Competitor Launches Similar Feature

**Response Timeline:**

| Timeframe | Action | Owner |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| 0–24 hours | Internal assessment of competitor feature | Product |
| 24–48 hours | Talking points distributed to customer-facing teams | Marketing |
| 48–72 hours | Customer communication (if needed) | CS |
| 1 week | Blog post / thought leadership response | Marketing |
| 2 weeks | Feature enhancement or roadmap update (if warranted) | Product |

**Communication Framework:**
- Never disparage the competitor publicly
- Acknowledge innovation is good for the market
- Redirect to our deeper, more comprehensive approach
- Emphasize our track record and customer results
- Highlight what they still can't do that we can

### 4.3 Scenario: Competitor Undercuts on Price

**Response Strategy:**

1. **Never compete on price alone** — We sell value, not features
2. **Quantify the gap** — Show total cost of ownership (their price + implementation + maintenance + opportunity cost)
3. **ROI reframe** — "Our customers see 300%+ ROI. What's the ROI of saving $500/month but spending 10x more in manual effort?"
4. **Risk positioning** — "The cheapest option is often the most expensive when you factor in switching costs later"

**Discount Authority (if needed):**

| Situation | Max Discount | Approval Required |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Competitive displacement | 15% | Sales Manager |
| Multi-year commitment | 20% | VP Sales |
| Strategic logo | 25% | CRO |
| Enterprise (> $100K ACV) | 30% | CEO |

### 4.4 Scenario: Customer Churning to Competitor

**Save Protocol (7-day window):**

**Day 1:** Executive outreach (VP or C-level)
**Day 2:** Root cause analysis call
**Day 3:** Custom retention offer prepared
**Day 4:** Offer presentation with ROI data
**Day 5:** Decision deadline with incentive
**Day 6:** Final escalation if needed
**Day 7:** Accept decision gracefully, leave door open

**Retention Offers (escalating):**
1. Free premium support upgrade (3 months)
2. Custom feature development commitment
3. Pricing adjustment (up to 20% for 12 months)
4. Extended contract with locked pricing
5. Executive sponsorship and quarterly strategy sessions

## 6. Objection Handling for Competitive Situations

### 6.1 "We're already using [Competitor]"

**Response:**
"That makes sense — [Competitor] is a solid tool for [what they do well]. What we hear from teams who've made the switch is that [Competitor] handles [basic function] well, but they hit a ceiling when they need [our differentiator]. 

Can I ask — are you fully satisfied with [specific pain point area], or is that something you'd improve if you could?"

### 6.2 "[Competitor] is cheaper"

**Response:**
"You're right that their list price is lower. What we've found is that total cost of ownership tells a different story. When you factor in [setup time, manual maintenance, limited functionality, eventual migration], teams typically spend 3–5x more with [Competitor] than the sticker price suggests.

Our customers see an average ROI of 300%+ within 6 months. Would it be helpful to build a custom ROI model for your situation?"

### 6.3 "[Competitor] has feature X that you don't"

**Response:**
"That's a fair point — [feature X] is something [Competitor] has invested in. Two things I'd consider: First, is [feature X] a must-have or a nice-to-have for your specific use case? Second, our approach to [related capability] actually delivers [better outcome] because [reason].

I'd love to understand the specific problem you're trying to solve with [feature X] — there may be a better way to achieve that outcome."

### 6.4 "We want to build this internally"

**Response:**
"Building internally gives you maximum control — I respect that instinct. The question is whether it's the best use of your engineering team's time. 

Our customers have calculated that replicating ARG-Builder's capabilities internally would require [X engineers × Y months = $Z cost], and that's before ongoing maintenance. Plus, you'd miss out on the AI improvements we ship every month based on learnings across hundreds of customers.

What if we could give you the customization of an internal build with none of the maintenance burden?"

## 7. Win/Loss Intelligence Loop

### 7.1 Win/Loss Interview Process

**Conduct within 2 weeks of decision:**

**Win Interview Questions:**
1. What was the deciding factor in choosing ARG-Builder?
2. What other solutions did you evaluate?
3. What almost made you choose a competitor?
4. How would you describe ARG-Builder to a peer?
5. What could we have done better in the sales process?

**Loss Interview Questions:**
1. What was the primary reason for choosing [Competitor]?
2. What did we do well during the evaluation?
3. What could we have done differently?
4. Was there a specific moment the decision shifted?
5. Under what circumstances would you reconsider ARG-Builder?

### 7.2 Intelligence Distribution

| Insight Type | Distribution | Frequency | Action Owner |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Win patterns | Sales team | Weekly | Sales Enablement |
| Loss reasons | Product + Sales | Weekly | Product Marketing |
