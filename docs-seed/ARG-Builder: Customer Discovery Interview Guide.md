# ARG-Builder: Customer Discovery Interview Guide

## Purpose & Methodology

This guide provides a structured framework for conducting customer discovery interviews to validate product-market fit, refine positioning, and identify the highest-value customer segments for ARG-Builder. The methodology follows the "Jobs to Be Done" framework combined with solution-oriented discovery to understand both the problem landscape and the customer's willingness to pay for a solution.

## Interview Structure

Each interview should last 45–60 minutes and follow a five-section structure: context setting, problem exploration, current solutions, solution validation, and commercial exploration. The interviewer should maintain a 20/80 talk-to-listen ratio, asking open-ended questions and probing deeper on emotional responses.

---

## Section 1: Context Setting (5 minutes)

**Objective:** Establish rapport, confirm role and responsibilities, and understand organizational context.

**Opening Script:**

"Thank you for taking the time today. I'm researching how mid-market companies manage operational knowledge and team coordination. There are no right or wrong answers — I'm genuinely interested in understanding your experience. Everything you share will be kept confidential. May I record this conversation for my notes?"

**Questions:**

1. "Tell me about your role at [Company]. What does a typical week look like for you?"
2. "How large is your team, and how is it structured?"
3. "How long have you been in this role, and how has the organization changed during that time?"

**What to listen for:** Team size, growth trajectory, organizational complexity, reporting structure, and the interviewee's level of influence over operational decisions.

---

## Section 2: Problem Exploration (15 minutes)

**Objective:** Understand the depth, frequency, and emotional impact of operational documentation challenges.

**Questions:**

4. "When a new team member joins, walk me through what their first 90 days look like. How do they learn how things work here?"
5. "Think about the last time a key employee left. What happened to the knowledge they had? How did the team adapt?"
6. "How consistent are your team's processes across different people doing similar work? Can you give me a specific example of inconsistency you've noticed?"
7. "When someone on your team has a question about how to handle a specific situation, what do they do? Where do they go for answers?"
8. "What's the most frustrating operational challenge you've faced in the past 6 months?"
9. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one operational problem overnight, what would it be?"

**Probing Questions:**

- "How often does that happen?"
- "What does that cost you — in time, money, or quality?"
- "How does that make you feel when it happens?"
- "Who else is affected by this problem?"
- "What would change if this problem didn't exist?"

**What to listen for:** Emotional intensity (frustration, resignation, urgency), frequency of problems, quantifiable costs, and whether the problem is acknowledged at leadership level.

---

## Section 3: Current Solutions (10 minutes)

**Objective:** Understand what they've tried, what worked, what failed, and how much they've invested.

**Questions:**

10. "What have you tried to solve this problem? Walk me through the approaches you've used."
11. "Have you worked with consulting firms on operational documentation? What was that experience like?"
12. "Do you use any tools for documentation or knowledge management today? (Notion, Confluence, Guru, etc.) How's that working?"
13. "How much time and money have you invested in solving this problem over the past 2–3 years?"
14. "What worked about those approaches? What didn't work?"
15. "If you had to grade your current operational documentation on a scale of 1–10, what would you give it? Why?"

**What to listen for:** Previous spend (indicates budget availability), satisfaction level with current solutions, specific failure modes, and openness to new approaches.

---

## Section 4: Solution Validation (15 minutes)

**Objective:** Present the ARG-Builder concept and gauge reaction, interest, and perceived value.

**Concept Introduction:**

"Based on what I'm hearing from companies like yours, we're developing a solution that uses AI to autonomously generate interactive operational reference guides. Instead of spending months manually documenting processes, the system interviews your team, maps your operations, and produces a complete interactive system in about 2 weeks. The result includes searchable processes, interactive timelines, and built-in features that drive 92% team adoption. Let me describe what this looks like..."

**Questions:**

16. "Based on what I just described, what's your initial reaction?"
17. "Which aspects of this would be most valuable for your organization?"
18. "What concerns or questions come to mind?"
19. "Who else in your organization would need to be involved in evaluating something like this?"
20. "On a scale of 1–10, how interested would you be in learning more about this?"

**Probing Questions:**

- "What would need to be true for you to move forward with something like this?"
- "What would make you say 'no' to this?"
- "How does this compare to what you expected?"
- "What features would be must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?"

**What to listen for:** Enthusiasm level (body language, tone), specific value drivers, concerns and objections, decision-making process, and urgency.

---

## Section 5: Commercial Exploration (10 minutes)

**Objective:** Understand budget, decision-making process, timeline, and willingness to pay.

**Questions:**

21. "If a solution like this existed today and delivered the results I described, what would it be worth to your organization annually?"
22. "How does your organization typically evaluate and purchase software? Who's involved in the decision?"
23. "What's your budget cycle? When do you typically make purchasing decisions?"
24. "If you were to move forward with something like this, what would the timeline look like?"
25. "Would you be interested in being an early adopter and helping shape the product?"

**What to listen for:** Budget range, decision-making authority, purchasing timeline, and willingness to be an early adopter (strongest signal of product-market fit).

## Qualification Framework

### BANT+ Scoring

After each interview, score the prospect on the following dimensions:

| Dimension | Score 1 (Low) | Score 3 (Medium) | Score 5 (High) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Budget** | No budget identified | Budget exists but unallocated | Budget allocated for this problem |
| **Authority** | No decision-making power | Influences decision | Final decision maker |
| **Need** | Mild inconvenience | Recognized problem | Critical pain point |
| **Timeline** | No urgency | Within 12 months | Within 6 months |
| **Problem Severity** | Manageable | Significant | Critical/urgent |
| **Previous Investment** | Nothing spent | <$50K spent | >$100K spent |
| **Enthusiasm** | Polite interest | Genuine interest | Eager to proceed |

**Scoring Interpretation:**

- **Score 28–35:** Hot prospect — prioritize for pilot program
- **Score 21–27:** Warm prospect — nurture and follow up
- **Score 14–20:** Cool prospect — add to long-term pipeline
- **Score 7–13:** Not a fit — deprioritize

## Interview Logistics

### Target Interview Count

Conduct 30–50 interviews across the following segments to achieve statistical significance:

| Segment | Target Interviews | Priority |
|---------|-------------------|----------|
| Operations Directors (200–500 employees) | 10–15 | High |
| Operations Directors (500–1,000 employees) | 8–12 | High |
| HR/People Leaders | 5–8 | Medium |
| CTOs/Technology Leaders | 5–8 | Medium |
| CEOs/Founders (mid-market) | 3–5 | Medium |

### Recruitment Channels

Recruit interviewees through LinkedIn outreach (personalized connection requests), warm introductions from network, industry events and conferences, online communities and forums, and paid research panels (UserTesting, Respondent.io).

### Incentives

Offer $50–$100 Amazon gift card for 45-minute interviews, or offer early access to ARG-Builder at discounted rates for qualified prospects who express interest.

## Analysis Framework

### Pattern Recognition

After completing 10+ interviews, analyze patterns across the following dimensions:

**Problem Patterns:** Which problems are mentioned most frequently? Which generate the strongest emotional response? Which have the highest quantifiable cost?

**Solution Patterns:** What have prospects tried before? What worked and what failed? What's their ideal solution look like?

**Value Patterns:** Which features generate the most excitement? What's the perceived value range? What are the must-have vs. nice-to-have features?

**Commercial Patterns:** What's the typical budget range? Who are the decision makers? What's the typical timeline?

### Synthesis Report

After completing all interviews, produce a synthesis report including:

1. **Problem validation:** Confirmed problems ranked by severity and frequency
2. **Solution validation:** Features ranked by perceived value and demand
3. **Market sizing:** Refined TAM/SAM/SOM based on interview data
4. **Pricing validation:** Willingness to pay ranges by segment
5. **Positioning:** Most compelling value propositions by segment
6. **Objections:** Common concerns and effective responses
7. **Recommendations:** Product, pricing, and go-to-market adjustments
