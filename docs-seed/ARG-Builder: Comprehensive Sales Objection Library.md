# ARG-Builder: Comprehensive Sales Objection Library

## Executive Summary

This document contains 50+ categorized sales objections with structured response frameworks, supporting evidence, and follow-up strategies. Every objection is an opportunity to demonstrate value — the key is acknowledging the concern, reframing the conversation, and providing proof that eliminates doubt.

---

## Response Framework (LAER Method)

| Step | Action | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| **L** — Listen | Hear the full objection without interrupting | Show respect, understand fully |
| **A** — Acknowledge | Validate the concern ("That's a fair point...") | Build trust, reduce defensiveness |
| **E** — Explore | Ask clarifying questions to understand root cause | Uncover real objection |
| **R** — Respond | Provide evidence-based response | Overcome with proof |

## Category 1: Price Objections

### 1.1 "It's too expensive"

| Element | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Acknowledge** | "I appreciate you being direct about budget. Let's make sure the investment makes sense for your situation." |
| **Explore** | "When you say too expensive — compared to what? Your current solution cost, your budget, or what you expected?" |
| **Respond** | "Our average customer saves 35% in operational efficiency within 90 days. At your team size, that's approximately $X/month in recovered productivity — which means the platform pays for itself in [X] weeks." |
| **Evidence** | ROI calculator showing payback period; case study with similar company size |
| **Follow-up** | "Would it help if I put together a custom ROI analysis based on your specific team size and processes?" |

### 1.2 "We don't have budget for this"

| Element | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Acknowledge** | "Budget constraints are real — I completely understand." |
| **Explore** | "Is this a timing issue (budget cycle), a priority issue (other investments first), or a total budget limitation?" |
| **Respond** | "Many of our customers started by quantifying the cost of their current approach — scattered docs, slow onboarding, repeated questions. When they calculated the hidden cost ($X/month in lost productivity), the budget conversation shifted from 'can we afford this?' to 'can we afford not to?'" |
| **Evidence** | Cost-of-inaction calculator; customer quote about budget justification |
| **Follow-up** | "Would a phased approach work? Start with one department, prove ROI, then expand with next quarter's budget." |

### 1.3 "Competitor X is cheaper"

| Element | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Acknowledge** | "You're right — there are less expensive options out there." |
| **Explore** | "What specifically are you comparing? Is it the same scope of features, or a subset?" |
| **Respond** | "The price difference typically reflects the difference in outcomes. Our customers achieve 92% team adoption vs. industry average of 30–40% for general tools. The cheapest tool is the one that actually gets used. A $50/month tool with 20% adoption costs more per active user than our solution at full adoption." |
| **Evidence** | Feature comparison table; adoption rate data; total cost of ownership analysis |
| **Follow-up** | "Would you like me to build a side-by-side TCO comparison including implementation time and adoption rates?" |

### 1.4 "Can you offer a discount?"

| Element | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Acknowledge** | "I appreciate you asking — let me see what we can do." |
| **Explore** | "What would make this a clear yes for you? Is it the monthly cost, the annual commitment, or the total first-year investment?" |
| **Respond** | "I can offer [annual commitment discount / multi-year discount / expanded scope at same price]. What I can't do is discount the core platform because our pricing reflects the value and support level you'll receive." |
| **Evidence** | Value justification; what's included in the price; comparison to consulting alternative |
| **Follow-up** | "If I could [specific offer], would you be ready to move forward this week?" |

### 1.5 "We need to wait until next quarter/year"

| Element | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Acknowledge** | "I understand — timing matters." |
| **Explore** | "What changes next quarter that makes this possible? Is it budget, bandwidth, or a specific event?" |
| **Respond** | "The challenge with waiting is the cost of delay. Every month without a structured system costs your team approximately [X hours] in inefficiency. Over 3 months, that's [Y hours × $Z/hour] = [$total] in lost productivity. Starting now means you're already seeing ROI by the time next quarter arrives." |
| **Evidence** | Cost-of-delay calculation; customer who wished they'd started sooner |
| **Follow-up** | "What if we started with a pilot now — minimal commitment — so you're ready to scale when budget opens up?" |

## Category 5: Competitive Objections

### 5.1 "We're already evaluating [Competitor]"

| Element | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Acknowledge** | "Good — it's smart to evaluate options." |
| **Explore** | "What drew you to them? What criteria are most important in your decision?" |
| **Respond** | "I'd encourage you to evaluate both on three dimensions: (1) adoption rate — will your team actually use it daily? (2) time to value — how quickly will you see results? (3) total cost of ownership — including setup, training, and ongoing maintenance. We consistently win on all three." |
| **Evidence** | Head-to-head comparison; adoption data; implementation timeline comparison |

### 5.2 "We can just use Notion/Confluence for this"

| Element | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Acknowledge** | "Those are great tools — we actually integrate with both." |
| **Explore** | "Have you tried using them for operational reference guides before? What was the experience?" |
| **Respond** | "The difference is purpose-built vs. general-purpose. Notion/Confluence are excellent for general documentation. But for structured operational reference — with personas, process timelines, instant search, and team adoption — they require significant customization and maintenance. Our customers who switched report 3x higher adoption and 60% less maintenance time." |
| **Evidence** | Feature comparison; switching case studies; adoption data |

### 5.3 "We'll build it ourselves"

| Element | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Acknowledge** | "Your team is clearly capable — that's an option." |
| **Explore** | "Have you estimated the development time and ongoing maintenance? What's the opportunity cost of those engineering hours?" |
| **Respond** | "Based on our experience, building a comparable system takes 6–9 months of engineering time ($300K–$500K in salary cost), plus ongoing maintenance of 20–30% annually. Our annual cost is a fraction of that, you're live in days not months, and we handle all updates and improvements. The question is: should your engineers build internal tools, or ship features that drive revenue?" |
| **Evidence** | Build vs. buy analysis; opportunity cost calculation; time-to-value comparison |
