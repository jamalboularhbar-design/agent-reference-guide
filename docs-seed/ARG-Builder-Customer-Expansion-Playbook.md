## 4. Expansion Playbooks by Vector

### 4.1 Seat Expansion Playbook

**Trigger:** Utilization > 85% OR new team members joining

**Process:**
1. Monitor utilization dashboard weekly
2. Alert CSM when account hits 85% threshold
3. CSM reaches out with growth planning conversation
4. Present tiered pricing for additional seats
5. Offer volume discount for larger commitment
6. Process expansion (often self-serve for small adds)

**Script — Proactive Seat Expansion:**

> "Hi [Name], I noticed your team's usage of ARG-Builder has been growing steadily — you're currently at [X]% of your seat allocation. That's a great sign that the team is getting value!
>
> I wanted to proactively discuss your growth plans. Are there additional team members who could benefit from access? We have flexible options for adding seats, and I can offer [volume pricing] for a larger commitment.
>
> Would it be helpful to do a quick 15-minute planning call this week?"

**Pricing for Seat Expansion:**

| Current Seats | Additional Seats | Discount |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| 1–25 | 1–10 | List price |
| 26–50 | 11–25 | 5% volume discount |
| 51–100 | 26–50 | 10% volume discount |
| 100+ | 50+ | 15% volume discount |

**Upgrade Paths:**

### 4.3 Department Rollout Playbook

**Trigger:** Cross-department interest OR champion advocacy

**Process:**
1. Identify adjacent departments through usage patterns
2. Map stakeholders in target department
3. Leverage existing champion for warm introduction
4. Conduct department-specific discovery
5. Run mini-pilot (2 weeks, 5–10 users)
6. Present department-specific ROI
7. Close expansion deal

**Script — Cross-Department Introduction:**

> "Hi [Champion Name], the results your team has achieved with ARG-Builder have been impressive — [specific metric: e.g., 40% reduction in onboarding time].
>
> I'm curious — do you think [adjacent department, e.g., Sales, HR, Finance] faces similar challenges with operational knowledge? We've seen great success when companies expand ARG-Builder across departments because [specific cross-functional benefit].
>
> Would you be open to introducing me to [their counterpart in the other department]? I'd love to explore whether we could deliver similar results for their team. Of course, I'd make sure it reflects well on you as the person who brought this to the organization."

**Department Expansion Pricing:**

| 2 departments | Per-department pricing | 5% multi-dept discount |
| 3–5 departments | Platform pricing | 10% + volume seats |

## 5. Expansion Conversation Framework

### 5.1 The GROW Model for Expansion

| Step | Purpose | Key Questions |
|------|---------|---------------|
| **G**oal | Understand their evolving objectives | "What are your top priorities for the next quarter?" |
| **R**eality | Assess current state and gaps | "How is ARG-Builder helping today? Where are the gaps?" |
| **O**ptions | Present expansion possibilities | "Here are three ways we could help you achieve [goal]..." |
| **W**ill | Secure commitment and next steps | "Which option resonates most? What would you need to move forward?" |

### 5.2 Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Expansion Integration

**QBR Agenda (60 minutes):**

| Time | Topic | Expansion Angle |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 0–10 min | Value delivered (metrics review) | Anchor on ROI achieved |
| 10–25 min | Usage insights & recommendations | Identify underutilized features → upgrade |
| 25–40 min | Strategic alignment (their goals) | Map goals to expansion opportunities |
| 40–50 min | Roadmap preview | Create excitement for upcoming capabilities |
| 50–60 min | Action items & next steps | Propose specific expansion with timeline |

### 5.3 Expansion Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We don't have budget right now" | "I understand budget cycles. Let's plan for [next quarter/FY]. In the meantime, would a pilot help build the business case?" |
| "We need to prove value first" | "Absolutely. Let's define what 'proven value' looks like and set a review date. What metrics would convince you?" |
| "Other departments aren't ready" | "That's fair. What would need to be true for them to be ready? Can we start with a small pilot to demonstrate value?" |
| "We're happy with what we have" | "That's great to hear! I want to make sure you're aware of [capability] that could help with [their stated goal]. No pressure, but worth knowing about." |
| "The timing isn't right" | "When would be better? I'll set a reminder and come back with a specific proposal. In the meantime, anything I can help with?" |
