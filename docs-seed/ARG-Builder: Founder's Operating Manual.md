# ARG-Builder: Founder's Operating Manual

## Executive Summary

This is the CEO's personal operating system — the frameworks, rhythms, and decision-making protocols that enable effective leadership at each stage of company growth. Running a startup is not about working harder; it is about working on the right things at the right time with the right people. This manual provides the structure to maintain clarity, energy, and effectiveness while scaling from founding team to 50+ employees.

## Founder's Role by Stage

### Role Evolution

| Stage | Revenue | Team Size | Primary Role | Time Allocation |
|-------|---------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Pre-seed | $0–$100K | 1–3 | Builder + Seller | 60% product, 30% sales, 10% admin |
| Seed | $100K–$1M | 4–10 | Seller + Recruiter | 40% sales, 30% recruiting, 20% product, 10% admin |
| Series A | $1M–$5M | 10–30 | Leader + Fundraiser | 30% strategy, 25% people, 20% sales, 15% fundraising, 10% product |
| Series B | $5M–$15M | 30–75 | CEO + Culture Keeper | 35% strategy, 30% people, 20% external, 15% culture |
| Growth | $15M+ | 75+ | Visionary + Allocator | 40% strategy, 30% external, 20% people, 10% culture |

### What to Stop Doing at Each Stage

| Stage Transition | Stop Doing | Start Doing |
|-----------------|-----------|-------------|
| Pre-seed → Seed | Writing all the code | Hiring engineers, reviewing architecture |
| Seed → Series A | Closing every deal | Building sales team, coaching AEs |
| Series A → B | Managing individual contributors | Managing managers, setting OKRs |
| Series B → Growth | Attending every meeting | Empowering VPs, focusing on strategy |

## Decision-Making Framework

### Decision Categories

| Category | Examples | Who Decides | Process |
|----------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Reversible + Low Impact | Tool selection, meeting format, office snacks | Anyone | Just do it, inform team |
| Reversible + High Impact | Pricing change, new channel, process change | Manager + CEO awareness | Decide fast, monitor, adjust |
| Irreversible + Low Impact | Domain name, brand color, office location | CEO + 1 advisor | Decide within 1 week |
| Irreversible + High Impact | Fundraising, key hire, pivot, acquisition | CEO + board + advisors | Structured process, 2–4 weeks |

### Decision Speed Rules

| Rule | Implementation |
|------|---------------|
| 70% information rule | Decide with 70% of desired information; waiting for 100% is too slow |
| 2-way door test | If reversible, decide immediately. If irreversible, take time |
| Disagree and commit | Once decided, everyone executes fully regardless of initial position |
| Decision journal | Record decision, rationale, expected outcome, review date |
| Post-decision review | Review major decisions at 30/60/90 days |

### Decision Journal Template

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Date | [Decision date] |
| Decision | [What was decided] |
| Context | [What situation prompted this] |
| Options considered | [2–3 alternatives] |
| Rationale | [Why this option was chosen] |
| Expected outcome | [What we expect to happen] |
| Review date | [When to evaluate the decision] |
| Actual outcome | [Filled in at review date] |
| Learnings | [What we learned] |

## Energy Management

### Energy Audit

| Activity | Energy Effect | Optimization |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Customer conversations | +++ (energizing) | Schedule 2–3x/week, never skip |
| Product strategy | ++ (energizing) | Protect Wednesday deep work |
| Recruiting top talent | ++ (energizing) | Batch interviews on Thursdays |
| Board/investor management | Neutral | Prepare well, keep concise |
| Administrative tasks | - (draining) | Delegate or batch to Friday |
| Conflict resolution | -- (draining) | Address immediately, don't let fester |
| Fundraising | -- (draining) | Time-box to specific periods |

### Energy Protection Rules

| Rule | Implementation |
|------|---------------|
| No meetings before 10am | Morning for thinking, exercise, family |
| Energy-first scheduling | High-energy tasks in peak hours (9am–12pm) |
| Recovery after draining activities | 30-min buffer after difficult conversations |
| Weekly complete disconnect | One full day with no work (Saturday or Sunday) |
| Quarterly reset | 3–5 days completely away from business |
| Annual sabbatical (after Year 3) | 2 weeks fully disconnected |

### Preventing Burnout

| Warning Sign | Intervention |
|-------------|-------------|
| Working weekends regularly | Audit delegation, hire or restructure |
| Dreading Monday mornings | Reconnect with mission, talk to customers |
| Snapping at team members | Take 48 hours off, assess workload |
| Unable to make decisions | Reduce decision load, empower team |
| Physical symptoms (sleep, appetite) | Mandatory week off, professional support |
| Losing excitement about product | Spend time with customers, revisit vision |

## People Management

### 1:1 Meeting Structure (30 minutes)

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Their agenda | 15 min | What's on their mind? Blockers? Needs? |
| Coaching | 10 min | Feedback, development, growth |
| Alignment | 5 min | Priorities, upcoming decisions, context |

### Feedback Framework (SBI)

| Component | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **S**ituation | When and where | "In yesterday's customer call..." |
| **B**ehavior | What you observed (factual) | "...you interrupted the client twice..." |
| **I**mpact | The effect it had | "...which made them visibly frustrated and they cut the call short." |

### Hiring Decision Framework

| Signal | Strong Hire | No Hire |
|--------|------------|---------|
| Culture fit | Adds to culture, brings new perspective | Requires culture compromise |
| Skill level | Can do the job in 30 days | Needs 6+ months to ramp |
| Motivation | Genuinely excited about our mission | Primarily motivated by compensation |
| References | "One of the best I've worked with" | Lukewarm or hedging |
| Gut feeling | "I'd be excited to work with them daily" | "They're fine, I guess" |

**Rule:** If it's not a "hell yes," it's a no. Hiring mistakes cost 6–12 months and $200K+.

## Crisis Management

### Crisis Response Framework

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Acknowledge the crisis (don't ignore or minimize) | Immediately |
| 2 | Gather facts (what happened, impact, scope) | First 2 hours |
| 3 | Assemble response team | First 2 hours |
| 4 | Communicate internally (team needs to hear from you first) | Within 4 hours |
| 5 | Communicate externally (customers, investors, public) | Within 24 hours |
| 6 | Execute response plan | Ongoing |
| 7 | Post-crisis review and process improvement | Within 1 week |

### Common Startup Crises

| Crisis | First Response | Key Principle |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| Key employee departure | Immediate 1:1, understand why, plan transition | Don't counter-offer out of panic |
| Cash crunch (< 3 months runway) | Cut non-essential spend, accelerate fundraising | Communicate honestly with team |
| Major customer churn | CEO calls customer directly, understand root cause | Learn, don't blame |
| Product outage | Engineering all-hands, customer communication | Transparency builds trust |
| Co-founder conflict | Mediated conversation, clear roles/responsibilities | Address early, don't let fester |
| PR crisis | Acknowledge, take responsibility, state actions | Speed and honesty matter most |
