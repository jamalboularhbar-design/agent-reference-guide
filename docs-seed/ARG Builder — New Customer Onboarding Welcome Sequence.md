# ARG Builder — New Customer Onboarding Welcome Sequence

**Purpose:** A 14-day automated email sequence sent to new customers after they sign up for ARG Builder. Designed to drive activation, reduce churn risk, and establish the founder relationship during the critical first two weeks.

---

## Sequence Overview

This sequence assumes the customer has completed payment and has access to the platform. Each email has a single clear CTA to drive one specific action. The sequence is designed for the Starter and Professional tiers; Enterprise customers receive a white-glove onboarding with their dedicated CSM instead.

| Day | Email | Goal | CTA |
|-----|-------|------|-----|
| 0 | Welcome + Quick Start | First login and orientation | Log in and complete setup wizard |
| 1 | Import Your First Documents | Content migration | Upload or paste 5 documents |
| 3 | Meet Your Knowledge Graph | Feature discovery | Explore the knowledge graph |
| 5 | Invite Your Team | Expansion | Send 3 team invitations |
| 7 | Your First Week Report | Value demonstration | Review your analytics dashboard |
| 10 | AI Governance in Action | Feature depth | Run your first content health scan |
| 14 | Your 14-Day Checkpoint | Retention + feedback | Book a 15-min check-in call |

## Day 3: Meet Your Knowledge Graph

**Subject:** This is what makes ARG Builder different from a wiki

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

By now you should have some content in your workspace. Today I want to show you the feature that makes people say "I've never seen anything like this" — the interactive knowledge graph.

Go to the Knowledge Graph tab in your sidebar. You'll see a visual map of how all your documents connect to each other. Nodes are documents; edges are relationships (references, dependencies, prerequisites). The AI automatically detects these connections based on content analysis.

What to look for: isolated nodes (documents that aren't connected to anything — potential orphans), clusters (groups of related content that might need a parent overview document), and gaps (areas where you'd expect connections but don't see them).

This is how you move from "we have documentation" to "we have an operational intelligence system." The graph tells you what's missing, not just what exists.

## Automation Setup Notes

This sequence should be configured in your email automation tool (ConvertKit, Customer.io, or similar) with the following triggers and conditions:

**Trigger:** Customer completes payment (Stripe webhook `checkout.session.completed`).

**Suppression rules:** Suppress Day 1 email if customer has already imported 5+ documents. Suppress Day 5 email if customer has already invited 3+ team members. Suppress entire sequence if customer is on Enterprise tier (they get white-glove onboarding instead).

**Personalization fields:** First name, workspace name, platform URL, booking link, and dynamic stats from the platform API (documents imported, team members active, content health score).

**Reply handling:** All emails should come from jamal.boularhbar@gmail.com with replies going to the same address. During the founding customer phase, Jamal personally responds to every reply.
