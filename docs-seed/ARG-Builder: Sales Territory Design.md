# ARG-Builder: Sales Territory Design

## Executive Summary

This document defines ARG-Builder's sales territory strategy — the structured methodology for dividing the total addressable market into balanced, manageable territories that maximize coverage, minimize conflict, and ensure equitable opportunity distribution across the sales team. Well-designed territories are the foundation of predictable revenue growth.

---

## Territory Design Principles

### Core Principles

| Principle | Implementation | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Balanced opportunity** | Equal revenue potential per territory | Fair quota attainment across reps |
| **Geographic efficiency** | Minimize travel time and overlap | Maximize selling time |
| **Account density** | Sufficient accounts per territory | Ensure pipeline coverage |
| **Growth potential** | Mix of existing + greenfield | Balance short-term and long-term |
| **Relationship continuity** | Minimize account reassignment | Protect existing relationships |
| **Scalability** | Design for 2x team size | Avoid constant redesign |

## Territory Operations

### Annual Territory Planning Cycle

| Month | Activity | Owner | Output |
|-------|----------|-------|--------|
| October | Market analysis and TAM refresh | Rev Ops | Updated market data |
| November | Territory modeling and scenarios | Rev Ops + VP Sales | 3 territory models |
| December | Leadership review and selection | VP Sales + CRO | Final territory plan |
| January 1 | Territory assignment and communication | VP Sales | Rep assignments |
| January (Week 2) | Territory planning workshops | Sales managers | Rep territory plans |
| February | 30-day check-in and adjustment | Rev Ops | Minor adjustments |
| Quarterly | Territory health review | Rev Ops | Quarterly report |

### Territory Change Policy

| Scenario | Policy | Approval Required |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| Rep departure | Accounts reassigned within 5 business days | Sales Manager |
| New hire | Territory carved from existing or new accounts | VP Sales |
| Account conflict | First to engage wins; if simultaneous, manager decides | Sales Manager |
| Account upgrade (SMB → MM) | Warm handoff with 30-day transition | Both managers |
| Mid-year rebalancing | Only if variance > 30% or rep departure | VP Sales + CRO |
| Customer request | Customer can request rep change; honored if reasonable | Sales Manager |
