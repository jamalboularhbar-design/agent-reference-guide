# ARG-Builder: Series A Readiness Checklist

## Specific Milestones, Metrics Thresholds, and Preparation Steps for Raising a Series A Round

---

## 1. Executive Summary

A Series A round typically raises $8M–$25M at a $40M–$100M pre-money valuation for B2B SaaS companies. Achieving Series A readiness requires demonstrating product-market fit, repeatable go-to-market motion, strong unit economics, and a credible path to $100M+ ARR. This checklist provides the specific thresholds, milestones, and preparation steps ARG-Builder must achieve.

## 3. Revenue & Growth Metrics

### 3.1 Minimum Thresholds

| Metric | Minimum for Series A | Strong for Series A | ARG-Builder Target |
|--------|---------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| ARR | $1.5M | $2.5M–$5M | $2.5M |
| MRR growth (MoM) | 12% | 15–20% | 15% |
| ARR growth (YoY) | 3x | 4–5x | 4x |
| Months of consistent growth | 6+ | 12+ | 9+ |
| Revenue quality (recurring %) | > 85% | > 95% | > 95% |

### 3.2 Revenue Composition

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| % recurring revenue | > 95% | Predictability |
| Customer concentration (top 10%) | < 30% of ARR | Risk diversification |
| Revenue by tier (Enterprise %) | > 40% | Validates upmarket motion |
| Expansion revenue (% of new) | > 30% | NRR potential |
| Pipeline coverage (next 2 quarters) | > 3x | Growth visibility |

### 3.3 Growth Quality Indicators

| Indicator | Red Flag | Green Flag |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Growth source | 100% founder-led | Repeatable, non-founder sales |
| Customer acquisition | Sporadic, unpredictable | Consistent monthly additions |
| Deal size trend | Declining | Stable or increasing |
| Sales cycle | Lengthening | Stable or shortening |
| Win rate | Declining | Stable or improving |

## 5. Unit Economics

### 5.1 Required Metrics

| Metric | Minimum | Strong | ARG-Builder Target |
|--------|---------|--------|--------------------|
| LTV/CAC ratio | > 3x | > 5x | > 5x |
| CAC payback (months) | < 18 | < 12 | < 12 |
| Gross margin | > 70% | > 80% | > 80% |
| Burn multiple | < 2.5x | < 1.5x | < 1.5x |
| Magic number | > 0.5 | > 0.75 | > 0.75 |

### 5.2 Financial Health

| Metric | Requirement | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Monthly burn rate | Clearly understood | Investors need to model runway |
| Runway remaining | > 6 months | Don't fundraise from desperation |
| Path to profitability | Credible model | Show when and how |
| Revenue per employee | > $150K ARR/FTE | Efficiency signal |
| Gross margin trend | Stable or improving | Not deteriorating |

### 5.3 Financial Model Requirements

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Historical financials | Monthly P&L since inception |
| 3-year projection | Monthly for Year 1, quarterly for Years 2–3 |
| Key assumptions | Clearly stated and defensible |
| Scenario analysis | Base, bull, bear cases |
| Use of funds | Detailed allocation of raise |
| Headcount plan | By function, by quarter |
| Unit economics evolution | How metrics improve with scale |

## 11. Common Series A Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|
| Fundraising too early (metrics not ready) | Use scorecard — don't start until score > 3.5 |
| Running out of runway during process | Start with 9+ months runway |
| Metrics declining during fundraise | Maintain operational focus, don't go "all-in" on fundraise |
| Too many meetings, not enough depth | Focus on 10–15 firms, go deep |
| No clear lead investor | Identify and prioritize lead candidates |
| Founder burnout | Share fundraising load, maintain health |
| Accepting bad terms out of desperation | Know your walk-away points in advance |
| Neglecting existing customers | Keep CS team fully staffed during raise |
