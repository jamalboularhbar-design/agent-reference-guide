# ARG-Builder: Founder's Fundraising Toolkit

## Executive Summary

This document is the complete fundraising toolkit for ARG-Builder — everything a founder needs to raise capital efficiently, from preparation through close. This covers seed through Series B, with specific guidance on timing, targeting, materials, process management, and negotiation.

---

## Fundraising Readiness Assessment

### Pre-Fundraise Checklist

| Category | Requirement | Status Criteria |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| **Metrics** | Clear growth trajectory | 3+ months of consistent MoM growth |
| **Story** | Compelling narrative | Can explain in 30 seconds why this is a $1B+ opportunity |
| **Team** | Credible founding team | Relevant experience, complementary skills |
| **Product** | Working product with users | Live customers using and paying |
| **Market** | Large addressable market | TAM > $10B with clear wedge |
| **Differentiation** | Clear competitive advantage | Defensible moat (technology, data, network) |
| **Financials** | Clean books | Monthly close, clear unit economics |
| **Legal** | Corporate housekeeping | Delaware C-Corp, clean cap table, IP assigned |
| **Data room** | Organized materials | All documents ready and accessible |

## Fundraising Timeline

### Typical Process (12–16 Weeks)

| Week | Phase | Activities | Deliverables |
|------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 1–2 | Preparation | Finalize materials, build target list, warm intros | Deck, data room, CRM |
| 3–4 | Soft launch | 5–10 meetings with "practice" investors | Refined pitch, feedback |
| 5–8 | Active fundraise | 30–50 meetings, partner meetings | Term sheets (target 2–3) |
| 9–10 | Negotiation | Compare terms, negotiate, select lead | Signed term sheet |
| 11–14 | Due diligence | Legal, financial, technical DD | DD complete |
| 15–16 | Close | Final docs, wire transfer | Money in bank |

## Pitch Materials

### Pitch Deck Structure (12 Slides)

| Slide | Content | Time | Key Message |
|-------|---------|------|-------------|
| 1. Title | Company name, tagline, your name | 15 sec | Memorable first impression |
| 2. Problem | Pain point with data | 60 sec | This is urgent and expensive |
| 3. Solution | What you built, demo screenshot | 90 sec | Elegant solution to real problem |
| 4. Market | TAM/SAM/SOM with methodology | 60 sec | Massive opportunity |
| 5. Traction | Growth metrics, logos, testimonials | 90 sec | This is working |
| 6. Business model | Revenue model, pricing, unit economics | 60 sec | Clear path to profitability |
| 7. Competition | Positioning map, differentiation | 60 sec | We win because... |
| 8. Go-to-market | Channels, CAC, sales motion | 60 sec | Repeatable growth engine |
| 9. Team | Founders + key hires, relevant experience | 60 sec | We're the team to do this |
| 10. Financials | Revenue projections, key assumptions | 60 sec | Credible path to scale |
| 11. Ask | Amount, use of funds, milestones | 45 sec | Clear plan for the capital |
| 12. Close | Contact, next steps, memorable closer | 30 sec | Let's do this together |

## Term Sheet Negotiation

### Key Terms to Negotiate

| Term | Founder-Friendly | Standard | Investor-Friendly | Priority |
|------|-----------------|----------|-------------------|----------|
| Valuation | Higher pre-money | Market rate | Lower pre-money | Critical |
| Board composition | Founder majority | 2-1-1 (2 founders, 1 investor, 1 independent) | Investor majority | Critical |
| Liquidation preference | 1x non-participating | 1x participating (capped) | 2x+ participating | High |
| Anti-dilution | Broad-based weighted average | Narrow-based weighted average | Full ratchet | High |
| Vesting | 4-year, 1-year cliff (standard) | Same | Longer vesting, double trigger | Medium |
| Pro-rata rights | Limited to lead | All investors pro-rata | Super pro-rata | Medium |
| Information rights | Quarterly updates | Monthly financials | Weekly metrics | Low |
| Protective provisions | Minimal (standard) | Standard set | Extensive veto rights | High |
| Drag-along | High threshold (80%+) | 60–70% threshold | Majority threshold | Medium |
| No-shop period | 30 days | 45 days | 60+ days | Low |

### Negotiation Tactics

| Situation | Tactic | Execution |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Multiple term sheets | Create urgency | "We have other offers and need to decide by [date]" |
| Single term sheet | Create perceived competition | "We're in advanced conversations with other firms" |
| Valuation too low | Anchor high, concede slowly | Start 30% above target, negotiate down |
| Bad terms | Trade terms for valuation | "We'll accept lower valuation for cleaner terms" |
| Investor wants board seat | Offer observer seat first | "Let's start with observer and revisit at Series A" |
| Excessive protective provisions | Push back on operational ones | "We need operational flexibility to move fast" |

## Post-Close Best Practices

| Communication | Frequency | Content | Format |
|--------------|-----------|---------|--------|
| Monthly update | Monthly | Metrics, wins, asks, challenges | Email (1 page) |
| Board meeting | Quarterly | Full business review | In-person/video (2 hours) |
| Annual letter | Annually | Year in review, strategy ahead | Written (3–5 pages) |
| Ad-hoc updates | As needed | Major wins, pivots, challenges | Email or call |

### Monthly Investor Update Template

| Section | Content | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| TL;DR | 3 bullet summary (good, bad, ask) | 3 lines |
| Key metrics | MRR, growth, customers, runway | Table |
| Highlights | Top 3 wins this month | 3 bullets |
| Challenges | Top 2 challenges and how you're addressing | 2 bullets |
| Asks | Specific help needed (intros, advice, hiring) | 2–3 bullets |
| Team | New hires, departures, open roles | Brief |
| Looking ahead | Next month's priorities | 3 bullets |

## Fundraising Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Harmful | Prevention |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|
| Raising too early | Weak metrics = bad terms | Wait for clear traction signals |
| Raising too little | Run out before next milestone | Raise 18–24 months of runway |
| Too many meetings | Founder distraction, signal fatigue | Batch meetings, limit to 3/day |
| No BATNA | Accept bad terms out of desperation | Always have Plan B (revenue, bridge) |
| Sharing too much too early | Lose negotiating leverage | Stage information release |
| Ignoring small investors | Miss strategic angels | Include 10–15% for angels |
| Not checking references | Bad board member | Talk to 3+ founders they've backed |
| Verbal commitments | Not binding, waste time | "Excited" ≠ committed until signed |
| Neglecting existing investors | Lose pro-rata, references | Keep current investors informed |
| Post-close radio silence | Lose trust, no help when needed | Monthly updates without fail |
