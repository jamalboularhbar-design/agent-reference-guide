# ARG-Builder: Investor Relations & Fundraising Playbook

## Executive Summary

This playbook defines ARG-Builder's approach to investor relations and Series A fundraising — from building relationships with target investors 12–18 months before raising, through the fundraising process itself, to post-close investor management. The target is a $10M–$15M Series A at a $50M–$75M pre-money valuation, raised within 8–12 weeks of going to market, with 3–5 term sheets generating competitive tension.

---

## Fundraising Timeline

### Pre-Raise Phase (12–18 months before)

| Month | Activity | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| -18 | Identify 50 target investors (Tier 1, 2, 3) | Build target list |
| -15 | Begin sharing monthly updates with 10 warm investors | Build relationships |
| -12 | Attend 2–3 conferences where target investors speak | Face-to-face connection |
| -9 | Request introductions to 5 new investors per month | Expand network |
| -6 | Share quarterly metrics showing trajectory | Demonstrate momentum |
| -3 | Signal intent to raise ("planning next round in Q[X]") | Create anticipation |

### Active Raise Phase (8–12 weeks)

| Week | Activity | Target |
|------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Launch: send deck to 15–20 investors simultaneously | Create urgency |
| 2 | First meetings (30-min intros) | 12–15 first meetings |
| 3 | Partner meetings (deep-dives) | 8–10 partner meetings |
| 4 | Customer references and due diligence | Support 5–6 active processes |
| 5 | Term sheet deadline communicated | "Deciding by end of Week 6" |
| 6 | Term sheets received | Target: 3–5 term sheets |
| 7 | Negotiate and select lead | Choose best partner |
| 8 | Legal documentation and close | Wire funds |

## Pitch Narrative Framework

### The Story Arc

The pitch follows a narrative structure that builds emotional and logical conviction:

| Act | Content | Emotion |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 1. The World Has Changed | Market shift creating the opportunity | Excitement about timing |
| 2. The Problem is Massive | Quantified pain ($2.8M/year) | Urgency |
| 3. Current Solutions Fail | Why consulting and tools don't work | Frustration (shared) |
| 4. We Found the Answer | ARG-Builder's unique approach | Hope and clarity |
| 5. It's Working | Traction, metrics, customer love | Confidence |
| 6. The Market is Enormous | $47B TAM, growing | Greed (opportunity) |
| 7. We're the Team | Why we'll win | Trust |
| 8. Here's the Plan | Use of funds, milestones | Alignment |
| 9. Join Us | The ask, the vision | Partnership |

### Key Proof Points

| Claim | Evidence |
|-------|----------|

## Term Sheet Negotiation

### Key Terms to Negotiate

| Term | Founder-Friendly | Investor-Friendly | Target |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|--------|
| Valuation | Higher pre-money | Lower pre-money | $50M–$75M pre |
| Board composition | 2 founders + 1 investor + 1 independent | Investor majority | 2-1-1 or 2-1-2 |
| Liquidation preference | 1x non-participating | > 1x participating | 1x non-participating |
| Anti-dilution | Broad-based weighted average | Full ratchet | Broad-based weighted average |
| Vesting | 4-year with 1-year cliff (credit for time served) | Fresh 4-year | Credit for time served |
| Protective provisions | Minimal | Extensive | Standard (reasonable) |
| Pro-rata rights | Standard | Super pro-rata | Standard |
| Information rights | Quarterly updates | Monthly detailed | Quarterly + monthly summary |

## Post-Close Investor Management

### Monthly Investor Update Template

Send on the 5th of each month. Keep concise (< 500 words). Include:

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Highlights | Top 3 wins (lead with strongest) |
| Key Metrics | MRR, customers, pipeline, burn, runway |
| Challenges | 1–2 honest challenges + what you're doing |
| Asks | 2 specific, actionable requests |
| Next Month | 3 priorities with measurable targets |

### Board Meeting Cadence

| Frequency | Format | Duration | Content |
|-----------|--------|----------|---------|
| Monthly | Email update | — | Metrics + highlights + asks |
| Quarterly | Board meeting | 2 hours | Full board deck (12 slides) |
| Annual | Strategy session | Half-day | Long-term planning, market review |
| Ad-hoc | Phone/video | 30 min | Major decisions, urgent issues |
