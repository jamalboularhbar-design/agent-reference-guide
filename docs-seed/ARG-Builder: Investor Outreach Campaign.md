## Investor Meeting Preparation

### Before Every Meeting

1. Research the partner's recent investments and public statements (Twitter/X, podcasts, blog posts).
2. Identify 2–3 specific portfolio companies where ARG-Builder could add value or where synergies exist.
3. Prepare 3 tailored questions that demonstrate you've done homework on their thesis.
4. Have the live product demo ready (your reference guide website) as proof of concept.
5. Prepare answers for the 10 most common objections (see Sales Collateral document).

### Meeting Flow (30 minutes)

| Segment | Duration | Focus |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Rapport & context | 3 min | Personal connection, why this firm |
| Problem statement | 4 min | $2.8M problem, market pain |
| Live demo | 8 min | Show the product working |
| Business model & metrics | 7 min | Unit economics, financials |
| Team & vision | 4 min | Why us, where we're going |
| Ask & next steps | 4 min | Clear ask, timeline, process |

### Post-Meeting Follow-Up

Send a thank-you email within 2 hours of the meeting. Include: (1) one specific thing discussed that excited you, (2) any materials they requested, (3) clear next steps with proposed timeline. Follow up with the monthly investor update regardless of outcome to maintain the relationship.

## Monthly Investor Update Template

**Subject:** ARG-Builder — [Month] Update: [Key Headline Metric]

**Structure:**

- **Highlights** (3 bullets — wins only)
- **Key Metrics** (MRR, customers, pipeline, NPS)
- **Challenges** (1–2 honest challenges you're working through)
- **Asks** (specific introductions, advice, or resources needed)
- **What's Next** (upcoming milestones for next 30 days)

Send this to all investors in your pipeline (even those who passed) every month. This builds trust, demonstrates momentum, and keeps you top-of-mind for future rounds or introductions.
