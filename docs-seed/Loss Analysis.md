# ARG-Builder: Competitive Win/Loss Analysis

## Executive Summary

This document defines ARG-Builder's win/loss analysis program — the systematic methodology for understanding why deals are won or lost, extracting actionable insights, and feeding those insights back into product, marketing, sales, and strategy. A rigorous win/loss program is the single most valuable competitive intelligence tool for a B2B SaaS company.

---

## Program Structure

### Analysis Framework

| Dimension | Questions Answered | Data Source |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Decision criteria** | What factors mattered most to the buyer? | Interviews, CRM |
| **Competitive dynamics** | Who did we compete against? How did we compare? | Interviews, sales notes |
| **Sales execution** | Did our sales process help or hinder? | Interviews, CRM |
| **Product fit** | Did our product meet their requirements? | Interviews, demo feedback |
| **Pricing/value** | Was our pricing competitive? Did they see value? | Interviews, proposals |
| **Timing** | Was the timing right? What triggered the evaluation? | Interviews, CRM |
| **Relationships** | Did relationships influence the decision? | Interviews, sales notes |

## Data Collection

### Interview Methodology

| Element | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| Timing | 2–4 weeks after decision (win or loss) |
| Interviewer | Neutral party (not the AE who ran the deal) — Product Marketing or external |
| Duration | 20–30 minutes |
| Format | Semi-structured phone/video interview |
| Incentive | $50 gift card or donation to charity of choice |
| Recording | With permission, transcribed for analysis |
| Sample size | 100% of losses > $50K ACV, 50% of wins > $50K ACV |

### Interview Guide (Loss)

| Section | Questions | Duration |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Context | "What triggered your search for a solution like ours?" | 3 min |
| Evaluation | "Who else did you evaluate? What criteria mattered most?" | 5 min |
| Experience | "How was your experience with our team during the evaluation?" | 5 min |
| Decision | "What ultimately drove your decision? What was the deciding factor?" | 5 min |
| Product | "Were there specific capabilities we lacked that the winner had?" | 5 min |
| Pricing | "How did our pricing compare? Was value clear?" | 3 min |
| Improvement | "What could we have done differently to win your business?" | 4 min |

### Interview Guide (Win)

| Section | Questions | Duration |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Context | "What triggered your search? What was the business problem?" | 3 min |
| Evaluation | "Who else did you consider? How many vendors?" | 3 min |
| Decision | "What was the primary reason you chose us over alternatives?" | 5 min |
| Differentiators | "What stood out about our product/team/approach?" | 5 min |
| Concerns | "Were there any concerns or hesitations during the process?" | 3 min |
| Experience | "How would you rate your buying experience? What could improve?" | 3 min |
| Advice | "What advice would you give our team for future prospects like you?" | 3 min |

## Analysis & Reporting

### Win/Loss Metrics Dashboard

| Metric | Definition | Target | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Overall win rate | Wins / (Wins + Losses) | > 30% | 25–35% (B2B SaaS) |
| Win rate vs. specific competitors | Wins against X / Total deals vs. X | > 50% each | Varies |
| Win rate by segment | Win rate per ICP segment | > 35% (target segment) | Varies |
| Win rate by deal size | Win rate per ACV band | Consistent across bands | Varies |
| Win rate by source | Win rate per lead source | Identify best sources | Varies |
| Average sales cycle (wins) | Days from opportunity to close-won | < 60 days (MM), < 120 (ENT) | Varies |
| Average sales cycle (losses) | Days from opportunity to close-lost | Shorter = earlier disqualification | Varies |
| Competitive encounter rate | % of deals with named competitor | Track trends | Varies |

### Loss Reason Taxonomy

| Category | Sub-reasons | Action Owner |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Product gap** | Missing feature, integration gap, scalability concern | Product |
| **Pricing** | Too expensive, unclear value, competitor cheaper | Product Marketing + Finance |
| **Sales execution** | Slow response, poor demo, wrong champion, missed timeline | Sales Leadership |
| **Timing** | Not ready, budget cycle, internal change | Sales (qualification) |
| **Competitor strength** | Better product fit, stronger brand, existing relationship | Product + Marketing |
| **No decision** | Lost to status quo, project deprioritized | Sales (qualification) |
| **Internal politics** | Champion left, reorganization, competing priorities | Sales (multi-threading) |

### Win Reason Taxonomy

| Category | Sub-reasons | Leverage Point |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Product superiority** | Better UX, more features, AI capabilities | Product Marketing messaging |
| **Value/ROI** | Clear ROI, faster time-to-value, lower TCO | Sales enablement |
| **Sales experience** | Responsive, consultative, trusted advisor | Sales training |
| **Brand/reputation** | Market leader perception, references, reviews | Marketing |
| **Implementation** | Faster deployment, better support, lower risk | CS + Sales |
| **Pricing** | Better value, flexible terms, transparent | Pricing strategy |
| **Relationships** | Executive alignment, champion advocacy | Sales methodology |

## Competitive Intelligence Output

### Competitor Profile Template

| Section | Content | Update Frequency |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Overview | Company, funding, positioning, target market | Quarterly |
| Product | Key features, strengths, weaknesses, roadmap | Monthly |
| Pricing | Published pricing, known discounting, packaging | Quarterly |
| Sales motion | Sales process, typical deal size, sales cycle | Quarterly |
| Win rate against | Our win rate, trending direction | Monthly |
| Key differentiators | Where they beat us, where we beat them | Monthly |
| Messaging | Their positioning, claims, proof points | Quarterly |
| Customer base | Notable customers, segments, geography | Quarterly |

### Battle Card Template

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Quick summary | 3-sentence competitor overview |
| Their pitch | What they say about themselves |
| Our pitch against them | How to position against them |
| Where they win | Honest assessment of their strengths |
| Where we win | Our key differentiators |
| Landmines to set | Questions that expose their weaknesses |
| Objection handling | Common objections when competing, with responses |
| Proof points | Customer quotes, data, case studies that counter them |
| Pricing comparison | How pricing compares (if known) |
| Trap questions they set | Questions designed to make us look bad, with responses |

## Program Governance

### Reporting Cadence

| Report | Audience | Frequency | Content |
|--------|----------|-----------|---------|
| Weekly win/loss summary | Sales leadership | Weekly | New interviews, quick insights |
| Monthly competitive brief | Sales + Marketing + Product | Monthly | Trends, battle card updates |
| Quarterly deep-dive | Executive team + Board | Quarterly | Full analysis, strategic recommendations |
| Annual competitive review | Full company | Annually | Market landscape, positioning strategy |

### Action Loop

| Insight Type | Action | Owner | SLA |
|-------------|--------|-------|-----|
| Product gap (recurring) | Add to roadmap consideration | Product | 2 weeks |
| Sales execution issue | Training or process update | Sales Enablement | 1 week |
| Pricing concern | Pricing review and adjustment | Product Marketing + Finance | 1 month |
| Messaging gap | Update positioning and collateral | Product Marketing | 2 weeks |
| Competitive threat | Battle card update + sales alert | Product Marketing | 1 week |
