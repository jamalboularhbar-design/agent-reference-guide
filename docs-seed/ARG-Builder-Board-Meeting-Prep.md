# ARG-Builder: Board Meeting Preparation & Governance

## Complete Framework for Effective Board Management from Seed Through Series B

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Board meetings are the highest-leverage hour in a CEO's month. Well-run boards provide strategic guidance, accountability, and access to networks. Poorly run boards waste time and create friction. This document provides the complete framework for ARG-Builder's board governance — from meeting preparation to follow-up, including templates, cadence, and best practices.

## 2. Board Composition Strategy

## 5. Pre-Meeting Preparation

### 5.1 Preparation Timeline

| Timing | Activity | Owner |
|--------|----------|-------|
| -3 weeks | Confirm date, time, location | EA/CEO |
| -2 weeks | Collect inputs from leadership team | CEO |
| -10 days | First draft of board deck | CEO |
| -7 days | Review with co-founder/CFO | CEO + CTO |
| -5 days | Distribute board deck to members | CEO |
| -3 days | Pre-read reminder | EA |
| -1 day | Final logistics confirmation | EA |
| Day of | 30-min prep with leadership team | CEO |

### 5.2 Board Member Pre-Read Package

| Document | Length | Purpose |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Board deck | 15–20 slides | Main presentation |
| Financial statements | 2–3 pages | Detailed financials |
| Metrics appendix | 3–5 pages | Deep data for reference |
| Strategic topic brief | 2–3 pages | Context for deep dive |
| Previous meeting minutes | 1–2 pages | Continuity |
| Action items status | 1 page | Accountability |

### 5.3 CEO Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Board deck complete and distributed (5+ days before)
- [ ] Pre-read materials sent
- [ ] Strategic topic prepared with clear framing questions
- [ ] Asks clearly defined (specific, actionable)
- [ ] Potential tough questions anticipated with answers prepared
- [ ] Bad news identified and framed constructively
- [ ] Action items from last meeting addressed
- [ ] 1:1 pre-calls with key board members (if sensitive topics)
- [ ] Logistics confirmed (room, video, food)
- [ ] Leadership team briefed on their sections

## 7. Board Dynamics Management

### 7.1 Common Board Challenges

| Challenge | Prevention | Response |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Board member dominates discussion | Set clear agenda with time limits | "Great point. Let's hear from others." |
| Operational micromanagement | Keep updates strategic, not tactical | "We've got this handled. Here's our approach." |
| Conflicting advice from members | Acknowledge, then decide | "I've heard both perspectives. Here's my decision." |
| Lack of preparation | Send materials early, highlight key sections | Pre-call with unprepared members |
| Avoiding hard conversations | CEO must raise tough topics proactively | "I want to discuss something difficult." |
| Investor pushing their portfolio company | Evaluate objectively, disclose conflicts | "Let's evaluate on merit." |

### 7.2 Managing Board Member Relationships

| Activity | Frequency | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| 1:1 call with each member | Monthly (15–30 min) | Relationship, early input |
| Informal coffee/dinner | Quarterly | Deeper relationship |
| Quick text/email updates | As needed | Keep informed, no surprises |
| Ask for specific help | Monthly | Activate their value |
| Share wins (customer, press) | As they happen | Keep them excited |
| Seek advice before decisions | Major decisions | Respect their role |

### 7.3 Closed Session Best Practices

**What happens in closed session (board without management):**
- Board members discuss CEO/management performance
- Sensitive compensation or personnel issues
- Concerns they don't want to raise in front of team
- Alignment on feedback to give CEO

**CEO's approach:**
- Don't be defensive about closed sessions — they're healthy
- Ask for feedback after: "Anything I should know?"
- Act on feedback promptly and visibly
- If no feedback given, that's usually a good sign

### 10.2 Continuous Improvement

| Signal | Issue | Action |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Low attendance | Disengagement | 1:1 conversation, consider replacement |
| No questions asked | Lack of preparation | Improve pre-reads, add discussion prompts |
| Meetings run over | Poor time management | Stricter agenda, timekeeper |
| Same topics repeated | No progress on issues | Better action item tracking |
