# Riad & Routes — Guest Check-In Protocol

## 1. Overview

This document defines the standard operating procedure for guest check-in at all Riad & Routes properties across Morocco. The check-in experience is the first physical touchpoint with our brand and must convey warmth, efficiency, and the promise of an exceptional stay. Every team member involved in check-in must execute this protocol flawlessly, regardless of arrival time or guest tier.

## 2. Pre-Arrival Preparation (T-24 Hours)

The check-in process begins well before the guest arrives at the property. The Front Desk Manager must complete the following preparation checklist no later than 24 hours before the expected arrival.

| Task | Responsible | Deadline | Verification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Confirm room assignment matches guest preferences (floor, view, bed config) | Front Desk Manager | T-24h | Cross-check with CRM notes |
| Verify room is cleaned, inspected, and staged with welcome amenities | Housekeeping Lead | T-6h | Physical walkthrough sign-off |
| Prepare welcome folder (property map, WiFi card, restaurant guide, emergency contacts) | Guest Relations | T-24h | Folder assembled and placed in room |
| Confirm airport transfer details with driver (flight number, terminal, name sign) | Transport Coordinator | T-24h | Driver confirmation SMS received |
| Check dietary restrictions and communicate to kitchen for welcome refreshments | F&B Manager | T-12h | Kitchen acknowledgment |
| Pre-set room temperature and lighting to "arrival mode" | Maintenance | T-2h | Thermostat at 22°C, ambient lighting on |
| Load guest profile on front desk tablet (name, photo if available, preferences, VIP tier) | Front Desk Agent | T-4h | Profile visible on check-in screen |

## 3. Arrival Sequence

### 3.1 Gate Greeting (0-2 minutes)

When the guest arrives at the riad entrance, the designated greeter (typically the doorman or a Guest Relations team member) must:

1. Open the vehicle door and greet the guest by name: "Marhaba, Mr./Mrs. [Last Name]. Welcome to Riad [Property Name]."
2. Offer immediate assistance with luggage — never allow a guest to carry their own bags past the threshold.
3. Guide the guest through the entrance courtyard, pointing out the central fountain and garden as a brief orientation moment.
4. Offer a cold towel (summer) or warm towel (winter) and a glass of fresh mint tea or orange blossom water.

### 3.2 Registration (2-8 minutes)

The Front Desk Agent handles registration while the guest is seated comfortably in the reception salon:

1. Present the registration card pre-filled with information from the booking (name, nationality, passport number if provided during booking). Guest only needs to verify and sign.
2. Collect passport or ID for the mandatory police registration (Fiche de Police). Photograph the document immediately and return it within 60 seconds — never hold a guest's passport longer than necessary.
3. Confirm the length of stay, any pre-booked experiences, and dietary requirements.
4. Present the room key (physical brass key for traditional riads, or key card for modern properties) in a branded leather holder.
5. Briefly explain meal times, spa availability, and the 24/7 WhatsApp concierge number.

### 3.3 Room Escort (8-15 minutes)

A dedicated team member escorts the guest to their room:

1. Walk at the guest's pace through the property, narrating key features (rooftop terrace, hammam, library).
2. Upon entering the room, demonstrate: AC/heating controls, safe operation, minibar contents, and the call button for service.
3. Confirm luggage has arrived and is placed on the luggage rack (never on the bed).
4. Ask if there is anything else needed and inform the guest that their dedicated contact for the stay is [Name] reachable at [WhatsApp number].
5. Depart with a warm "We hope you enjoy your stay. Please don't hesitate to ask for anything."

## 4. Late Arrival Protocol (After 22:00)

For guests arriving after 22:00, the following modifications apply:

- Night security opens the main door (the doorman is off-shift after 21:00).
- A cold welcome plate (fruit, pastries, water) is pre-set in the room since the kitchen closes at 22:30.
- Registration is abbreviated: collect passport photo only, defer full registration to the following morning.
- Room escort is shortened — demonstrate only essential controls (lights, AC, safe).
- Noise levels must be minimal; avoid conversations in corridors near occupied rooms.

## 5. VIP & Repeat Guest Enhancements

| Guest Tier | Additional Check-In Elements |
| :--- | :--- |
| First-time guest | Standard protocol as above |
| Returning guest (2nd+ stay) | Welcome-back card signed by GM, preferred room pre-assigned, minibar stocked with noted preferences |
| VIP / High-value client | GM personally greets at gate, upgraded room with rose petal arrangement, complimentary hammam session pre-booked, handwritten note from owner |
| Celebrity / UHNW | Private entrance used, no other guests in reception area, security sweep completed T-1h, NDA-trained staff only |

## 6. Problem Resolution During Check-In

If any issue arises during check-in (room not ready, booking discrepancy, guest complaint), the following escalation applies:

1. **Level 1 (Front Desk Agent):** Apologize sincerely, offer immediate comfort (upgraded refreshments, lounge access). Attempt resolution within 5 minutes.
2. **Level 2 (Front Desk Manager):** If unresolved in 5 minutes, the manager takes over. Authority to offer room upgrade, complimentary dinner, or spa credit up to 500 MAD.
3. **Level 3 (General Manager):** For serious issues (double-booking, safety concern, VIP dissatisfaction), the GM is contacted immediately regardless of time. Authority to offer full complimentary night or alternative property transfer.

## 7. Post-Check-In Tasks

Within 30 minutes of the guest settling into their room:

- Front Desk Agent updates the PMS (Property Management System) with actual arrival time, room number, and any special notes.
- Guest Relations sends a WhatsApp welcome message with the digital property guide link.
- Kitchen is notified of the guest's presence for breakfast planning.
- Housekeeping is informed of the occupied room for turndown service scheduling.
