# ARG-Builder: Customer Journey Mapping

## Executive Summary

This document defines ARG-Builder's end-to-end customer journey — the complete map of touchpoints, emotions, actions, and moments of truth from first awareness through long-term advocacy. Understanding the journey enables the organization to design experiences that reduce friction, amplify delight, and create loyal customers who drive organic growth.

---

## Journey Overview

### Journey Stages

| Stage | Duration | Customer Goal | Our Goal | Primary Owner |
|-------|----------|--------------|----------|---------------|
| 1. Awareness | Variable | "I have a problem" | Be discovered | Marketing |
| 2. Consideration | 2–4 weeks | "What solutions exist?" | Educate and differentiate | Marketing + Sales |
| 3. Evaluation | 2–6 weeks | "Is this the right solution?" | Prove value | Sales + Product |
| 4. Purchase | 1–4 weeks | "Let me buy this" | Remove friction | Sales + Legal |
| 5. Onboarding | 2–4 weeks | "Help me get started" | Deliver first value | CS + Product |
| 6. Adoption | 1–3 months | "Make this part of my workflow" | Drive habit formation | CS + Product |
| 7. Expansion | 3–12 months | "I need more" | Grow the account | CS + Sales |
| 8. Advocacy | Ongoing | "I want to share this" | Amplify their voice | Marketing + CS |

## Detailed Journey Map

### Stage 1: Awareness

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Trigger events** | New hire surge, process breakdown, audit failure, key person departure, scaling challenges |
| **Channels** | Google search, LinkedIn, peer recommendations, industry events, podcasts, G2/Capterra |
| **Content consumed** | Blog posts, thought leadership, industry reports, social media |
| **Questions asked** | "How do other companies handle operational knowledge?" "Why is onboarding so slow?" |
| **Emotional state** | Frustrated, overwhelmed, searching for answers |
| **Touchpoints** | Blog, social media, SEO content, ads, PR mentions |
| **Success metric** | Website visit, content engagement, brand recall |
| **Moment of truth** | First impression of brand — does it feel credible and relevant? |

### Stage 2: Consideration

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Actions** | Research solutions, read reviews, compare options, download guides, attend webinars |
| **Channels** | Website, G2, webinars, email nurture, case studies |
| **Content consumed** | Comparison guides, case studies, ROI calculators, demo videos, whitepapers |
| **Questions asked** | "How is this different from Confluence/Notion?" "What ROI can I expect?" "Who else uses this?" |
| **Emotional state** | Curious, cautious, comparing, building internal case |
| **Touchpoints** | Website pages, gated content, retargeting ads, email sequences |
| **Success metric** | MQL conversion, content downloads, demo requests |
| **Moment of truth** | Case study relevance — "This company is just like mine" |

### Stage 3: Evaluation

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Actions** | Request demo, start trial, involve stakeholders, evaluate against requirements |
| **Channels** | Demo calls, trial environment, sales emails, technical documentation |
| **Content consumed** | Product demos, trial experience, security documentation, implementation guides |
| **Questions asked** | "Can it handle our specific use case?" "How long to implement?" "What about security?" |

| **Touchpoints** | Demo, trial, sales calls, technical review, reference calls |
| **Success metric** | Trial activation, stakeholder engagement, proposal request |
| **Moment of truth** | "Aha moment" during demo — seeing their specific problem solved |

### Stage 4: Purchase

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|

| **Channels** | Email, phone, contract portal, procurement system |

## Friction Points & Interventions

### Critical Friction Points

| Stage | Friction Point | Impact | Intervention |
|-------|---------------|--------|-------------|
| Awareness | Can't find us in search | Lost prospects | SEO investment, content strategy |
| Consideration | Unclear differentiation | Confusion, drop-off | Comparison pages, positioning |
| Evaluation | Complex trial setup | Abandonment | Guided setup, templates |
| Purchase | Slow procurement | Deal stalls | Streamlined contracts, self-serve |
| Onboarding | Overwhelmed by options | Low activation | Progressive disclosure, guided paths |
| Adoption | Team resistance | Low usage | Change management support, training |
| Expansion | Budget constraints | Flat revenue | ROI documentation, executive alignment |
| Advocacy | No easy mechanism | Missed referrals | Referral program, review prompts |
