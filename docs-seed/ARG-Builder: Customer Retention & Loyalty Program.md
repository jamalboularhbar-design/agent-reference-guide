# ARG-Builder: Customer Retention & Loyalty Program

## Executive Summary

This document defines ARG-Builder's comprehensive retention and loyalty strategy — the systematic approach to keeping customers engaged, satisfied, and expanding over time. ## Proactive Retention Tactics

### By Risk Level

| Risk Level | Health Score | Indicators | Automated Actions | Human Actions |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| **Healthy** | 80–100 | High usage, growing adoption, positive sentiment | Expansion prompts, loyalty rewards | QBR, relationship building |
| **Neutral** | 60–79 | Stable usage, no growth, no complaints | Engagement campaigns, feature tips | Check-in call, value reinforcement |
| **At-risk** | 40–59 | Declining usage, support tickets up, missed QBRs | Alert CSM, re-engagement sequence | Urgent outreach, executive escalation |
| **Critical** | 0–39 | Minimal usage, negative NPS, cancellation signals | CSM + Manager alert, save offer prep | Executive call, custom save plan |

### Retention Plays (Playbook)

| Play | Trigger | Actions | Success Metric |
|------|---------|---------|---------------|
| **Value Reinforcement** | Usage decline > 20% over 30 days | Send ROI summary, schedule training, share best practices | Usage returns to baseline |
| **Executive Alignment** | No executive engagement in 90 days | Request executive meeting, share industry insights, propose strategic review | Executive meeting scheduled |
| **Feature Re-engagement** | Low adoption of key features | Personalized demo, use case examples, implementation support | Feature adoption > 50% |
| **Expansion Seed** | High usage, approaching limits | Share growth data, propose expansion, offer pilot of next tier | Expansion conversation started |
| **Save Offer** | Cancellation request received | Understand reasons, offer alternatives (downgrade, pause, discount), escalate | Customer retained (any plan) |
| **Win-back** | Customer churned < 90 days ago | Personalized outreach, "what's new" update, special return offer | Customer reactivated |

## Churn Analysis & Prevention

### Churn Taxonomy

| Churn Type | Definition | Preventability | Strategy |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| Voluntary (value) | Customer doesn't see enough value | High | Better onboarding, value demonstration |
| Voluntary (budget) | Customer can't afford | Medium | Downgrade options, payment plans |
| Voluntary (competitor) | Customer switches to competitor | Medium | Competitive differentiation, switching cost |
| Voluntary (need change) | Customer no longer needs product | Low | Expand use cases, new features |
| Involuntary (payment) | Payment failure, card expired | High | Dunning management, payment retry |
| Involuntary (company) | Company closed, acquired, restructured | Low | Early detection, multi-threading |

### Dunning Management (Involuntary Churn Prevention)

| Day | Action | Channel | Content |
|-----|--------|---------|---------|
| Day 0 | Payment fails | Email + In-app | "Payment failed, please update" |
| Day 1 | Retry payment | Automated | Silent retry |
| Day 3 | Second notification | Email | "Action needed: update payment method" |
| Day 5 | Retry payment | Automated | Silent retry |
| Day 7 | Urgent notification | Email + SMS | "Account at risk — update now" |
| Day 10 | Retry payment | Automated | Silent retry |
| Day 12 | Final warning | Email + Phone | "Account will be suspended in 48h" |
