# ArtKech Design Studio — Client Feedback & Revision Management

## 1. Purpose

Client feedback is the most common source of project delays, scope creep, and team frustration in creative studios. This SOP defines how we solicit, receive, interpret, and act on client feedback — turning subjective opinions into actionable design direction while protecting the team's time and the project's profitability.

## 2. Feedback Collection Methods

| Method | Best For | Turnaround Expected |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| In-person review (at studio) | Brand identity, complex projects, VIP clients | Immediate feedback |
| Video call (screen share) | Remote clients, quick reviews | Same-session feedback |
| Figma comments | UI/UX projects, detailed page-level notes | 24-48 hours |
| Annotated PDF (markup) | Print layouts, brochures, multi-page documents | 24-48 hours |
| Email (written) | Simple projects, confirmations, minor tweaks | 24-48 hours |
| WhatsApp voice note | Quick reactions, tone/feeling feedback | Same day |

### 2.1 Feedback Request Template

> Subject: [Project Name] — Ready for Your Review
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> The [deliverable] is ready for your review. You can find it here: [link]
>
> **When reviewing, please consider:**
> 1. Does the overall direction feel right for your brand?
> 2. Are there specific elements you'd like us to adjust?
> 3. Is there anything missing that you expected to see?
>
> **Please share your consolidated feedback by [date] so we can stay on track for the [next milestone] deadline.**
>
> A few tips for giving feedback that helps us deliver exactly what you want:
> - Be specific: "Make the headline larger" is better than "Make it pop more"
> - Explain the why: "Our audience is older, so larger text helps readability"
> - Consolidate: Please gather all stakeholder feedback into one response
>
> Looking forward to your thoughts!

## 3. Interpreting Client Feedback

### 3.1 Translation Guide

| Client Says | They Probably Mean | Designer Should |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| "Make it pop" | Needs more contrast or visual hierarchy | Increase size contrast, add color accent, or add whitespace |
| "It feels too corporate" | Too rigid, needs warmth or personality | Soften geometry, add organic elements, warmer colors |
| "Can you make the logo bigger?" | Logo doesn't feel prominent enough in context | Increase whitespace around logo, or adjust layout hierarchy |
| "I don't like it but I can't say why" | Emotional disconnect with the direction | Schedule a call, show alternatives, use mood boards to identify preference |
| "My wife/partner doesn't like it" | Untrained eye giving feedback on professional work | Acknowledge, redirect to business objectives and target audience |
| "Can we try something completely different?" | Current direction isn't resonating | Clarify: is it the concept or the execution? Offer 1 new direction |
| "It's almost there, just a few tweaks" | Could be minor OR could be 20 changes | Ask for specific list before starting any work |
| "Make it more modern" | Feels dated compared to competitors | Research current trends in their industry, propose specific updates |

### 3.2 Feedback Quality Assessment

| Quality Level | Characteristics | Our Response |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Excellent | Specific, actionable, references brief, consolidated | Execute directly, minimal clarification needed |
| Good | Clear direction but some vague points | Brief clarification email, then execute |
| Fair | Mix of specific and vague, multiple stakeholders disagree | Schedule 15-min call to align before executing |
| Poor | Contradictory, emotional, no clear direction | Schedule meeting, re-present with rationale, guide decision |

## 4. Revision Round Management

### 4.1 What Counts as a Revision Round

| Counts as 1 Round | Does NOT Count as a Round |
| :--- | :--- |
| All feedback submitted within the agreed review window | Feedback on something we got wrong (our error) |
| Changes to approved elements (client changed their mind) | Fixing a typo we introduced |
| New ideas or additions beyond original brief | Adjusting something that doesn't match the approved brief |
| Contradicting previous feedback | Technical corrections (wrong file format, resolution) |

### 4.2 Revision Limits by Project Type

| Project Type | Included Rounds | Additional Round Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Logo design | 3 rounds | 2,000 MAD |
| Brand identity | 3 rounds | 3,500 MAD |
| Single deliverable (card, flyer, poster) | 2 rounds | 1,500 MAD |
| Multi-page (brochure, catalog) | 2 rounds | 2,500 MAD |
| Social media content (monthly) | 1 round per batch | Included in retainer |
| Website design | 2 rounds per page | 2,000 MAD per round |

### 4.3 Revision Tracking

For each project, the Account Manager maintains a revision log:

| Round | Date Submitted | Date Feedback Received | Changes Requested | Hours Spent |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| R1 | Jun 5 | Jun 7 | 8 specific changes listed | 4 hours |
| R2 | Jun 9 | Jun 11 | 3 minor adjustments | 1.5 hours |
| R3 (final) | Jun 12 | Jun 12 | "Approved! Love it." | 0 hours |

### 4.4 Exceeding Revision Limits

When a client approaches their revision limit:

**At the final included round:**
> "This is your final included revision round. We'll incorporate all your feedback from this round. If additional changes are needed after this, they'll be quoted at [rate] per round. To make the most of this round, please ensure all stakeholders have reviewed and consolidated their feedback."

**When limit is exceeded:**
> "We've completed all [X] included revision rounds for this project. We're happy to continue refining — additional rounds are available at [rate] each. Shall I proceed with a new round, or would you like to approve the current version?"

## 5. Feedback Consolidation Protocol

### 5.1 The Problem

Multiple stakeholders giving separate, contradictory feedback is the #1 cause of revision overruns. Our policy:

**One voice, one document.** The client must designate a single decision-maker who consolidates all internal feedback before sending it to us.

### 5.2 How We Enforce This

- Stated in contract: "Feedback must be submitted as a single consolidated document from the designated project contact"
- Stated in welcome email: "To keep the project on track, please gather all stakeholder input before sending feedback"
- If we receive conflicting feedback from multiple people: pause, request consolidation
- We do NOT implement contradictory changes and hope the client figures it out

### 5.3 Handling Multiple Stakeholders

| Scenario | Our Approach |
| :--- | :--- |
| 2 stakeholders disagree on direction | Ask designated contact to make the decision, offer our professional recommendation |
| CEO overrides marketing team's feedback | Accept (CEO is the decision-maker), document the override |
| Committee review (5+ people) | Request a single spokesperson, offer to join their internal meeting to present |
| Feedback arrives in 3 separate emails | Compile into one list, send back for confirmation before acting |

## 6. Feedback Response Times

### 6.1 Our Commitments

| Action | Timeline |
| :--- | :--- |
| Acknowledge receipt of feedback | Within 4 hours |
| Clarification questions (if needed) | Within 24 hours |
| Revision delivery (minor changes, < 2 hours work) | Within 48 hours |
| Revision delivery (major changes, 2-8 hours work) | Within 3-5 business days |
| Revision delivery (significant rework) | Quoted timeline provided within 24 hours |

### 6.2 Client Commitments (Stated in Contract)

| Action | Expected Timeline | Consequence of Delay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Review and provide feedback | Within 3 business days of delivery | Project timeline shifts by equivalent delay |
| Approve final deliverables | Within 5 business days | Project marked "pending client" in tracking |
| Respond to clarification questions | Within 24 hours | Work paused until response received |

### 6.3 Feedback Delay Escalation

| Days Without Response | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| Day 3 | Gentle reminder: "Just checking in — have you had a chance to review?" |
| Day 5 | Direct message: "We're holding your slot in our schedule. Could you review by [date]?" |
| Day 7 | Call from Account Manager: "Is everything okay? Can we help with the review process?" |
| Day 14 | Formal email: "Project paused. Timeline will be rescheduled upon your feedback." |
| Day 30 | Project closed. Remaining balance invoiced. Restart requires new timeline + fee. |

## 7. Difficult Feedback Situations

### 7.1 "I'll Know It When I See It" Client

- Never accept this as a brief
- Use visual exercises: show 10 examples, ask them to sort into "yes / no / maybe"
- Present 3 very different directions to triangulate their preference
- Document their reactions to narrow the field
- If still unclear after 2 presentations: honest conversation about whether we're the right fit

### 7.2 Endless Revisions (Perfectionist Client)

- Acknowledge their high standards (don't dismiss)
- Remind of revision limits early and often
- Offer a "polish round" (2 hours of micro-adjustments) as a final step
- Set a hard deadline: "Final files will be delivered on [date] based on your last approved version"

### 7.3 Design-by-Committee

- Insist on single decision-maker (contractual requirement)
- Offer to present to the committee directly (we control the narrative)
- If overruled by committee: document, execute, note in post-mortem
- Consider declining future projects with this client if pattern repeats
