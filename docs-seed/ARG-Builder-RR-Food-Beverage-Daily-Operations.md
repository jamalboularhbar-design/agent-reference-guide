# Riad & Routes — Food & Beverage Daily Operations

## 1. Purpose

This SOP covers the daily food and beverage operations across all Riad & Routes properties, from breakfast service through evening dining and in-room service. F&B is a core pillar of the guest experience — our kitchens produce authentic Moroccan cuisine elevated to luxury standards, using locally sourced ingredients and traditional techniques.

## 3. Breakfast Service Protocol

### 3.1 Setup (06:30 – 07:30)

1. Lay tables with fresh linen, cutlery, glassware, and flower arrangement
2. Prepare the buffet station: fresh-squeezed orange juice, seasonal fruit platter, Moroccan breads (msemen, baghrir, khobz), local honey, argan oil, amlou, jams, butter, olive oil
3. Set up hot station: eggs to order (scrambled, fried, boiled, omelette), pancakes, Moroccan crepes
4. Prepare beverages station: fresh mint tea, coffee (espresso machine), hot chocolate, fresh juices
5. Check dietary restriction cards for each occupied room — prepare alternatives as needed
6. Set ambient music (traditional Gnawa or Andalusian instrumental, low volume)

### 3.2 Service Execution

- Greet each guest by name as they arrive: "Good morning, Mr./Mrs. [Name]. Your usual table?"
- For returning guests, remember their preferences: "Your café au lait is on its way"
- Eggs and hot items are prepared fresh to order — never pre-cooked and held
- Refill juice and bread baskets proactively (never let them run empty)
- Clear plates within 2 minutes of guest finishing (watch for signals, don't rush)
- Present the bill only if guest is on a room-only rate; half-board guests sign the check

### 3.3 Dietary Accommodations

| Requirement | Standard Preparation | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Gluten-free | GF bread available, separate toaster, labeled items | Chef confirms cross-contamination prevention |
| Vegan | Plant milk, avocado, hummus, grilled vegetables | Clearly labeled on buffet |
| Halal | All meat is halal by default (Morocco standard) | Confirm with guest if needed |
| Kosher | Advance notice required (48h), separate preparation | Outsourced to certified provider if needed |
| Allergies (nuts, dairy, etc.) | Kitchen briefed per guest, allergen cards on table | Chef personally confirms safe items |

## 4. Market Procurement (Daily)

### 4.1 Morning Market Run (06:00 – 07:00)

The Sous Chef or designated kitchen porter visits the souk daily for:

| Item | Supplier | Quality Check |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Fresh vegetables & herbs | Preferred stall in Rahba Kedima | Seasonal, no wilting, vibrant color |
| Fresh fruit | Fruit vendor near Bab Agnaou | Ripe but firm, no bruising |
| Fresh bread | Bakery on Derb Dabachi | Warm from oven, collected at 06:30 |
| Fresh fish (if on menu) | Fish market, Marché Central | Eyes clear, gills red, no smell |
| Fresh flowers (for tables) | Flower stall, Jemaa el-Fna entrance | Roses, jasmine, or seasonal blooms |

### 4.2 Budget & Tracking

- Daily market budget: 800 – 1,200 MAD depending on occupancy
- All purchases recorded in the daily market log (item, quantity, price, supplier)
- Receipts collected and submitted to Finance every Friday
- Chef reviews market spend weekly against food cost percentage target (28-32% of F&B revenue)

## 5. Dinner Service

### 5.1 Menu Planning

- Menu changes daily based on market availability and guest preferences
- Standard format: 3-course set menu with 2 options per course (Moroccan + International)
- VIP guests may request custom menus (48h advance notice preferred)
- Seasonal specialties rotate monthly (tagine variations, couscous Friday tradition, pastilla)

### 5.2 Pre-Service Briefing (18:30)

Chef briefs the service team on:
- Tonight's menu with descriptions and allergen flags
- Wine pairing suggestions for each course
- Guest dietary requirements (pulled from PMS)
- Table assignments and any special occasions (birthday cake, anniversary setup)
- Expected covers and timing

## 6. Food Safety & Hygiene

### 6.1 Temperature Controls

| Item | Storage Temp | Max Hold Time (cooked) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Fresh meat/fish | 0-4°C | Cook within 24h of purchase |
| Dairy products | 2-5°C | Use by expiry date |
| Cooked food (hot holding) | Above 63°C | Maximum 2 hours |
| Cooked food (cold storage) | Below 5°C | Maximum 48 hours, labeled with date |
| Frozen items | -18°C or below | Defrost in fridge, never at room temp |

### 6.2 Hygiene Standards

- All kitchen staff: clean uniform daily, hair net/hat, no jewelry, nails short and clean
- Handwashing: upon entering kitchen, after handling raw meat, after break, after touching face
- Cutting boards: color-coded (red=raw meat, green=vegetables, blue=fish, white=dairy/bread)
- Deep clean: kitchen deep-cleaned every Sunday (all surfaces, behind equipment, extraction hood)
- Pest control: monthly inspection by licensed company, bait stations checked weekly

## 7. Inventory Management

### 7.1 Stock Rotation (FIFO)

All items follow First In, First Out. New deliveries are placed behind existing stock. Items are labeled with receipt date. Any item past its use-by date is discarded immediately and logged as waste.

### 7.2 Weekly Inventory Count (Every Sunday)

The Sous Chef conducts a full inventory count:
- Dry goods (spices, grains, oils, canned items)
- Refrigerated items (dairy, meat, vegetables)
- Frozen items
- Beverages (wines, spirits, soft drinks, water)
- Cleaning supplies

Results are compared against the theoretical consumption (based on covers served) to identify waste or theft.

### 7.3 Par Levels

| Category | Par Level (days of stock) | Reorder Point |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Fresh produce | 1-2 days | Daily market run |
| Dairy & eggs | 3 days | When below 2 days |
| Dry goods & spices | 14 days | When below 7 days |
| Wines & spirits | 30 days | When below 14 days |
| Cleaning supplies | 14 days | When below 7 days |
