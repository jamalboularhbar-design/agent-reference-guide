## 3. Common Due Diligence Questions

### 3.1 Business & Strategy

| Question | Preparation Notes |
|----------|------------------|
| What problem are you solving and for whom? | 30-second and 2-minute versions ready |
| Why now? What's changed in the market? | AI maturity + remote work + knowledge loss data |
| What's your unfair advantage/moat? | AI-native architecture + data flywheel + category creation |
| How big is the market opportunity? | TAM $47B, SAM $8.2B, SOM $820M (with sources) |
| What's your go-to-market strategy? | Direct sales + PLG + channel partnerships |
| Who are your competitors and how do you win? | Category positioning + win rate data |
| What are the biggest risks to the business? | Honest assessment with mitigation plans |
| What would make you fail? | Market timing, execution, funding — with contingencies |

| Question | Preparation Notes |

| How efficient is your sales motion? | CAC payback, magic number, sales efficiency |

## 4. Metrics Preparation

### 4.1 Key Metrics Dashboard

**Revenue Metrics:**
- MRR/ARR (current + 12-month trend)
- MRR growth rate (month-over-month)
- Revenue by plan tier
- Revenue concentration (top 10 customers as % of total)
- Expansion revenue vs. new revenue

**Efficiency Metrics:**
- CAC by channel (blended and segmented)
- LTV by segment
- LTV/CAC ratio
- CAC payback period (months)
- Burn multiple (net burn / net new ARR)
- Magic number (net new ARR / prior quarter S&M spend)

**Retention Metrics:**
- Gross revenue retention (GRR)
- Net revenue retention (NRR)
- Logo retention rate
- Churn by segment/reason
- Expansion rate

**Product Metrics:**
- DAU/MAU ratio
- Feature adoption rates
- Time to value (new customers)
- NPS/CSAT scores
- Support ticket volume trend

## 5. Reference Customer Preparation

### 5.1 Reference Selection Criteria

Select 5–8 customers who:
- Represent different segments/use cases
- Have measurable ROI to share
- Have a strong champion willing to speak
- Have been customers for 6+ months
- Are recognizable logos (if possible)

### 5.2 Reference Briefing Template

**Before the call, brief your reference on:**
- Who they'll be speaking with (investor name/firm)
- What topics will likely come up
- What you'd appreciate them highlighting
- What they should feel free to be honest about
- Timeline (usually 20–30 minutes)

**Suggested talking points for references:**
1. Why they chose ARG-Builder
2. What problem it solved
3. Measurable results/ROI
4. Quality of the team and support
5. What could be improved (shows authenticity)

## 6. Common Red Flags to Address Proactively

| Red Flag | Proactive Response |
|----------|-------------------|
| Customer concentration (>30% from one customer) | Diversification plan + pipeline data |
| High churn rate | Root cause analysis + improvement trend |
| Negative unit economics | Path to positive with timeline |
| Key person dependency | Succession planning + team depth |
| No IP protection | Filing timeline + trade secret strategy |
| Regulatory risk | Compliance roadmap + legal opinion |
| Competitive threat | Moat analysis + differentiation data |
| Slow growth | Market timing argument + acceleration plan |

## 7. Negotiation Preparation

### 7.1 Valuation Justification

**Methods to support valuation:**
1. Revenue multiple (comparable SaaS companies)
2. Growth rate premium (adjust for growth vs. peers)
3. Market opportunity (% of TAM captured)
4. Comparable transactions (recent funding rounds)
5. DCF analysis (for later-stage)

### 7.2 Term Sheet Key Terms

| Term | Founder-Friendly | Standard | Investor-Friendly |
|------|-----------------|----------|-------------------|
| Liquidation preference | 1x non-participating | 1x participating (capped) | 2x+ participating |
| Board composition | Founder majority | Balanced | Investor majority |
| Anti-dilution | Broad-based weighted avg | Narrow-based weighted avg | Full ratchet |
| Vesting | 4yr/1yr cliff (standard) | 4yr/1yr cliff | Acceleration on change |
| Pro-rata rights | Limited | Standard | Super pro-rata |
| Information rights | Quarterly | Monthly | Weekly |

### 7.3 Walk-Away Points

Define in advance:
- Minimum acceptable valuation
- Maximum dilution tolerance
- Non-negotiable governance terms
- Timeline requirements
- Strategic value requirements (beyond money)

## 8. Timeline Management

### 8.1 Typical Due Diligence Timeline

| Week | Activity | Your Preparation |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Initial document review | Data room complete and organized |
| 2 | Financial deep-dive | CFO/finance available for questions |
| 3 | Product/technical review | CTO available, demo environment ready |
| 4 | Customer references | References briefed and scheduled |
| 5 | Legal review | Clean legal docs, no surprises |
| 6 | Final questions + term sheet | Negotiation strategy prepared |

### 8.2 Acceleration Tactics

- Have data room ready BEFORE first meeting
- Respond to all requests within 24 hours
- Proactively share information (don't wait to be asked)
- Schedule reference calls in advance
- Have legal counsel on standby for term sheet review
- Create urgency with competing interest (if genuine)
