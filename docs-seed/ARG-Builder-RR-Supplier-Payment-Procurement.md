# Riad & Routes — Supplier Payment & Procurement Process

## 1. Purpose

This document governs how Riad & Routes selects, onboards, manages, and pays its network of suppliers. Our supplier relationships directly impact guest experience quality — from the freshness of breakfast ingredients to the reliability of tour guides. Maintaining strong, fair partnerships ensures consistent service delivery.

## 3. Supplier Onboarding

### 3.1 Selection Criteria

New suppliers are evaluated on:

1. **Quality:** Sample/trial period results (minimum 2-week trial for food suppliers)
2. **Reliability:** Delivery consistency, communication responsiveness
3. **Price:** Competitive within 10% of market rate (we don't always choose cheapest)
4. **Ethics:** No child labor, fair wages, sustainable practices where possible
5. **Proximity:** Local suppliers preferred (supports community, reduces delivery time)
6. **Backup:** Must not be our sole source for critical supplies (dual-supplier policy)

### 3.2 Onboarding Checklist

| Step | Action | Responsible | Timeline |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Identify need and shortlist 3 candidates | Department Head | Week 1 |
| 2 | Request samples/quotes from all 3 | Procurement Manager | Week 1 |
| 3 | Trial period (2 weeks for food, 1 month for services) | Department Head | Week 2-4 |
| 4 | Evaluate trial results, select preferred supplier | Department Head + GM | Week 5 |
| 5 | Negotiate terms (price, delivery schedule, payment terms) | Procurement Manager | Week 5 |
| 6 | Sign supplier agreement (template in shared drive) | GM (signatory) | Week 6 |
| 7 | Set up in accounting system with payment details | Finance Manager | Week 6 |
| 8 | First official order placed | Department Head | Week 7 |

### 3.3 Supplier Agreement Key Terms

Every supplier agreement includes:
- Agreed pricing (fixed for 6 months, reviewed bi-annually)
- Delivery schedule and lead times
- Quality standards and rejection criteria
- Payment terms (net-15 for food, net-30 for services)
- Cancellation notice period (30 days)
- Confidentiality clause (no disclosure of our pricing to competitors)
- Force majeure provisions

## 4. Purchase Order Process

### 4.1 Routine Orders (Within Budget)

1. Department Head identifies need and checks current stock levels
2. Submits purchase request via WhatsApp to Procurement Manager (item, quantity, urgency)
3. Procurement Manager verifies against monthly budget allocation
4. If within budget: order placed directly with supplier
5. Delivery received, checked against order (quantity, quality, condition)
6. Delivery note signed and filed for invoice matching

### 4.2 Non-Routine / Over-Budget Orders

1. Department Head submits written request with justification
2. Procurement Manager reviews and adds budget impact note
3. GM approves or rejects (threshold: anything over 5,000 MAD or outside budget)
4. If approved: standard order process follows
5. Budget variance is noted in monthly finance report

### 4.3 Emergency Orders

For urgent needs (equipment failure, unexpected guest requirement):
- Department Head can authorize up to 2,000 MAD without pre-approval
- Must notify Procurement Manager within 2 hours
- Receipt/invoice must be submitted same day
- Emergency orders exceeding 2,000 MAD require GM phone approval

## 5. Invoice Processing & Payment

### 5.1 Invoice Receipt & Verification

1. All invoices are received by the Finance Manager (email: finance@riadandroutes.com or physical delivery)
2. Invoice is matched against: purchase order, delivery note, and agreed pricing
3. Any discrepancy is flagged to Procurement Manager for resolution with supplier
4. Verified invoices are stamped "Approved for Payment" with date and initials

### 5.2 Payment Schedule

| Payment Method | Used For | Processing Day | Approval Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Cash (petty cash) | Market purchases, tips, small urgent items | Daily | Under 1,000 MAD: Dept Head |
| Bank transfer | Regular suppliers, services, subscriptions | Tuesday & Friday | Finance Manager + GM (>10,000 MAD) |
| Check | Rent, insurance, large one-off purchases | 1st of month | GM signature required |
| Card | Online subscriptions, emergency purchases | As needed | Finance Manager |

### 5.3 Petty Cash Management

- Each property maintains a petty cash float of 10,000 MAD
- Daily market purchases (fresh produce, bread, flowers) are paid from petty cash
- Every withdrawal requires a signed receipt or market ticket
- Petty cash is reconciled every Friday by the Finance Manager
- Float is replenished to 10,000 MAD every Monday via bank withdrawal

## 6. Supplier Performance Review

### 6.1 Monthly Scorecard

Every active supplier is scored monthly:

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring (1-5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Delivery punctuality | 25% | 5=always on time, 1=frequently late |
| Product/service quality | 30% | 5=consistently excellent, 1=frequent issues |
| Price competitiveness | 20% | 5=best value, 1=significantly overpriced |
| Communication & responsiveness | 15% | 5=instant response, 1=hard to reach |
| Flexibility & problem-solving | 10% | 5=always accommodating, 1=rigid/unhelpful |

### 6.2 Actions Based on Score

| Score Range | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| 4.5 – 5.0 | Preferred supplier status, consider volume increase |
| 3.5 – 4.4 | Satisfactory, continue relationship |
| 2.5 – 3.4 | Warning issued, improvement plan required within 30 days |
| Below 2.5 | Relationship terminated, replacement supplier activated |

### 6.3 Annual Supplier Review Meeting

Once per year (January), the GM and Procurement Manager meet with each key supplier to:
- Review the past year's performance
- Renegotiate pricing for the coming year
- Discuss new products/services available
- Address any outstanding issues
- Confirm continued partnership or initiate transition

## 7. Accounts Payable Aging Report

The Finance Manager produces a weekly aging report:

| Aging Bucket | Target | Action if Exceeded |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Current (0-15 days) | 70% of payables | Normal processing |
| 16-30 days | 25% of payables | Prioritize in next payment run |
| 31-60 days | < 5% of payables | Finance Manager contacts supplier to explain delay |
| 60+ days | 0% | GM intervention, immediate payment or dispute resolution |

**Policy:** No supplier should wait more than 45 days for payment. Maintaining prompt payment protects our reputation and ensures priority service during peak seasons.
