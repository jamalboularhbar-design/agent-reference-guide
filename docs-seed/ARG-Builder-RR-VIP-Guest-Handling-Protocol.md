# Riad & Routes — VIP Guest Handling Protocol

## 1. Purpose

This protocol defines the elevated service standards for VIP guests at Riad & Routes properties. VIP guests represent our highest-value relationships — they generate disproportionate revenue, drive referrals within their networks, and shape our reputation in the ultra-luxury segment. Every interaction must exceed their already-high expectations.

## 2. VIP Classification

| Tier | Criteria | Identifier in PMS |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Tier 1 — Gold | Repeat guest (3+ stays) OR total spend > 50,000 MAD | Gold star badge |
| Tier 2 — Platinum | 5+ stays OR total spend > 150,000 MAD OR referred 3+ bookings | Platinum star badge |
| Tier 3 — Diamond | Celebrity, UHNW ($30M+ net worth), royalty, or strategic partner | Diamond star badge |
| Tier 4 — Incognito | Any guest requesting complete privacy/anonymity | Black star badge |

Classification is determined by the Guest Relations Manager and approved by the General Manager. Tier upgrades are communicated to all departments via the daily briefing.

## 3. Pre-Arrival Intelligence Gathering

For all VIP guests, the Guest Relations Manager compiles a dossier 72 hours before arrival:

### 3.1 Information Sources

- Previous stay notes in CRM (room preferences, complaints, compliments, dietary needs)
- Social media scan (recent posts indicating interests, celebrations, travel companions)
- Booking agent notes (if booked through a travel advisor)
- Google alerts (for public figures — recent news, achievements)
- Direct pre-arrival questionnaire (sent 7 days before, optional response)

### 3.2 Dossier Contents

| Section | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| Guest profile | Full name, nationality, age range, languages spoken, photo |
| Travel companions | Names, relationships, ages (especially children) |
| Preferences | Room temperature, pillow type, minibar preferences, newspaper, wake-up habits |
| Dietary | Allergies, restrictions, favorite cuisines, wine preferences |
| Occasions | Birthdays, anniversaries, celebrations during stay |
| Sensitivities | Known dislikes, past complaints, topics to avoid |
| Interests | Hobbies, sports, cultural interests (for experience recommendations) |
| Security | Bodyguard requirements, privacy level, paparazzi risk assessment |

The dossier is shared with: GM, Front Desk Manager, Housekeeping Supervisor, Head Chef, and Spa Manager — on a need-to-know basis.

## 4. Room Preparation (VIP-Specific)

Beyond the standard pre-arrival checklist, VIP rooms receive:

| Tier | Additional Room Preparation |
| :--- | :--- |
| Gold | Welcome fruit basket (seasonal), handwritten welcome card from GM, upgraded bathroom amenities (Aesop instead of standard) |
| Platinum | All Gold items + fresh flower arrangement (roses or orchids), premium minibar (champagne, artisan chocolates), monogrammed bathrobe |
| Diamond | All Platinum items + room scented with guest's preferred fragrance, custom pillow menu pre-set, private butler assigned, artwork rotated to match known taste |
| Incognito | Minimal branding in room, no name on any visible document, staff briefed to use room number only, separate entrance prepared |

## 5. Service Delivery Standards

### 5.1 Communication Rules

- Address VIP guests by title and surname unless they explicitly request first-name basis
- Never ask a VIP guest to repeat information — all preferences should be anticipated
- Response time to any request: under 5 minutes (Gold), under 3 minutes (Platinum/Diamond)
- Never say "no" — say "Let me find a way to make that happen" and escalate if needed

### 5.2 Dining

- Head Chef personally presents the menu on the first evening (Platinum and above)
- Dietary preferences are pre-loaded — kitchen never asks "any allergies?" to a returning VIP
- Private dining can be arranged in the courtyard, rooftop, or in-room with 4 hours notice
- Wine pairing suggestions are prepared based on the guest's known preferences

### 5.3 Experiences & Tours

- VIP guests receive priority booking on all experiences (even if "sold out" for regular guests)
- Private guides are assigned (not shared with other groups)
- Vehicle upgrade: V-Class or Land Cruiser only (never Duster)
- Complimentary upgrade to premium experiences when available (e.g., hot air balloon → private balloon)

### 5.4 Departure

- GM personally bids farewell (Platinum and above)
- Departure gift: branded leather travel pouch with argan oil set (Gold), custom photo book of their stay (Platinum), bespoke gift based on interests (Diamond)
- Vehicle is pre-cooled/heated and loaded with water + snacks for airport transfer
- Follow-up thank-you email within 24 hours of departure, signed by GM

## 6. Incident Management for VIP Guests

Any issue affecting a VIP guest is automatically a Level 3 escalation:

1. The staff member who identifies the issue immediately informs the Guest Relations Manager (not the standard front desk escalation path).
2. Guest Relations Manager assesses and resolves within 15 minutes. If unable, the GM is contacted.
3. Resolution must include both an immediate fix AND a gesture of goodwill (complimentary service, upgrade, or gift).
4. A post-incident debrief is conducted within 24 hours to prevent recurrence.
5. The incident and resolution are logged in the guest's CRM profile for future reference.

**Zero-tolerance issues for VIP guests:**
- Noise disturbance from other guests or construction
- Any wait time exceeding 5 minutes for any service
- Incorrect dietary preparation
- Privacy breach (name mentioned in public areas, unauthorized room entry)
- Vehicle not ready at scheduled time

## 7. Confidentiality & Privacy

All staff sign an enhanced NDA covering VIP guest information. Specific rules:

- No photographs of VIP guests or their belongings (instant termination offense)
- No discussion of VIP guest identity or activities with other guests, friends, family, or media
- Social media posts mentioning or implying VIP guest presence are prohibited
- Guest name is never used in radio/walkie-talkie communication — use room number only
- For Tier 4 (Incognito) guests: staff are told only "a guest in Room X requires enhanced privacy" — no further details
