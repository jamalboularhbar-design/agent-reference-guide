# ARG-Builder: Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Template

## Executive Summary

This document provides ARG-Builder's standardized Quarterly Business Review template — the structured framework for conducting strategic reviews with customers, internally across departments, and with the board. QBRs are the primary mechanism for demonstrating value, identifying expansion opportunities, and ensuring strategic alignment.

---

## Customer QBR Framework

### Meeting Structure (60 minutes)

| Section | Duration | Content | Presenter |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Opening & agenda | 5 min | Welcome, agenda confirmation, attendee introductions | CSM |
| Business context | 10 min | Customer's current priorities, challenges, market changes | Customer |
| Value delivered | 15 min | Usage metrics, ROI achieved, goals met | CSM |
| Roadmap alignment | 10 min | Upcoming features relevant to customer's needs | Product/CSM |
| Strategic recommendations | 10 min | Optimization opportunities, expansion suggestions | CSM |
| Action items & next steps | 10 min | Commitments, timeline, next QBR date | Both |

### Pre-QBR Preparation Checklist

| Task | Owner | Timeline | Output |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| Pull usage analytics (90-day) | CSM | 2 weeks before | Usage dashboard |
| Calculate ROI metrics | CSM + Analytics | 2 weeks before | ROI summary |
| Review support tickets | CSM | 1 week before | Issue summary |
| Check health score trends | CSM | 1 week before | Health report |
| Identify expansion opportunities | CSM + AE | 1 week before | Expansion brief |
| Prepare presentation deck | CSM | 1 week before | QBR deck |
| Send agenda to customer | CSM | 5 days before | Email |
| Confirm attendees | CSM | 3 days before | Attendee list |
| Internal pre-brief | CSM + AE + Manager | 2 days before | Alignment |

### Value Delivered Section

| Metric Category | Metrics to Present | Data Source |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Adoption | Active users, login frequency, feature adoption | Product analytics |
| Efficiency | Time saved, processes automated, search usage | Product analytics |
| Quality | Error reduction, consistency improvement, compliance | Customer data |
| Financial | Cost savings, revenue impact, ROI calculation | Joint calculation |
| Strategic | Goals achieved, milestones hit, capabilities unlocked | Goal tracking |

### ROI Calculation Framework

| Value Driver | Measurement | Typical Impact |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| Time savings (knowledge search) | Hours saved × hourly rate × employees | $50K–$200K/year |
| Onboarding acceleration | Days reduced × new hire cost/day | $20K–$80K/year |
| Error reduction | Errors prevented × cost per error | $30K–$100K/year |
| Process standardization | Variance reduction × cost of variance | $40K–$150K/year |
| Knowledge retention | Reduced dependency on key personnel | $25K–$75K/year |
| **Total typical ROI** | | **$165K–$605K/year** |

## QBR Best Practices

### Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with value delivered and business outcomes | Lead with product features or usage stats |
| Tie metrics to customer's stated goals | Present generic dashboards |
| Include forward-looking recommendations | Only look backward |
| Invite executive sponsors from both sides | Limit to operational contacts |
| Send pre-read materials 3+ days before | Surprise attendees with new information |
| Document action items with owners and dates | Leave with vague next steps |
| Follow up within 24 hours with summary | Let momentum die after the meeting |
