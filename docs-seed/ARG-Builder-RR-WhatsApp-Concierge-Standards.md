# Riad & Routes — WhatsApp Concierge Communication Standards

## 1. Purpose

WhatsApp is the primary communication channel between Riad & Routes and our guests — from pre-booking inquiries through post-departure follow-up. This document defines the tone, timing, templates, and escalation rules for all WhatsApp interactions. Consistency in digital communication is as important as consistency in face-to-face service.

## 4. Tone & Language Guidelines

### 4.1 Brand Voice

Our WhatsApp communication is: **warm, professional, personal, and efficient.**

- Use the guest's name in every message
- Be conversational but never casual (no slang, no excessive emojis)
- Maximum 2 emojis per message (🌿 for nature, ☀️ for weather, 🙏 for gratitude are approved)
- Write in the guest's preferred language (English, French, Spanish, Arabic — ask if unclear)
- Keep messages concise — no walls of text. Use bullet points for lists.
- Always end with an open question or clear next step

### 4.2 What NOT to Do

- Never use abbreviations (u, ur, thx, pls)
- Never send voice notes unless the guest initiates voice communication
- Never share guest information in group chats
- Never use automated chatbot responses for in-house guests (human only)
- Never leave a conversation without a clear resolution or next step
- Never argue or be defensive — even if the guest is wrong

## 6. Handling Difficult Conversations

### 6.1 Price Negotiation

If a guest asks for a discount:
> Thank you for your interest in Riad [Property], [Name]. Our rates reflect the quality of experience we provide — handcrafted details, personal service, and an intimate setting.
>
> That said, I'd love to find a way to make this work for you. For stays of 3+ nights, we offer [specific package/value-add]. Would that be of interest?

Never reduce the rate without offering a reason (length of stay, returning guest, off-peak dates).

### 6.2 Complaint via WhatsApp

If a guest messages a complaint while in-house:
1. Acknowledge within 2 minutes: "Thank you for letting us know, [Name]. I'm sorry about this. Let me look into it right now."
2. Investigate (do not keep the guest waiting more than 10 minutes without an update)
3. Resolve and confirm: "This has been taken care of. [Explain what was done]. Is there anything else I can help with?"
4. Follow up in person within 30 minutes (don't rely solely on WhatsApp for complaint resolution)

### 6.3 Cancellation Request

> I'm sorry to hear you need to cancel, [Name]. I completely understand that plans change.
>
> Based on our cancellation policy, [explain applicable terms]. Would you prefer to:
> • Cancel and receive [refund amount/credit]
> • Reschedule to alternative dates (we'd be happy to hold your reservation)
>
> Please let me know how you'd like to proceed, and I'll take care of it immediately.

## 7. Media Sharing Guidelines

| Content Type | When to Share | Rules |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Room photos | During booking inquiry | Maximum 3 photos, high quality, current |
| Property video | First-time inquiries | 30-second branded reel only |
| Menu/experience details | When guest asks or for recommendations | PDF or single image, not multiple messages |
| Location/directions | Pre-arrival | Google Maps pin + text description |
| Guest photos | NEVER proactively | Only if guest shares first and gives permission |

## 8. Data Privacy & Security

- Guest phone numbers are stored only in the WhatsApp Business account and PMS
- Conversations are never screenshot and shared externally
- Financial information (card numbers, bank details) is NEVER exchanged via WhatsApp
- If a guest sends sensitive info via WhatsApp, acknowledge receipt and immediately delete the message after noting the info securely
- All staff with WhatsApp access sign a data handling agreement
