# ARG-Builder: Crisis Communication Plan

## Comprehensive Framework for Managing Communications During Business-Critical Events

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Every company will face a crisis — a data breach, a major outage, a PR incident, a legal challenge, or a leadership departure. The difference between companies that survive crises and those that don't is preparation. This plan defines ARG-Builder's communication protocols for every crisis scenario, ensuring rapid, transparent, and coordinated responses that protect customer trust and brand reputation.

## 2. Crisis Classification

### 2.1 Crisis Severity Levels

| Level | Definition | Examples | Response Time |
|-------|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Level 1 (Critical) | Existential threat to business | Data breach, CEO misconduct, regulatory action | Immediate (< 1 hour) |
| Level 2 (Major) | Significant brand/revenue impact | Major outage (> 4 hours), security vulnerability, key executive departure | < 4 hours |
| Level 3 (Moderate) | Limited impact, contained | Minor data incident, negative press, customer complaint viral | < 24 hours |
| Level 4 (Low) | Minimal impact, routine | Employee social media issue, minor product bug in press | < 48 hours |

## 5. Crisis Playbooks

### 5.1 Data Breach Playbook

| Phase | Timeline | Actions | Owner |
|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| Containment | 0–1 hour | Isolate systems, stop data flow, preserve evidence | CTO |
| Assessment | 1–4 hours | Determine scope, data affected, individuals impacted | Security |
| Internal notification | 2–4 hours | Brief crisis team, inform employees | CEO |
| Legal assessment | 4–8 hours | Regulatory obligations, notification requirements | Legal |
| Customer notification | < 72 hours | Notify affected customers per regulation | Comms + CS |
| Regulatory notification | < 72 hours | File with relevant authorities (GDPR, state AG) | Legal |
| Public communication | 24–72 hours | Blog post, press statement | Comms |
| Remediation | 1–2 weeks | Fix vulnerability, improve controls | Engineering |
| Post-incident | 2–4 weeks | Full report, lessons learned, customer follow-up | All |

**Key Messages (Data Breach):**
- What happened (factual, no speculation)
- What data was affected
- What we're doing about it
- What customers should do
- How we're preventing recurrence
- How to contact us for questions

### 5.2 Major Outage Playbook

| Phase | Timeline | Actions | Owner |
|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| Detection | 0–5 min | Alert triggered, on-call responds | Engineering |
| Status page update | < 15 min | "Investigating" posted | On-call |
| Internal escalation | < 30 min | Crisis team notified if > 30 min | Engineering lead |
| Customer communication | < 1 hour | Email to affected customers | CS |
| Progress updates | Every 30 min | Status page + Slack updates | Comms |
| Resolution | ASAP | Fix implemented, service restored | Engineering |
| Resolution communication | < 1 hour post-fix | "Resolved" status, email update | Comms |
| Post-mortem | < 48 hours | Root cause analysis published | Engineering |
| Follow-up | < 1 week | SLA credits, customer calls | CS |

### 5.3 Negative Press / Viral Complaint Playbook

| Phase | Timeline | Actions | Owner |
|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| Monitoring | Ongoing | Social listening, news alerts | Marketing |
| Assessment | < 2 hours | Evaluate severity, accuracy, reach | Comms + Legal |
| Internal brief | < 4 hours | Inform leadership, align on response | CEO |
| Response decision | < 8 hours | Respond publicly, privately, or not at all | CEO + Comms |
| Response execution | < 24 hours | Publish response (if decided) | Comms |
| Monitoring | 48–72 hours | Track sentiment, coverage, spread | Marketing |
| Follow-up | 1 week | Assess lasting impact, adjust strategy | Comms |

### 5.4 Key Executive Departure Playbook

| Phase | Timeline | Actions | Owner |
|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| Internal alignment | Before announcement | Align on narrative, successor plan | CEO + Board |
| Employee notification | Day of | All-hands or email from CEO | CEO |

## 6. Message Templates

### 6.1 Data Breach (Customer Email)

```
Subject: Important Security Notice from ARG-Builder

Dear [Customer Name],

We are writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected your data.

WHAT HAPPENED
On [date], we detected [brief description of incident]. We immediately [containment actions taken].

WHAT INFORMATION WAS INVOLVED
Based on our investigation, the following data may have been accessed: [specific data types].

WHAT WE ARE DOING
- [Action 1: e.g., "Engaged forensic security firm"]
- [Action 2: e.g., "Implemented additional security controls"]
- [Action 3: e.g., "Notified relevant authorities"]

WHAT YOU CAN DO
- [Recommendation 1: e.g., "Reset your password"]
- [Recommendation 2: e.g., "Monitor for unusual activity"]
- [Recommendation 3: e.g., "Enable MFA if not already active"]

We take the security of your data extremely seriously. We are committed to transparency and will provide updates as our investigation continues.

If you have questions, please contact our dedicated response team at [email/phone].

Sincerely,
[CEO Name]
CEO, ARG-Builder
```

### 6.2 Major Outage (Status Page)

```
[INVESTIGATING]
We are currently investigating reports of [service] being unavailable. 
Our team is actively working to identify and resolve the issue.
We will provide updates every 30 minutes.
Posted: [timestamp]

[IDENTIFIED]
We have identified the root cause as [brief technical description].
Our engineering team is implementing a fix.
Estimated resolution: [time estimate].
Posted: [timestamp]

[RESOLVED]
The issue affecting [service] has been fully resolved.
Duration: [X hours Y minutes]
All services are operating normally.
A detailed post-mortem will be published within 48 hours.
We apologize for the disruption and thank you for your patience.
Posted: [timestamp]
```

### 6.3 Negative Press (Public Statement)

```
We are aware of [article/post/claim] regarding [topic].

[If accurate]: We take this feedback seriously. [Acknowledge, explain context, describe actions being taken.]

[If inaccurate]: This characterization does not accurately reflect [reality]. [Provide factual correction with evidence.]

[If partially accurate]: While [acknowledge valid points], [correct inaccuracies and provide context].

We remain committed to [relevant value/principle] and welcome the opportunity to discuss this further with anyone who has questions.

Contact: [press email]
```

## 7. Media Management

### 7.1 Media Response Protocol

| Scenario | Response | Spokesperson |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Routine press inquiry | Respond within 24 hours | VP Marketing |
| Crisis-related inquiry | Respond within 4 hours | CEO (via PR) |
| Hostile/investigative | Legal review before response | CEO + Legal |
| Industry commentary | Respond within 48 hours | CEO or CPO |
| Customer reference request | Coordinate with CS | VP Marketing |

### 7.2 Spokesperson Guidelines

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Stick to approved messaging | Speculate or guess |
| Express empathy and concern | Blame others |
| Provide factual information | Share confidential details |
| Commit to transparency and updates | Make promises you can't keep |
| Redirect to official channels | Engage in arguments |
| Say "I don't know yet, but I'll find out" | Say "no comment" |

### 7.3 Social Media Crisis Protocol

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Pause all scheduled posts | Immediately |
| 2 | Monitor mentions and sentiment | Continuous |
| 3 | Prepare holding statement | < 1 hour |
| 4 | Post official response (if warranted) | < 4 hours |
| 5 | Respond to direct inquiries | Ongoing |
| 6 | Resume normal posting | When crisis resolved |

## 8. Stakeholder-Specific Communication

### 8.1 Employee Communication

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Employees hear it first | Internal communication before external |
| Honest and transparent | Share what you know and what you don't |
| Provide context | Explain why and what it means |
| Give talking points | Help them respond to external questions |
| Show empathy | Acknowledge impact on them |
| Provide updates | Regular cadence until resolved |
| Open Q&A | Allow questions, answer honestly |

### 8.2 Customer Communication

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Proactive, not reactive | Tell them before they find out elsewhere |
| Specific and factual | What happened, what's affected, what to do |
| Empathetic | Acknowledge impact on their business |
| Action-oriented | Clear next steps for them |
| Accessible | Easy to reach us for questions |
| Follow-up | Don't go silent after initial communication |
| Personalized (enterprise) | Direct calls from CSM or executive |

### 8.3 Investor/Board Communication

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Early warning | Brief board chair immediately for Level 1–2 |
| Factual assessment | Impact on business, customers, financials |
| Action plan | What we're doing, timeline, resources needed |
| Regular updates | Until crisis resolved |
| Lessons learned | Post-crisis report with prevention plan |
