# ARG-Builder: Sales Development Representative (SDR) Playbook

## Cold Outreach Sequences, Call Scripts, Objection Handling, and Quota Attainment

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The SDR team is the tip of ARG-Builder's revenue spear — responsible for generating qualified pipeline that feeds the entire sales organization. This playbook provides everything an SDR needs to succeed: target identification, multi-channel outreach sequences, call scripts, email templates, objection handling, and performance benchmarks.

## 2. SDR Role Definition

### 2.1 Mission

Generate qualified meetings between target prospects and Account Executives by identifying, engaging, and qualifying potential customers through outbound prospecting and inbound lead follow-up.

### 2.2 Key Metrics

| Metric | Daily Target | Weekly Target | Monthly Target |
|--------|-------------|--------------|---------------|
| Outbound activities (total) | 80–100 | 400–500 | 1,600–2,000 |
| Emails sent | 50–60 | 250–300 | 1,000–1,200 |
| Calls made | 25–35 | 125–175 | 500–700 |
| LinkedIn touches | 10–15 | 50–75 | 200–300 |
| Conversations (live) | 5–8 | 25–40 | 100–160 |
| Meetings booked | 1–2 | 5–8 | 20–30 |
| Qualified opportunities (SQL) | — | 3–5 | 12–18 |
| Pipeline generated | — | — | $300K–$500K |

### 2.3 Qualification Criteria (SQL Definition)

A meeting becomes a Sales Qualified Lead (SQL) when:

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Authority | Decision maker or strong influencer identified |
| Need | Confirmed operational knowledge/documentation pain |
| Timeline | Active initiative or budget cycle within 6 months |
| Fit | Company matches ICP (size, industry, complexity) |
| Engagement | Prospect agreed to 30+ minute discovery call with AE |

## 6. Call Scripts

### 6.1 Cold Call Opening

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from ARG-Builder. I know I'm calling out of the blue — do you have 30 seconds for me to tell you why, and then you can decide if it's worth continuing?"
>
> [If yes:]
>
> "I work with [Title]s at companies like [2 similar companies] who were struggling with [specific pain: operational knowledge scattered across tools, new hires taking months to ramp up, documentation always out of date].
>
> We help them [one-sentence value prop]. I'm curious — is operational consistency something that's on your radar at [Company]?"

### 6.2 Voicemail Script (30 seconds max)

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from ARG-Builder. I'm reaching out because we help [industry] companies like [similar company] reduce operational overhead by 60% using AI-powered knowledge systems. I sent you a quick email with more context — would love to connect for 15 minutes. My number is [number]. Again, [Your Name] from ARG-Builder. Thanks!"

### 6.3 Discovery Questions (If Conversation Continues)

**Opening Questions:**
- "How does your team currently manage operational documentation and procedures?"
- "When a new person joins, how do they learn 'how things work here'?"
- "If I asked 5 people on your team the same process question, would I get the same answer?"

**Pain Exploration:**
- "What happens when a key person leaves — how much knowledge walks out?"
- "How much time do your people spend searching for information vs. doing their actual job?"
- "What's the biggest operational challenge you're trying to solve this quarter?"

**Qualification:**
- "Is this something you're actively looking to solve, or more of a 'someday' priority?"
- "Who else would be involved in evaluating a solution like this?"
- "Do you have budget allocated for operational improvement initiatives?"

### 6.4 Meeting Booking Close

> "Based on what you've shared, I think there could be a strong fit. What I'd suggest is a 30-minute call with [AE Name], who works specifically with [industry] companies on these challenges. They can show you exactly how [similar company] solved this and explore whether the same approach would work for [Company].
>
> I have availability on [Day 1] at [Time] or [Day 2] at [Time] — which works better for you?"

## 7. Objection Handling

### 7.1 Common Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already have Confluence/Notion" | "Totally understand — most of our customers came from Confluence or Notion. The difference is that those tools require you to build and maintain everything manually. ARG-Builder generates complete operational systems automatically and keeps them current. Would it be worth seeing the difference in a quick demo?" |
| "We're not looking right now" | "I appreciate the honesty. Can I ask — is that because operations are running smoothly, or because it's not a priority this quarter? Either way, when would be a better time to revisit? I'll set a reminder." |
| "Send me an email" | "Happy to! To make sure I send something relevant, can I ask one quick question? What's your biggest operational challenge right now — knowledge consistency, onboarding speed, or something else?" |
| "How much does it cost?" | "It depends on your team size and needs — typically $2K–$10K/month. But honestly, the more important question is the ROI. Our customers see 500%+ return within 6 months. Would it be worth a quick call to see if similar results are realistic for [Company]?" |
| "I'm not the right person" | "No problem at all — who on your team handles operational efficiency or knowledge management? I'd love an introduction if you're open to it." |
| "We tried something like this before and it didn't work" | "I hear that a lot, actually. Can I ask what you tried and what went wrong? The reason I ask is that most 'knowledge management' tools fail because they require manual effort. ARG-Builder is fundamentally different — it generates and maintains everything autonomously. That's usually what was missing before." |
| "Just send me information" | "Of course. What would be most useful — a case study from a [industry] company, a product overview, or our ROI calculator? I want to send something that's actually worth your time." |

### 7.2 Gatekeeper Handling

| Scenario | Response |
|----------|----------|
| "What's this regarding?" | "I'm following up on some research I sent [First Name] about operational efficiency — they'll know what it's about." |
| "They're in a meeting" | "No problem. When would be a good time to try back? Also, would you mind letting them know [Your Name] from ARG-Builder called? They can reach me at [number]." |
| "They're not interested" | "I appreciate you letting me know. Just to make sure — did they specifically say that about ARG-Builder, or is this a general policy? I ask because we work with several [industry] companies and the feedback has been really positive." |
| "Send an email instead" | "Happy to. What's the best email for [First Name]? I want to make sure it gets to them directly." |
