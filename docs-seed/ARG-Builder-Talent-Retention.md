# ARG-Builder: Talent Retention Strategy

## Comprehensive Framework for Retaining Top Performers in a Competitive Market

---

## 1. Executive Summary

In early-stage SaaS, losing a key engineer or sales leader can set the company back 6–12 months. With average startup turnover at 25% and the cost of replacing a senior employee at 150–200% of their salary, retention is not just an HR priority — it is a business survival imperative. This document defines ARG-Builder's complete talent retention strategy across compensation, culture, growth, and engagement.

## 3. Compensation & Equity

### 3.1 Compensation Philosophy

> ARG-Builder compensates at the 65th–75th percentile of market for base salary and targets top-quartile total compensation through equity participation. We believe in paying fairly, rewarding performance generously, and creating meaningful ownership for every team member.

### 3.2 Compensation Structure

| Component | Philosophy | Benchmark |

| Base salary | 65th–75th percentile of market | Levels.fyi, Pave, Option Impact |

## 7. Retention Interventions

### 7.1 Early Warning System

| Signal | Detection Method | Response |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Engagement survey decline | Quarterly survey | Manager conversation within 1 week |
| Reduced output/activity | Performance data | 1:1 check-in |
| LinkedIn profile update | Social monitoring (opt-in) | Proactive career conversation |
| Peer departure | Exit interview data | Team check-in |
| Life event (relocation, family) | Manager awareness | Flexibility conversation |
| Compensation below market | Annual benchmarking | Proactive adjustment |
| Stalled career progression | Tenure without promotion | Growth plan creation |
| Conflict with manager | Skip-level feedback | Mediation or transfer |

### 7.2 Stay Interview Framework

**Conducted:** Quarterly for high-performers, semi-annually for all

**Questions:**
1. "What do you look forward to when you come to work each day?"
2. "What are you learning here? What do you want to learn?"
3. "Why do you stay at ARG-Builder?"
4. "When was the last time you thought about leaving? What prompted it?"
5. "What can I do to make your experience better?"
6. "Do you feel your compensation reflects your contribution?"
7. "What would make you leave? What would another company need to offer?"

### 7.3 Retention Offers (Last Resort)

| Scenario | Offer Components | Authority |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Counter-offer (competing offer) | Salary match + equity refresh + growth plan | VP + CEO approval |
| Flight risk (no offer yet) | Proactive raise + new challenge + recognition | Manager + VP |
| Role dissatisfaction | Internal transfer + transition support | HR + receiving manager |
| Burnout risk | Reduced workload + sabbatical + support | Manager + HR |
| Relocation | Remote arrangement + travel budget | VP approval |

**Counter-offer policy:** Generally avoid counter-offers (50% leave within 12 months anyway). Instead, focus on proactive retention so counter-offers are rarely needed.

## 8. Exit Management

### 8.1 Exit Interview Process

| Step | Timing | Owner | Purpose |
|------|--------|-------|---------|
| Manager conversation | Day of notice | Manager | Understand reasons, explore retention |
| HR exit interview | Within 3 days | HR | Structured feedback collection |
| Skip-level conversation | Within 1 week | Manager's manager | Broader context |
| Written survey | Last week | HR | Quantitative data |

### 8.2 Exit Interview Questions

| Category | Questions |
|----------|-----------|
| Reason for leaving | Primary reason, contributing factors, what could have changed it |
| Role satisfaction | Was the role what was promised? What was missing? |

| Compensation | Was compensation fair? How did it compare to your new role? |
| Culture | What did you love? What would you change? |
