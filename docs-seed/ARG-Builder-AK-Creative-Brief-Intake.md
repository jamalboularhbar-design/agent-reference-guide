# ArtKech Design Studio — Creative Brief Intake Process

## 1. Purpose

The creative brief is the foundation of every project at ArtKech Design Studio. A well-crafted brief prevents scope creep, aligns expectations, and ensures the creative team delivers work that meets the client's business objectives — not just their aesthetic preferences. This SOP defines how we receive, qualify, structure, and distribute briefs across the studio.

## 2. Brief Intake Channels

| Channel | Response Time | Qualification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Website inquiry form (artkech.ma/brief) | Within 4 business hours | Auto-assigned to Account Manager |
| Email (hello@artkech.ma) | Within 4 business hours | Triaged by Studio Coordinator |

## 3. Qualification Criteria

Before accepting a project, the Account Manager assesses:

| Criteria | Minimum Threshold | Action if Below |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Budget alignment | > 5,000 MAD for single deliverable | Refer to freelancer network or decline politely |
| Timeline feasibility | > 5 business days for standard projects | Discuss rush fee (+30-50%) or adjust scope |
| Scope clarity | Client can articulate what they need (even roughly) | Schedule discovery call before proceeding |
| Brand fit | Project aligns with ArtKech's portfolio direction | Decline if it would dilute brand positioning |
| Payment terms | 50% upfront accepted | Do not proceed without deposit confirmation |

## 4. Discovery Call Structure (30-45 minutes)

### 4.1 Agenda

| Block | Duration | Content |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Introduction | 5 min | Introduce team, explain process, set expectations for the call |
| Client background | 10 min | Business overview, industry, target audience, competitors |
| Project objectives | 10 min | What problem are we solving? What does success look like? |
| Deliverables & scope | 10 min | Specific outputs needed, formats, sizes, quantities |
| Timeline & budget | 5 min | Deadline, budget range, decision-making process |
| Next steps | 5 min | Confirm brief will be sent within 48h, explain approval process |

### 4.2 Key Questions to Ask

**Business context:**
- "Who is your target audience? Describe your ideal customer."
- "Who are your top 3 competitors? What do you like/dislike about their visual identity?"
- "What's the business goal behind this project? (Launch, rebrand, campaign, expansion?)"

**Creative direction:**
- "Do you have existing brand guidelines we should follow?"
- "Share 3-5 examples of design work you admire (any industry). What do you like about each?"
- "Are there any styles, colors, or approaches you want us to avoid?"

**Practical constraints:**
- "Where will this be used? (Print, digital, social, signage, packaging?)"
- "Who needs to approve the final work? How many decision-makers?"
- "Is there existing content (photos, copy, logos) we'll be working with, or do we create everything?"

## 5. Brief Document Template

After the discovery call, the Account Manager produces a structured brief within 48 hours:

### 5.1 Brief Sections

| Section | Content | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Project title | Clear, descriptive name | "Café Nomade — Brand Identity & Menu Design" |
| Client | Company name, contact person, email, phone | Café Nomade, Youssef B., youssef@cafenomade.ma |
| Background | 2-3 sentences on the business and context | "New specialty coffee shop opening in Guéliz, targeting young professionals and digital nomads" |
| Objective | What the project must achieve (business outcome) | "Establish a distinctive brand identity that differentiates from existing cafés and attracts the 25-40 demographic" |
| Target audience | Demographics, psychographics, behaviors | "25-40, urban professionals, value aesthetics and quality, active on Instagram, willing to pay premium" |
| Deliverables | Specific list of outputs with specs | Logo (primary + secondary), color palette, typography system, business cards, menu design (A3 folded), social media templates (5) |
| Tone & personality | Brand voice attributes (3-5 adjectives) | "Artisanal, warm, contemporary, confident, locally-rooted" |
| Inspirations | Visual references provided by client + our additions | Mood board link (Milanote or Pinterest) |
| Constraints | Technical requirements, brand guidelines, restrictions | "Must work in both Arabic and French. No green (competitor's color). Print-ready for offset." |
| Timeline | Key milestones with dates | Concepts: June 5 → Revisions: June 12 → Final: June 19 → Print files: June 22 |
| Budget | Confirmed amount and payment schedule | 35,000 MAD total. 50% deposit, 25% at concept approval, 25% at final delivery |
| Approval process | Who approves, how many rounds included | Client + business partner. 2 revision rounds included, additional at 2,000 MAD/round |

### 5.2 Brief Approval

- Brief is sent to client for written approval (email reply or signed PDF)
- Client has 3 business days to approve or request amendments
- No creative work begins until brief is approved AND deposit is received
- Approved brief is uploaded to project management system (Notion) and shared with creative team

## 6. Brief Distribution & Kickoff

### 6.1 Internal Kickoff Meeting (30 minutes)

Once brief is approved, the Account Manager schedules an internal kickoff:

| Attendee | Role in Meeting |
| :--- | :--- |
| Account Manager | Presents brief, answers questions about client |
| Creative Director | Assigns team, sets creative direction |
| Lead Designer (assigned) | Asks clarifying questions, confirms approach |
| Studio Coordinator | Confirms timeline in project management system, flags conflicts |

### 6.2 Kickoff Outputs

- Creative team confirmed and notified
- Project created in Notion with all milestones
- Time tracking code assigned
- Slack channel created (for projects > 20,000 MAD)
- Client folder created in Google Drive (organized by deliverable)

## 7. Brief Amendments After Approval

| Change Type | Process | Cost Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Minor clarification (no scope change) | Email confirmation, update brief document | No charge |
| Additional deliverable (within same project) | Revised brief + revised quote, client approval | Additional fee quoted |
| Scope reduction (fewer deliverables) | Revised brief, credit note if deposit covers it | Partial refund possible |
| Complete direction change | New discovery call, new brief, reset timeline | May require new deposit |
| Timeline acceleration (rush) | Assess feasibility, quote rush fee | +30-50% of remaining balance |

## 8. Performance Metrics

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
