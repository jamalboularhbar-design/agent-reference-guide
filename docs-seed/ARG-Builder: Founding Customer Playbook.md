# ARG-Builder: Founding Customer Playbook

## Executive Summary

This playbook provides the exact scripts, emails, outreach strategies, and qualification frameworks needed to land ARG-Builder's first 10–15 founding customers within 90 days of launch. Founding customers are critical for validating product-market fit, generating case studies, refining pricing, and building social proof for the broader go-to-market launch. Each founding customer receives a significant discount (40–60% off standard pricing) in exchange for detailed feedback, a case study commitment, and a reference call agreement.

---

## Founding Customer Profile

The ideal founding customer is a mid-market company (200–1,000 employees) experiencing acute operational pain — typically triggered by rapid growth, leadership transitions, or failed documentation initiatives. They have budget authority, a willingness to try new approaches, and enough operational complexity to demonstrate ARG-Builder's full value.

| Criterion | Ideal Profile |
|-----------|---------------|
| Company Size | 200–1,000 employees |
| Revenue | $50M–$500M annual |
| Industry | Professional services, technology, healthcare, financial services |
| Pain Trigger | Recent growth spurt, failed consulting project, high turnover, new leadership |
| Decision Maker | VP Operations, COO, Chief of Staff, VP People |
| Budget | $25K–$75K available for operational improvement |
| Timeline | Willing to start within 30 days |
| Commitment | Case study + reference call + quarterly feedback |

## Outreach Strategy: The Concentric Circle Approach

### Circle 1: Direct Network (Days 1–14)

These are people you know personally or have a direct professional relationship with. Conversion rate: 30–40%.

**Action Plan:**
1. List every professional contact who works at a mid-market company in an operational role.
2. Identify 20–30 contacts who match the founding customer profile.
3. Send personalized messages (not mass emails) referencing your specific relationship.
4. Offer a private demo within 48 hours of positive response.

**Outreach Template — Direct Network:**

Subject: Building something that would have solved [specific problem you discussed]

Hi [Name],

Remember when we talked about [specific operational challenge they mentioned]? I've been building something that directly solves that problem.

ARG-Builder is an AI platform that generates complete interactive operational systems in 12–19 hours. I'm looking for 10–15 founding customers to help shape the product, and given [their company]'s growth, I immediately thought of you.

Founding customers get 60% off everything for the first year, direct access to me, and genuine input on the product roadmap. The only commitment is a case study after 90 days and quarterly feedback.

Would you have 30 minutes this week for a demo? I think you'll be surprised at what this can do.

[Founder Name]

### Circle 2: Warm Introductions (Days 7–30)

These are contacts reachable through one degree of separation. Conversion rate: 15–25%.

**Action Plan:**
1. Identify 50–75 target companies that match the founding customer profile.
2. Map your network for connections to decision-makers at those companies.
3. Request warm introductions using the template below.
4. Follow up within 24 hours of any introduction.

Thanks either way!
[Founder Name]

### Circle 3: Community & Content (Days 14–60)

Leverage professional communities, LinkedIn content, and industry events to attract inbound interest. Conversion rate: 5–10%.

**Action Plan:**
1. Post 3x/week on LinkedIn about operational challenges mid-market companies face.
2. Share specific insights (not product pitches) that demonstrate expertise.
3. Engage actively in Slack communities (Operations, SaaS, mid-market leadership groups).
4. Offer free "operational health assessments" that naturally lead to ARG-Builder conversations.
5. Host a small virtual roundtable (8–12 attendees) on "Scaling Operations Without Consultants."

**LinkedIn Post Framework:**

Post 1 (Problem): "I talked to 15 mid-market COOs last month. Every single one told me the same thing: their operational documentation is either nonexistent or completely ignored. Here's why..."

Post 2 (Insight): "The average mid-market company spends $180K on consultants to produce a PDF that 80% of the team never opens. What if the problem isn't the content — it's the format?"

Post 3 (Social proof): "We just helped [Company] go from zero operational documentation to 92% team adoption in 10 days. Here's what made the difference..."

### Circle 4: Cold Outreach (Days 30–90)

**Action Plan:**
1. Build a list of 200 mid-market companies showing growth signals (hiring, funding, expansion).
2. Identify the VP Operations, COO, or Chief of Staff at each company.
3. Send personalized cold emails referencing specific company signals.
4. Follow up with a 3-touch sequence over 14 days.

**Cold Email Template:**

I'm looking for 10 founding customers to shape the product. Founding members get 60% off Year 1, direct founder access, and genuine product roadmap input.

Worth a 20-minute conversation to see if there's a fit?

[Founder Name]

## Qualification Framework: BANT+

Use this framework to qualify prospects during discovery calls. Every founding customer must score 4/5 or higher.

| Criterion | Score 1 (Poor) | Score 3 (Moderate) | Score 5 (Strong) |
|-----------|----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Budget** | No budget allocated | Budget possible but unconfirmed | Budget approved, decision-maker engaged |
| **Authority** | Talking to individual contributor | Manager-level, needs approval | VP/C-level, can sign today |
| **Need** | Mild interest, no acute pain | Recognized problem, exploring options | Urgent pain, failed previous solutions |
| **Timeline** | "Maybe next year" | "Next quarter" | "This month" |
| **Fit** | Poor profile match | Partial match (size or industry) | Perfect match (size + industry + pain) |

**Minimum qualifying score:** 20/25 for founding customer status.

## Demo Script (30 Minutes)

### Opening (3 minutes)
"Thanks for taking the time, [Name]. Before I show you anything, I'd love to understand — what's your biggest operational challenge right now? What prompted you to take this call?"

*Listen actively. Take notes. Map their pain to ARG-Builder capabilities.*

### Problem Validation (5 minutes)
"That's exactly what we're hearing from other companies at your stage. Let me share some context on the problem..."

*Present the $2.8M problem framework. Ask: "Does that resonate with your experience?"*

### Live Demo (12 minutes)
"Let me show you what ARG-Builder actually produces. This is a live system we built for [reference company]..."

*Walk through the reference guide website. Show: search, command palette, process timelines, persona switching. Emphasize adoption features.*

### Founding Customer Offer (5 minutes)
"We're selecting 10–15 founding customers to shape this product. Here's what that looks like..."

*Present the founding customer value proposition. Emphasize exclusivity and limited availability.*

### Close (5 minutes)
"Based on what you've seen, does this feel like it could solve [their specific pain]? What would need to be true for you to move forward as a founding customer?"

*Handle objections. Set next steps with specific dates.*
