# ARG-Builder: Strategic Planning & OKR System

## Executive Summary

This document defines ARG-Builder's strategic planning methodology and OKR (Objectives and Key Results) system — the structured approach that aligns every team member's daily work to the company's long-term vision. This system ensures strategic clarity cascades from the board room to individual contributors without losing fidelity.

## OKR Framework

### OKR Structure

| Element | Definition | Criteria | Example |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Objective** | Qualitative goal that inspires action | Ambitious, clear, time-bound, actionable | "Become the #1 choice for mid-market operations teams" |
| **Key Result** | Quantitative measure of progress | Specific, measurable, achievable, relevant | "Achieve 50 new mid-market customers (100–500 employees)" |
| **Initiative** | Project or action that drives a key result | Clearly owned, time-boxed, resourced | "Launch industry-specific templates for 5 verticals" |

### OKR Scoring

| Score | Meaning | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| 0.0–0.3 | Failed to make progress | Blocked, wrong approach, or deprioritized |
| 0.4–0.6 | Made progress but fell short | Good effort, external factors, or over-ambitious |
| 0.7–0.8 | Achieved target (sweet spot) | Well-calibrated, strong execution |
| 0.9–1.0 | Exceeded expectations | Possibly under-ambitious (sandbag check) |

## OKR Cadence

### Weekly Check-In (15 min per team)

| Element | Time | Content |
|---------|------|---------|
| Confidence update | 3 min | Red/yellow/green on each KR |
| Progress metrics | 5 min | Current numbers vs. target |
| Blockers | 5 min | What's preventing progress |
| Help needed | 2 min | Cross-functional requests |

### Monthly Review (60 min, leadership)

| Element | Time | Content |
|---------|------|---------|
| Scorecard review | 15 min | All OKRs scored 0–1.0 |
| Deep-dive (1–2 OKRs) | 20 min | Root cause on off-track items |
| Adjustments | 15 min | Reallocation, reprioritization |
| Decisions needed | 10 min | Escalated items requiring leadership input |

### Quarterly Review (Half-day, all-hands)

| Element | Time | Content |
|---------|------|---------|
| Quarter results | 30 min | Final scores, celebrations, learnings |
| Next quarter context | 30 min | Market update, strategic shifts |
| New OKR presentation | 60 min | Each team presents Q+1 OKRs |
| Cross-team alignment | 60 min | Dependencies, shared initiatives |
| Q&A | 30 min | Open discussion |

## Strategic Decision Framework

### When to Pivot vs. Persist

| Signal | Pivot Indicator | Persist Indicator |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Customer feedback | "This doesn't solve my problem" | "I love this but want more" |
| Metrics trend | Declining engagement over 3 months | Growing engagement, slow but steady |
| Win rate | < 10% and declining | > 20% and stable/growing |
| NRR | < 80% | > 100% |
| Team morale | Disbelief in product direction | Excited about roadmap |
| Market | Fundamental shift in buyer behavior | Growing TAM |

## Alignment Mechanisms

### Cascading OKRs

| Level | Sets OKRs | Informed By | Contributes To |
|-------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Company | CEO + Leadership | Board strategy, market | Board expectations |
| Department | VP/Director | Company OKRs | Company OKRs |
| Team | Manager | Department OKRs | Department OKRs |
| Individual | IC + Manager | Team OKRs | Team OKRs |

### Alignment Rules

| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 60/40 split | 60% of team OKRs cascade from above, 40% are team-originated |
| Max 3–5 objectives | Per team per quarter (focus over breadth) |
| Max 3–4 KRs per objective | Keep measurable and trackable |
| Shared KRs | At least 1 KR per team should be shared with another team |
| Stretch targets | At least 1 KR should be a stretch (70% confidence of hitting) |
